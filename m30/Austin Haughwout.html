He is most recently known for his expulsion from Central Connecticut State University on the grounds of threatening a mass shooting in campus. ==Assault victim== Haughwout first received media attention over an earlier incident in May 2014, when at the age of 17 he was assaulted by a 23-year-old woman who objected to his use of a drone for photography at a public beach. Haughwout recorded a [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=elnENHrliKc video] of the assault on his cellphone and posted it to YouTube. The drone was flying in woodland behind his home. The video, posted on July 10, had received almost 2 million views by July 21, and led to an [[Federal Aviation Administration|FAA]] investigation as to whether Haughwout had violated aviation law, although local police said that they did not believe that he had violated state law.{{cite news|url=http://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2015/jul/21/drone-handgun-video-teen-connecticut|title=Teen's video of handgun-toting drone prompts federal investigation|publisher=[[The Guardian]]|date=21 Jul 2015|authors=Associated Press}}{{cite news|url=http://cnsnews.com/news/article/teenagers-video-gun-firing-drone-spurs-investigation|title=Teenager's video of gun-firing drone spurs investigation|authors=Associated Press|date=21 Jul 2015|publisher=[[Cybercast News Service]]}}{{cite news|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2015/jul/21/flying-gun-drone-investigated-faa-after-engineerin/|title=‘Flying Gun’ drone investigated by FAA after student’s YouTube video goes viral|last=Ernst|first=Douglas|date=21 Jul 2015|publisher=[[The Washington Times]]}} ===Drone with flamethrower=== In December 2015, Haughwout uploaded a [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lmD3rXUR1Tw video] of a drone carrying a [[flame thrower]], which he had also created, and which was being used to roast a turkey. Again, the local police stated that no laws were broken.{{cite news|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/news/national/watch-teen-tests-fire-breathing-turkey-roasting-drone-article-1.2460793|title=WATCH: Connecticut teen tests fire-breathing, turkey-roasting drone|last=Alba|first=Alejandro|publisher=[[New York Daily News]]|date=9 Dec 2015}} ==Legislation== The video with the drone-mounted handgun had been posted weeks after a proposal in the [[Connecticut House of Representatives]] to restrict armed drones had lapsed because of inaction.
