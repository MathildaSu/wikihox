The Consulate was previously located in Suite 600 of [[Post Oak Central|3 Post Oak Central]] in the [[Uptown Houston|Uptown District]].[http://www.cgihouston.org/ Home page]. Consulate-General of India in Houston. Retrieved on December 21, 2009. The space, which was the consulate's first location, had {{convert|11353|sqft|sqm}} of space. In 2011 the consulate announced that it planned to outsource visa services. As of January 2011 it provided over 700 consular services to its service area, then Alabama, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, Kansas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Oklahoma and Texas, every day. ''[[The Times of India]]'' said that the mission was "considered one of the top ten missions in the world"."[http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/nri/us-canada-news/Indian-Consulate-to-outsource-visa-related-services/articleshow/7325750.cms Indian Consulate to outsource visa related services]." ''[[The Times of India]]''. January 20, 2011. Retrieved on October 12, 2012. On August 30, 2011, the consulate moved into a new standalone facility at 4300 Scotland. The [[Government of India]] had purchased the {{convert|24829|sqft|sqm|adj=on}} property, which had a built-up area that had {{convert|18500|sqft|sqm}} of space. The [[Consulate-General of India, Atlanta]] announced that it planned to begin its services on October 1, 2012.
