She is best known for her videos on [[YouTube]] mixing with four [[CDJ]] players without using [[headphones]].{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/Juicymtv|title=Juicy M TV|work=YouTube|accessdate=4 October 2014}} ==Early life== Martas' early life was connected to music. At the age of five, she attended dance courses, then piano class in music school and also worked as a news host on a music TV channel while studying journalism at university.{{cite web|url=http://electronicalife.com/from-the-eastern-bloc-to-block-parties-an-interview-w-ukraines-dj-juicy-m|title=From The Eastern Bloc To Block Parties – An Interview w/ Ukraine’s DJ Juicy M|work=Electronica Life|accessdate=4 October 2014}} ==Career== She began her DJ career in 2007 as a resident DJ in fashionable nightclubs like Orangerea Supper Club, Patipa, and Matrix in Ukraine. The following year, she was the headlining act of the Fashion TV tour. Having participated in the DMC World Eliminations in Ukraine and World Technical DJ Championship in Dubai, she was named “Best Female DJ of the Year” at the 2010 ProDJ Awards in Ukraine.{{cite web|url=http://juicy-m.com/about/|title=About|work=Juicy M|accessdate=4 October 2014}} In 2011–2012, she had her first international gigs in Europe and Asia as a hip hop and R&B DJ. She opened for [[Black Eyed Peas]], [[Backstreet Boys]], [[Afrojack]], [[Jay Sean]], [[Skrillex]], and others. Juicy M has also modeled for well-known magazines like ''[[FHM]]''{{cite web|url=http://www.fhm.com.ph/fhm-babes/girls-of-fhm/dj-juicy-m-may-2012|title=DJ Juicy M - May 2012|work=FHM|accessdate=4 October 2014}} and ''XXL''.{{cite web|url=http://scopecube.com/2012/07/06/dj-juicy-m-xxl-magazine-ukraine-august-2012-photos/|title=DJ Juicy M XXL Magazine Ukraine|work=Scopecube|accessdate=4 October 2014}} Juicy M had always dreamed about huge festivals like [[Tomorrowland (festival)|Tomorrowland]], [[Electric Daisy Carnival]] and [[Ultra Music Festival]],{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z1F8qCaIHF0|title=Juicy M - Questions & Answers|work=YouTube|accessdate=4 October 2014}} so in 2013 she started playing and producing [[Electronic dance music|EDM]]. She gained huge popularity after posting her first mixing video with four CDJs on her [[YouTube]] page which gained over 7 million views,{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4xS3TIa2gSQ|title=Juicy M & 4 decks|work=YouTube|accessdate=4 October 2014}} and after releasing her first bootleg remix of [[Major Lazer]]'s "[[Watch Out for This (Bumaye)]]", supported by numerous DJs like [[Calvin Harris]], [[Laidback Luke]], [[Bob Sinclar]] and others in April 2013.{{cite web|url=http://www.1001tracklists.com/track/136131_major-lazer-watch-out-for-this-bumaye-juicy-m-emzy-remix/index.html|title=Major Lazer feat. Busy Signal & The Flexican & FS Green - Watch Out For This (Bumaye) (Juicy M & Emzy Remix) [SECRETLY CANADIAN] Tracklists / Playlists|date=22 June 2014|publisher=|accessdate=4 October 2014}} In June 2013, she started a weekly podcast called "JuicyLand", which is broadcast in 11 countries.{{Citation needed|date=September 2014}} In September 2013, Juicy M hit ''Billboard'''s [[Next Big Sound]] chart.{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/archive/charts/2013/next-big-sound-25|title=Next Big Sound - 2013 Archive - Billboard Charts Archive|work=Billboard|accessdate=4 October 2014}} In 2014, she founded her own label, JUMMP Records and released her first single titled "Samurai" together with German producer Ton! Dyson on June 12,{{cite web|url=http://www.beatport.com/release/samurai/1307726|title=Samurai [JUMMP Records] :: Beatport|publisher=|accessdate=4 October 2014}} which peaked at number 32 on the [[Beatport]] chart. ==Discography== ===Singles=== ;2016 *"Hit The Road" (with Chrom3) *"LA Girls" (with Harrison) [Armada Music] *"Rowcraft" (with Jimmy Clash) feat.
