xml:space="preserve" bytes="2919"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Neill King|timestamp=20131230195440|year=2013|month=December|day=30|substed=yes}} '''Neill King''' (born October 22, 1957 in [[Wimbledon, London|Wimbledon]], [[Surrey]]) is an [[England|English]] [[record producer]] and [[recording engineer]]. He studied classical music and trumpet on leaving [[Wimbledon College]], then trained as a recording engineer at [[Eden Studios]], [[Chiswick]], London, before working throughout the UK and Europe with artists such as [[Elvis Costello]], [[Nick Lowe]], [[Madness (band)|Madness]], [[The Undertones]], [[Squeeze (band)|Squeeze]], [[A-ha]], [[The Smiths]], [[Shakin Stevens]], [[Joe Jackson (musician)|Joe Jackson]], [[Rockpile]], [[Phil Everly]], [[Cliff Richard]], [[Billy Bremner]], [[Dr. Feelgood]], [[The Jets (British rockabilly band)|The Jets]], [[John Hiatt]], [[The Long Ryders]], [[Little Richard]], [[Billy Fury]], [[Men Without Hats]], [[Spear of Destiny]], [[Transvision Vamp]], [[The Saints (1960s band)|The Saints]], [[Jean Patrick Capdevielle]], and [[La Bionda]] amongst others. ==United States== King moved to California in 1990 and continued producing, engineering and mixing records in the US, Canada and Australia for [[Green Day]], [[Menswe@r]], [[The Muffs]], [[Jawbreaker (band)|Jawbreaker]], [[Mother Hips]], [[Jebediah]], Tuuli, [[The Supertones]], [[Gob (band)|Gob]], [[Bleach]], [[Cadet]], [[Slick Shoes]], [[The Undecided]], [[All Star United]], [[Pluto]], [[Waking Hours]], [[Age of Electric]], [[Buck-O-Nine]], [[Vallejo (band)|Vallejo]], [[Texas Tornados]], [[Dada]], [[Celine Dion]], [[Robert Cray]], [[John Campbell (blues guitarist)|John Campbell]], [[The Buzzcocks]] and [[En Vogue]].
