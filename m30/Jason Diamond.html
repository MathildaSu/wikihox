Diamond''', M.D., [[Fellow of the American College of Surgeons|F.A.C.S.]] (born December 21, 1970 in [[Glen Ridge, New Jersey]]) is an American [[plastic surgeon]], recognized for his work within the medical community as well as his role as a television personality. He is in private practice specializing in facial plastic and reconstructive surgery and has offices in [[Beverly Hills, California|Beverly Hills]], [[New York City]], [[Dubai]], and [[Moscow]]. ==Early Life and Training== Dr. Jason Diamond was born to Geoffrey and Gail Diamond. Raised in [[Newark, New Jersey]], Diamond attended [[Newark Academy]] for high school where he was captain of the [[baseball]] team that won the state championship; he pitched the final game. Dr Jason Diamond attended the [[University of Rochester]] in [[New York]] for his undergraduate studies and attended medical school at the [[University of Rochester School of Medicine]]. ==Personal life== Jason Diamond is married to Jessica Combs, who was born in Eastern Kentucky in [[Knott County, Kentucky|Knott County]], the same town as her best friend, Hollywood celebrity [[Rebecca Gayheart]]. Diamond and his wife reside in [[Beverly Hills, California|Beverly Hills, CA]] and have two children, Jake Sutton Diamond (born February 2, 2008) and Jasper Jones Diamond (born January 11, 2011). Diamond’s sister, Marisa Dworkin, is married to Jason Dworkin, the owner of Nana’s Deli in [[Livingston, New Jersey]]. ==Television Appearances== Diamond has most notably appeared on the hit E! Network show, ''[[Dr. 90210]]'', alongside the show’s star, Dr. [[Robert Rey]]. In addition to E!, Dr. Jason Diamond reviews facial plastic surgery procedures for a variety of television and print publications and has worked as a correspondent for ''[[CBS News]]'', [[Extra (TV program)|''Extra'']], ''[[Entertainment Tonight]]'', ''[[Discovery Health]]'', as well as the hit [[MTV]] show ''[[True Life]]''.
