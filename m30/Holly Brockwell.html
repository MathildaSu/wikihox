xml:space="preserve" bytes="5627"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Holly Brockwell|timestamp=20170801062848|year=2017|month=August|day=1|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Use dmy dates|date=October 2016}} {{Use British English|date=October 2016}} '''Holly Brockwell''' (born 1985 or 1986){{Cite news|url=http://www.nottinghampost.com/scottish-referendum-tweet-nottingham-resident/story-22952712-detail/story.html |title=Scottish referendum: How a tweet from a Nottingham resident took the Twittersphere by storm |date=2014-09-19 |newspaper=[[Nottingham Post]] |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141210112425/http://www.nottinghampost.com/scottish-referendum-tweet-nottingham-resident/story-22952712-detail/story.html |archivedate=2014-12-10 }} is an English [[Technology journalism|technology journalist]], [[Copywriting|copywriter]] and the founder of the tech site ''Gadgette''. She has also written columns for ''[[Gizmodo]]'', the [[BBC]], ''[[The Guardian]]'' and other media outlets. == Early life == Brockwell is from [[Nottingham]] and currently lives in London. She originally hoped to study computer science at university, but instead took a degree in English and became an advertising copywriter and an assistant reviewer and then an editor at ''ShinyShiny'', a women's technology website.{{cite news |author=zroll |url=http://www.look.co.uk/list-for-life/people/what-its-like-to-be-a-woman-in-tech-media-11367 |title='What it’s like to be a tech journalist' |type=interview |newspaper=[[Look (UK magazine)|Look]] |date=2015-12-09 |accessdate=2017-08-01 }} === NHS sterilization denial === In 2012 Brockwell requested the ability to be [[Sterilization (medicine)|sterilized]] from the United Kingdom's [[National Health Service]]. She was denied due to her age,{{Cite news|url=http://www.bbc.com/news/magazine-34521525 |title=100 Women 2015: Desperate not to have children |date=2015-11-22|work=Magazine |publisher=[[BBC]] |access-date=2017-08-01 }} which according to the NHS is common practice because surgeons prefer to wait until prospective patients are at least 30 years of age and have had children.{{Cite web|url=http://www.nhs.uk/conditions/contraception-guide/pages/female-sterilisation.aspx#Who|title=Female sterilisation - Contraception guide |website=NHS Choices |publisher=National Health Service |access-date=2017-06-03}} At the end of March 2016, after turning 30, she was granted permission for the procedure.{{cite news |first=Holly |last=Blott |url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/femail/article-3513800/Holly-Brockwell-reveals-happiness-wins-four-year-battle-sterilised-Morning.html |title=Woman, 30, defends being sterilised after winning four year battle with NHS |newspaper=[[The Daily Mail]] |date=2016-03-29 }} == Blogging and ''Gadgette'' == In 2012 Brockwell blogged about an advertising agency and its campaign about benefit schemes for their staff [https://hollybrockwell.com/2012/09/05/ad-agency-laughs-at-people-on-benefits/ Ad agency laughs at people on benefits by Holly Brockwell] which sparked controversy and negative publicity.[http://www.decisionmarketing.co.uk/news/iris-faces-uproar-over-chav-parody Iris faces uproar over ‘chav’ parody by Decision Marketing] The agency accused Brockwell of libel but later apologised. The next year Brockwell sparked controversy again after writing about a 2013 [[Hyundai]] advert that was later withdrawn.[http://www.independent.co.uk/news/media/advertising/hyundai-pulls-viral-zero-emission-car-ad-featuring-man-attempting-suicide-8588650.html Hyundai pulls viral zero emission car ad featuring man attempting suicide by [[The Independent]]], In 2015 Brockwell started her own women’s tech site called ''Gadgette'' in order to tackle the apparent condescending and discriminating attitudes towards women in the technology industry. The launch of the website faced backlash, according to an op-ed by Brockwell.{{cite news |first=Holly |last=Brockwell |url=https://www.theguardian.com/media-network/2015/jul/22/sexism-tech-industry-womens-site |title=I've been viciously attacked for daring to start a women's tech site |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |type=opinion |date=2015-07-22 }} In 2017 she was the first journalist hired by [[Wikitribune]].{{cite news |first=Kitty |last=Knowles |url=https://www.thememo.com/2017/07/07/wikitribune-inside-the-media-mashup-waging-war-on-fake-news/ |title=WikiTribune: Inside the media mashup waging war on fake news |website=The Memo |date=2017-07-07 |accessdate=2017-08-01 }} == Awards == In 2015 she won the SheSays Award for The Drum Woman of the Year.{{cite web |first=Katie |last=McQuater |url=http://www.thedrum.com/news/2015/11/13/editor-chief-tech-publication-gadgette-holly-brockwell-named-drum-woman-year-shesays |title=Editor-in-chief of tech publication Gadgette Holly Brockwell named The Drum Woman of the Year at SheSays Awards |website=The Drum |date=2015-11-13 }} ==References== {{Reflist}} ==External links== *[https://hollybrockwell.com/ Personal website] {{DEFAULTSORT:Brockwell, Holly}} [[Category:Women technology writers]] [[Category:Technology journalists]] [[Category:British women writers]] [[Category:Living people]] [[Category:1980s births]]
