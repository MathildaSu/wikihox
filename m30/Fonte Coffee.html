It was founded in [[Seattle, Washington]] by Paul Odom in 1992. The company provides a variety of different products, including [[Espresso]] pods, [[decaffeinated coffee]], [[teas]] and a variety of blends of [[coffee]]. ==History== The company was founded in 1992 by Paul Odom. Odom started his career in the food and beverage industry, working for [[Coca-Cola]] and also Frito-lay. After meeting [[roaster]] Steve Smith in 1991, he decided to develop the startup Fonte Coffee a year later. From the outset the Smith and Odom wanted to create a special [[coffee]] and aimed to do so with a Probat Roaster, which is known for its quality within the industry.{{cite news|last1=Abu-Jaber|first1=Diana|title=The Coffee Caste System: A-list beans for A-list places|publisher=[[The Oregonian]]|date=June 2001}} After developing numerous variations during the first 10 years, the company served over 200 different outlets in the United States, Canada and Japan. The [[Boston Herald]] reported in 2003 about the unique approach the company took in roasting coffee beans for its customers. The company had begun taking orders via the phone and also the [[Internet]]. It was reported that once an order had been placed, the senior roaster Smith would personally roast the coffee beans for each client.{{cite news|last1=Exum|first1=David|title=Fonté Coffee offers individual attention|publisher=[[Boston Herald]]|date=December 3, 2003}} In 2004, the company began roasting a unique [[Ethiopia]]n blend of coffee. The [[Coffee production in Ethiopia|Ethiopian Harrar]] is a major part of [[coffee bean]] production in [[Ethiopia]]. After Smith was impressed by the [[Coffee production in Ethiopia|Ethiopian Harrar]], Fonte created a special production [[coffee]], which was available at various upmarket coffee chains.{{cite news|last1=Cassidy|first1=Laura|title=Eat Your Heart Out|publisher=[[Seattle Weekly]]|date=June 16, 2004}} The following year, the [[coffee]] company launched Bahama Bama, which was an iced tea brand created by the company.{{cite news|title=The Fresh Face of Coffee (and Tea)|publisher=Pastry Art & Design|date=March 2005}} During the same period they also introduced a Chinese [[black tea]]{{cite news|title=Fonte Coffee and their new releases|agency=The Gourmet Retailer|date=April 28, 2005}} and their coffee blends were also reviewed in the [[Chicago Tribune]].{{cite news|last1=Enna|first1=Renee|title=Good Eating - Custom Coffee|publisher=[[Chicago Tribune]]|date=April 27, 2005}} 2005 also saw their roast-to-order service reach record sales.{{cite news|title=Record growth for Fonté’s roast-to-order program|publisher=CoffeeTalk|date=22 May 2005}} In 2006, Steve Smith was one of 32 master roasters to judge at the [[Cup of Excellence]] in [[Brazil]]. Smith was responsible for grading 550 entries and judging the best coffee crop in [[Brazil]].{{cite news|title=Fonté Roaster Judges in Brazil|publisher=Fresh Cup Magazine|date=February 2006}} In the same year, Odom discussed how he had founded the company and focused heavily on producing quality [[coffee]] products. During the interview with CAFE, he said he focused on selling his products initially to [[chef]]s, [[restaurateur]]s and [[hotelier]]s.{{cite news|last1=Schwartz|first1=Lynn|title=Coffee Roasting with Paul Odom|publisher=CAFÉ|date=March 2006}} In April 2006, the company announced that it would be releasing a new line of [[iced tea]]s. The range was branded the Select Series and consisted of hand-crafted teas. They included a black tea and a blend of several Asian teas.{{cite news|title=Fonté Coffee Roaster announces new iced tea line|publisher=CoffeeTalk|date=25 April 2006}} Smith featured in [[FOX News]] discussing different coffee beans and blends in 2009.{{cite news|last1=Ferretti|first1=Elena|title=King of Coffee|publisher=FoxNews|date=August 25, 2009}} Fonté opened their first coffee bar in [[Seattle]] in 2009 and it went onto win a number of awards.{{cite news|last1=Raisen|first1=Hanna|title=Seattle's 10 Best Coffee Houses|publisher=[[Seattle Weekly]]|date=11 June 2012}} A 20th Anniversary limited edition [[coffee]] blend was created in 2012 by Smith and Odom.{{cite news|title=Fonte Coffee Roaster Marks 20 Years With Limited Edition "anniversary Blend"|publisher=StarChefs}} ==Roasting== Steve Smith is seen as the company's master [[roaster]] and has discussed his roasting techniques and schedules in the media. In 2005, he featured in The Morning Cup where he discussed his plans over a 7 day period.{{cite news|title=Master Roaster Steve Smith offers glimpse at the daily life of an artisan|publisher=The Morning Cup|date=April 18, 2005}} In 2007, Smith featured in Roast Magazine to discuss how to put together a roasting schedule. The company specialises in roasting to order, which Smith discussed at length. He also stated that making to decision to roast to order is one of the biggest decisions a roaster has to make.{{cite news|last1=Smith|first1=Steve|title=Roasting in Good Time|publisher=Roast Magazine|date=Jan 2007}} ==Coffee Bars== In August 2009, the company opened its first wine and coffee bar in downtown [[Seattle]]. The coffee supplied at the establishment is usually small, one off roasts of the rarest beans from different global locations.{{cite news|title=Fonté Wine and Coffee Bar|publisher=Urbanspoon Seattle}} In 2010, the coffee bar was ranked as the 4th place to get [[coffee]] by [[Seattle Weekly]] in the city of [[Seattle]].{{cite news|last1=Tosti|first1=Rose|title=The Best in Coffee 2010|publisher=[[Seattle Weekly]]|date=27 December 2010}} ==References== {{Reflist}} [[Category:Coffee brands]] [[Category:Coffee houses of the United States]] [[Category:Coffee in Seattle, Washington]] [[Category:Companies based in Seattle, Washington]]
