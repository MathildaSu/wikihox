xml:space="preserve" bytes="1537"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Bhojpuri Boys|timestamp=20140101190357|year=2014|month=January|day=1|substed=yes|help=off}} {{refimprove|date=December 2012}} {{notability|music|date=March 2008}} '''The Bhojpuri Boys''' is a [[Music in Mauritius|musical group]] from [[Mauritius]].{{cite book|author=Rosabelle Boswell|title=Le malaise cr√©ole: ethnic identity in Mauritius|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=gN9Kc6685BQC&pg=PA206|accessdate=25 December 2011|year=2006|publisher=Berghahn Books|isbn=978-1-84545-075-5|page=206}} They are named after the ''[[Bhojpuri]]'' language, which they tried to revive in hybrid form. Their band's lead, Mr Kishore (Mewanand) Taucoory, the lyricist of the band, launched his first album ''Langaro'' in 1994. Mr Kishore Taucoory inherited traditional music from his father Mr Sirkissoon Taucoory (a musician and singer of his time). They have been winners of local and international competitions representing Mauritius. Their songs include ''Hey Langaro'', ''Lougarou'', ''Damad baboo'', ''Nisa Nisa'', ''Hey dadi'', ''Naiya Sirey'', ''Baje Baje'', and ''La France''. ==References== {{reflist}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Bhojpuri Boys}} [[Category:Mauritian music]] {{Mauritius-stub}}
