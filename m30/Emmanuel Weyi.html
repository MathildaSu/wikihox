Chairman emeritus, Colorado Sickle Cell Foundation (2014–present) |signature = |website = {{url|emmanuelweyi.com}} }} '''Emmanuel Ntima Weyi''' is a presidential candidate{{cite web|year=2015|title=Emmanuel Weyi|publisher=Emmanuel Weyi|url=http://emmanuelweyi.com|accessdate=March 19, 2015}} for the [[Democratic Republic of the Congo|Democratic Republic of Congo]]. He served as the Co-founder and [[Chief Executive Officer]] of Groupe Weyi International{{cite web|year=2015|title=groupe weyi international|publisher=groupe weyi international|url=http://groupeweyiinternational.com/|accessdate=March 19, 2015}} – a sustainable mining and [[renewable energy]] enterprise focused on the Congo from 2009-2014. He also founded {{fact|date=March 2015}} the Colorado Sickle Cell Foundation,{{cite web|year=2015|title=Colorado Sickle Cell Foundation|publisher=Colorado Sickle Cell Foundation|url=http://coloradosicklecell.com/|accessdate=March 19, 2015}} a [[nonprofit organization|non-profit organization]] focused on bringing awareness, medical care and funding to sufferers of [[sickle-cell disease|sickle-cell anemia]]. ==Early life and education== Weyi was born in [[Kisangani]] on the 29th of May 1959, where parents his parents were both from. His father, Robert Weyi, was a banker and his mother, Catherine Ntima Diamoneka, was a businesswoman. Weyi grew up in a family with 2 brothers and 7 sisters.{{cite news|author=Nakaso, Dan|date=February 6, 2015 |title=Mining Interests. Connect &Collaborate, ICOSA Network. |newspaper=Radio| url=|accessdate=March 14, 2015}} One of his siblings passed away, an event that is said to have profoundly affected him. Weyi moved to the capital of [[Kinshasa]] with his family as in 1960 as a young infant. In 1971, his father became the head of the [[Union des Banques Congolaises|Union Zaroise de Banques]]. That same year, Weyi completed his completed his elementary schooling at a [[Catholic school]].{{cite news|author=Nakaso, Dan|date=February 6, 2015 |title=Mining Interests. ICOSA Network. |newspaper=ICOSA Network| url=http://www.icosamedia.com/2015/02/connect-collaborate-mining-interests/|accessdate=March 14, 2015}} In 1972, at the behest of his father, Weyi went me back to the Bas-Congo to attend a Catholic boarding school for secondary education, making frequent trips back to capital. After graduating high school in 1979, Weyi attended Ecole Internationale Des Hautes Etudes in [[Paris]] to study International Relations. In 1985, Weyi married Odette Kisolokele, whom he had met in Paris a few years prior. The two held their wedding in Kinshasa before moving to the United States with their firstborn son. ==Early career== {{original research|date=March 2015}} After living in the US for a year, Weyi attended a language school for 12 months in North Dallas, where he learned how to speak and write proper English, adding a fifth language to his fluency in [[French language|French]], [[Lingala]], [[Swahili language|Swahili]] and [[Kongo language|Kikongo]]. In 1989, Weyi spent two years taking computer science classes and then started the Weyi Trading Company, an importing and exporting business that he ran until the end of 1994. It was in this early 90’s period that Weyi began to learn about the mining industry and become versed in international business. In 1995, he sold Weyi Trading Company and moved with his family to Denver, Colorado. ==Later career== In 1998, Weyi founded Cliché Advertising Firm, an advertising agency that focused on promoting clients in international business and nonprofit causes. In 2003, he moved his business focus back to the Congo, and started Groupe Weyi, a social enterprise focused on sustainable mining in the Democratic Republic of Congo.{{cite web|year=2011|title=Conflict minerals: demonise the criminals, not the miners|location=Washington, D.C.|publisher=oecdinsights|url=http://oecdinsights.org/2011/10/10/conflict-minerals-demonise-the-criminals-not-the-miners/|accessdate=March 14, 2015}} Additionally, his representation and interaction with non-profit agencies in the US and Congo led him to found the Colorado Sickle Cell Foundation in 2005. The organization’s vision was to provide awareness and assistance regarding sickle cell anemia to families, friends, and communities in the state of Colorado and abroad. In 2011, entrepreneur Chuck Blakeman,{{cite web|year=2014|title=The Emerging Work World, author page of Chuck Blakeman on Inc Magazine|publisher=INC magazine|url=http://www.inc.com/author/chuck-blakeman|accessdate=March 14, 2015}} and the company became Groupe Weyi International under the two co-founders. The two began to comment on the policies and corruption within the Congo in an endeavor to bring awareness and transparency to business in the region.{{cite web|year=2014|title= "The complete and total embargo of artisanal Congolese minerals." (PDF) SEC. Commentary by Chuck Blakeman and Emmanuel Weyi|publisher=SEC gov|url=http://www.sec.gov/comments/s7-40-10/s74010-530.pdf|accessdate=March 14, 2015}} The company expanded its headquarters into a second branch in Kinshasa, DRC. ==2011 Election Invitation== In 2011, after having been a member of the Colorado Mining Association for 2 years, Weyi began to take a deeper look at the business and political systems in the Congo. After seeing the suffering in the Congolese people, and experiencing a large company theft while on a trip to the Congo, Weyi decided to try to solve the problem from the root by moving toward the political realm. He decided to form a political party called Congolese Central Party.{{fact|date=March 2015}} As the founder and president of the party, he assembled a team to begin strategizing and crafting an innovate system to improve the opportunities and condition of the Congolese people. That year he was invited to run for the presidency of the Democratic Republic of Congo by the Presidential Coalition for the 2011 Democratic Republic of Congo election.{{cite web|year=2015|title= Coaltion Presidentielle pour Les Elections 2011 En Republique Democratic du Congo (PDF) Congo Net Radio |publisher=Congo Net Radio|url=http://www.congonetradio.com/Benkalala-doc-1-Invitation-Washington.pdf|accessdate=March 14, 2015}}{{failed verification|date=March 2015}} Weyi declined the invitation in order to set his sights on full and effective leadership in Congo for the 2016 election.{{fact|date=March 2015}} ==2016 Presidential Campaign== Weyi decided to begin setting up a campaign in 2012 for the 2016 election in the Democratic Republic of Congo, officially announcing his public candidacy in December 2014.{{cite web|year=2015|title= Emmanuel Weyi: Democratic Republic of Congo Presidential Candidate. |publisher=youtube |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yVKMsRFFb5E|accessdate=March 14, 2015}} He has been running under the slogan “Stand by Me.” ==External links== '''Official''' * {{Official website|http://emmanuelweyi.com/}} (Campaign) ==References== {{Reflist}} {{Persondata | NAME = Weyi, Emmanuel | ALTERNATIVE NAMES = | SHORT DESCRIPTION = Congolese politician | DATE OF BIRTH = May 29, 1959 | PLACE OF BIRTH = [[Kisangani]], [[Democratic Republic of the Congo|Democratic Republic of Congo]]. | DATE OF DEATH = | PLACE OF DEATH = }} {{DEFAULTSORT:Weyi, Emmanuel}} [[Category:1959 births]] [[Category:Living people]] [[Category:Democratic Republic of the Congo politicians]]
