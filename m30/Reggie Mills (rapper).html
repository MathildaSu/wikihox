}} '''Nicolas Reggie Mills''' (born October 14, 1994), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Reggie Mills''', is an American rapper and songwriter, and former executive at [[Amalgam Digital]].{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/5830e987e4b0d28e55215040?timestamp=1479719705313|work=The Huffington Post|title=Reggie Mills: The Next Independent Rapper Success Story|accessdate=November SXSW 2016|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} He then headlined a thirty city North American tour where he performed with acts including [[Lil Uzi Vert]], [[Bryson Tiller]], [[Young Thug]], [[A$AP Rocky]], [[Travis Scott]] and more.{{cite web|url=http://artistecard.com/reggiemills#!/shows/2016/169123|work=Artist E-Card|title=Reggie Mills Tour 2016|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} ==Early life== Reggie is the oldest of 3 children born to his parents, George and Juliette Mills. Immediately following his birth in [[Montclair, New Jersey]], he was raised by his grandmother in [[Teshie-Nungua]], [[Ghana]] for the first seven years of his life. From the age of 12, he gained an interest in creating music.{{cite web|url=http://hiphopapproved.org/news/hiphop-approved-exclusive-interview-with-reggie-mills|work=Hip Hop Approved|title=HipHop Approved Exclusive Interview with Reggie Mills|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} He picked up drumming and playing guitar while attending [[St. Joseph High School (Metuchen, New Jersey)|St. Joseph High School]] in [[Metuchen, New Jersey]]. In 2012 Reggie enrolled in [[Rutgers University]] in Newark, NJ, where he eventually dropped out after only a month and a half. ==Career== ===Amalgam Digital=== In 2013 Reggie connected with the founder and then CEO of [[Amalgam Digital]], Joe Anyextee.{{cite web|url=http://www.villagevoice.com/music/amalgam-ceo-mc-anyextee-goes-from-the-boardroom-to-the-booth-6601055|work=Village Voice|title=Amalgam CEO MC Anyextee Goes From The Boardroom To The Booth|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} He began interning with the company immediately. In 2014 he was hired as a record executive where he focused on talent scouting and managed a team of interns. In 2015 Mills was promoted to Vice President of the company where he facilitated all business affairs, oversaw artist development and routed tours across North America which included showcases at the SXSW Music Festival.{{cite web|url=http://2015.do512.com/events/2015/3/22/amlgam-digital-hiphopdx-2015-music-festival-showcase|work=Do 512|title=AMLGAM DIGITAL & HIPHOPDX 2015 MUSIC FESTIVAL SHOWCASE|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} ===Music=== In December 2014, Reggie released his first [[Extended play|EP]], ''Live To Prosper'', under the Amalgam Digital imprint.{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/live-to-prosper/id955928063|work=iTunes|title=Reggie Mills: Live To Prosper|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} This release included four features from then [[Taylor Gang Records]] artist, [[Neako]],{{cite web|url=http://rapdose.com/2013/06/18/neako-discusses-lvlysl-upcoming-projects-working-with-currensy/|work=Rap Does|title=Neako Discusses LVLYSL, Upcoming Projects, Working With Currensy, Rappers He Admired As A Kid & More|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} as well as production from [[Curtiss King]].{{cite web|url=http://www.risinghype.com/video/reggie-mills-ppdo-2/|work=Rising Hype|title=REGGIE MILLS – PPDO|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} Following the release of the project, he began the Live To Prosper tour where he performed with artists including [[Cam’ron]], [[DJ Quik]], [[Joe Budden]] and [[Dash (rapper)|Da$h]].{{cite web|url=https://eventseeker.com/event/aug-21-2015/154404601-amalgam-digital-deady-boyz-present-goodjoblarry-party-concert-live-performances-by-larry-june-secaucus|work=Event Seeker|title=Amalgam Digital & Deady Boyz Present #LiveToProsperTour|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} This tour led up to his debut at SXSW 2015 where Reggie hosted and performed alongside [[Maxo Kream]], [[Neako]], [[Nitty Scott, MC]], [[G4SHI]], [[Chuuwee]], & more. In November 2015, Mills released the Reggie Miller EP featuring atlanta rappers UnoTheActivist & Thouxanbandfauni. The inspiration for the EP came from retired [[NBA]] star [[Reggie Miller]].{{cite web|url=http://respect-mag.com/2015/11/listen-now-reggie-mills-reggie-miller-ep/|work=Respect Mag|title=Listen Now: Reggie Mills – ‘Reggie Miller (EP)’|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} In March 2016 Reggie hosted and performed at seven SXSW stages. In April 2016 Reggie released the Reggie Flair EP with features from Boogz Boogetz,{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/boogz-boogetz-and-reggie-millz-when-i-want-new-song.1965978.html|work=Hot New Hip Hop|title=Boogz Boogetz & Reggie Millz - When I Want: New Song|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} UnoTheActivist and Thouxanbandfauni.{{cite web|url=http://www.audiomack.com/song/dj-warface/reggie-flair-ft-thouxanbandfauni-prod-by-the-architectz|title=Nasa-Reggie Flair (Ft. ThouxanbandFauni) Prod. By The Architectz|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} Following the release of this project, he began the North American leg of the Reggie Flair tour.{{cite web|url=http://blog.dramalikethedj.com/reggie-millz-reggie-flair-ep/|work=Drama Like The DJ|title=Reggie Mills – Reggie Flair EP|accessdate=November 15, 2016}} ==References== {{Reflist|2}} ==External links== *[http://www.ReggieMills.com Reggie Mills Official Website] *[http://www.Twitter.com/MillsReggie Reggie Mills Twitter] *[http://www.Instagram.com/ReggieMills Reggie Mills Instagram] *[http://www.Facebook.com/ReggieXMills Reggie Mills Facebook Page] {{DEFAULTSORT:Mills, Reggie}} [[Category:1994 births]] [[Category:21st-century American musicians]] [[Category:African-American male rappers]] [[Category:East Coast hip hop musicians]] [[Category:Living people]] [[Category:People from New Jersey]] [[Category:Rappers from New Jersey]] [[Category:American hip hop singers]]
