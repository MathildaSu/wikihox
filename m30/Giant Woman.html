The episode follows Steven ([[Zach Callison]]) urging Amethyst and Pearl ([[Michaela Dietz]] and [[Deedee Magno]] respectively) to demonstrate their ability to fuse together into a giant four-armed woman named Opal (guest voiced by [[Aimee Mann]]). Amethyst and Pearl, however, will not stop squabbling. The episode was written by Joe Johnston and [[Jeff Liu]], with Ki-Yong Bae, Jin-Hee Park and Young-Wha Yoon providing [[animation direction]], Kevin Dart serving as [[art director]] and [[Ian Jones-Quartey]] providing [[supervising direction]]. According to Liu, the episode was worked on nearly a year after premiering. [[Rebecca Sugar]] stated the song "Giant Woman", which she performed at the 2013 [[San Diego Comic-Con International]], was "humbled by the presence" of Mann's guest voice. In the United States, the episode originally aired on [[Cartoon Network]] on February 24, 2014. The episode was seen by 1.893 million viewers, becoming the most watched episode aired by the network for the week of April 7 to April 13, 2014. ''Cartoon Brew''{{'s}} Kendra Beltran questioned Steven's desire to see Opal, while highlighting Steven's reaction toward Steven Jr.'s plight as "the moment of the week." ''[[The A.V. Club]]''{{'s}} Eric Thurm opined that the episode "[[Steven the Sword Fighter]]" contained "no full song from creator and ''[[Adventure Time]]'' musical wizard Rebecca Sugar here along the lines of 'Giant Woman'". ==Plot== {{More plot | date=March 2014 }} Steven urges Amethyst and Pearl to fuse together into a giant four-armed woman named Opal. Amethyst and Pearl, however, will not stop squabbling. Soon after seeing him being "eaten" by a giant bird monster they change into Opal. Steven who is still confused by the fused woman is assured that she is Pearl and Amethyst when they sing the first lines of "Giant woman". ==Production== [[File:Aimee Mann 2008.jpg|thumb|upright|right|[[Aimee Mann]], pictured in 2008, voiced the character of Opal.]] The episode was written by Joe Johnston and [[Jeff Liu]]. Ki-Yong Bae, Jin-Hee Park and Young-Wha Yoon provided [[animation direction]], Kevin Dart served as [[art director]] and [[Ian Jones-Quartey]] provided [[supervising direction]]. According to Liu, the episode was worked on nearly a year after premiering upon producing an animation for Amethyst and Pearl's fusion dance. The final board, written tenth in production order, was submitted to the network on June 11, 2013, before being approved June 26. The song "Giant Woman" was performed by series creator [[Rebecca Sugar]] at the 2013 [[San Diego Comic-Con International]]. Sugar stated that the song was "humbled by the presence" of her "absolute musical hero," [[Aimee Mann]], who provided the voice of Opal. The episode also features the songs "Opal" and "Amalgam" by pianist Aivi Tran and sound designer Steven Velma of the musical group Aivi & Surasshu. The former song was composed upon consideration of how Pearl and Amethyst would sound as a merged entity, knowing that the result "would be powerful and magnificent". They noted that Pearl would straighten out Amethyst's "normal [[Swing (jazz performance style)|swinging]] beat", and Amethyst "would push Pearl's classy piano towards a more wild playing style." Both tracks were released onto [[SoundCloud]] on February 28 and March 2, respectively. The episode was first announced onto the site's official production blog with an panel of the episode's storyboard. In addition, several promotional pieces were published by Johnston and character designer Colin Howard, encouraging followers to watch. Johnston stated the episode was his favorite episode to work on with Liu, but has since been "eclipsed by more recent stuff we've done." Various background art and animation props were released after its premiere, before the full storyboard was made available on [[Scribd]] on March 5. ==Reception== Upon its premiere on February 24, 2014, "Giant Woman" was seen by 1.893 million viewers. It received a [[Nielsen ratings|Nielsen rating]] of 0.36 for adults in the 18- to 49-year-old demographic, meaning that it was seen by 455,000 adults upon its premiere. In addition, it received a Nielsen household rating of 1.2, and was ranked as the most watched episode aired by the network for the week of April 7 to April 13, 2014. Writing for ''Cartoon Brew'', Kendra Beltran questioned Steven's desire to see Opal, wondering if the fascination was "a symbol for a growing boy going through some changes". She noted the series to usually not "make me laugh out loud", but highlighted Steven's tearful reaction toward Steven Jr. being devoured by a bird as "the moment of the week."
