xml:space="preserve" bytes="6541"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Gold (2015 film)|timestamp=20160615121915|year=2016|month=June|day=15|substed=yes}} {{coi|date=June 2016}} {{Infobox film | name = Gold | image = Gold Poster AlexanderTuschinski.jpg | caption = | director = [[Alexander Tuschinski]] | producer = Alexander Tuschinski | writer = | starring = | music = [[Ludwig van Beethoven]] | cinematography = Alexander Tuschinski | editing = Alexander Tuschinski | distributor = | released = {{film date|2015|07|03|Greece}} | runtime = 7 minutes | country = Germany | language = English | budget = }} '''''Gold.''''' is a German [[experimental film|experimental]] [[short film|short]] [[documentary film]] directed by [[Alexander Tuschinski]]. It intercuts [[ghost town|abandoned 19th century gold-mining towns]] in the desert with [[sequoia tree]]s in a forest. The film had its world premiere at [[Mykonos Biennale]] on July 3, 2015,{{cite news|url=http://mykonosbiennale.com/filmfestival/film/gold-alexander-tuschinski/|title=Mykonos Biennale 2015: Official program-guide entry for "Gold." - the program-gude omits the full stop at the end of the film's title.|accessdate=21 June 2015}}. where it was screened in competition{{cite news|url=https://filmfreeway.com/festival/MykonosBiennale|title=Film Freeway: Page for Mykonos Biennale 2015 - the Golden Pelican is awarded to selected films from the program.|accessdate=21 June 2015}}. and received the Biennale's Golden Pelican Award by [[Lydia Venieri]].{{cite news|url=http://plusinsight.de/2015/08/alexander-tuschinskis-experimenteller-kurzfilm-gold/|title=plusinsight: article from August 2015 about Gold winning the award at Mykonos Biennale|accessdate=August 14, 2015}}. It had its German premiere at [[Berlin Short Film Festival]] on July 4, 2015,{{cite news|url=http://www.berlinshort.com/screenings-2015/|title=Berlin Short Film Festival 2015: Official program-guide.|accessdate=21 June 2015}}, was screened in competition at [[:de:Internationales Filmfest Braunschweig|Braunschweig International Film Festival]] 2015.{{cite news|url=http://www.filmportal.de/film/gold_b4ca6bba450e486d9e8b621fdd8b48ac|title=Filmportal: Data about the film (German)|accessdate=10 June 2016}} and had its US-premiere at [[Hollywood Reel Independent Film Festival]] in 2016. == Production == The film is set to the fourth movement of [[Symphony No. 7 (Beethoven)|Beethoven's seventh symphony]], which has been called "Apotheosis of Dance" by [[Richard Wagner]]. The director's intention was to intercut nature and human structures to show nature overtaking. It was filmed with a tight schedule and the crew travelled long distances in a short amount of time to get many different shots needed. Tuschinski edited the film from six hours of material from "countless camera-angles", as most shots are shown only very briefly due to the often rapid editing. Planning the film, he was inspired by the early works of his friend and mentor [[Hugo Niebeling]] that connect cinematoraphy and music in a very direct way.{{cite news|url=http://plusinsight.de/2015/06/gold-von-alexander-tuschinski/|title=plusinsight: Interview with Alexander Tuschinski about "Gold." and its premiere at Mykonos Biennale|accessdate=21 June 2015}}. == Reception == {{Quote|text=He knows how to use the power of images.|sign=[[Ursula Drees]]|source=article about "Gold" on plusinsight.com{{cite news|url=http://plusinsight.de/2015/06/gold-von-alexander-tuschinski/|title=Plusinsight: Article about Gold from June 2015, featuring an interview with Alexander Tuschinski|accessdate=21 June 2015}}.}} {{Quote|text=Alexander Tuschinski in "Gold." narrates the constant story of the exploits just with images and music (...).
