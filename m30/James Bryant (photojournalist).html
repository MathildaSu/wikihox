Bryant''' (born June 18, 1952 in [[Muscogee County, Georgia]]){{citation needed|date=May 2012}} is an American [[photojournalist]]. Bryant is a freelance photojournalist who has covered a wide variety of photo assignments ranging from local news and sports to international travel and war. ==Personal life== Bryant studied [[photojournalism]] at [[Syracuse University]], graduating in 1978. He resides in [[Port Orchard, Washington]]. ==Career== {{citation needed|date=May 2012}} Bryant has given numerous seminars and classes on photojournalism throughout his career. His photos have appeared in the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'', ''[[LA Times]]'', ''[[Newsweek]]'', ''[[San Francisco Chronicle]]'', ''[[The New York Times]]'', ''[[Toronto Star]]'', ''[[Washington Post]]'', and many other newspapers and magazines. Bryant is a member of the [[American Society of Media Photographers]] (ASMP) and the [[National Press Photographers Association]] (NPPA). In his career, spanning over three decades, Bryant's location assignments have taken him to more than 25 countries, some of which include [[Kenya]], [[Vietnam]], [[Japan]], [[Palau]], [[Thailand]], and the [[Azores Islands]]. ==Book Contributions== * ''A Day in the Life of Japan'' (1985){{cite book|author=Smolan, Rick Smolan and Cohen, David Elliot|title=A Day in the Life of Japan|publisher=Collins Pub San Francisco|date=November 1985|isbn=0002175800 |isbn=978-0002175807}} * ''Photojournalism, The Visual Approach'' (1986){{cite book|title=Photjournalism, The Visual Approach|author=Hoy, Frank P.|publisher=Prentice Hall College Div;
