He is the son of a steelworker and cleaning lady. His father was able to talk [[The Kiski School]] outside of [[Pittsburgh|Pittsburgh, PA]] into giving him a full scholarship.{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2006/04/30/business/yourmoney/30lunch.html|title=Working With an Ex, and Keeping It Civil|date=2006-04-30|publisher=nytimes.com|accessdate=2015-05-13}} Ferrazzi then attended [[Yale University]], where he founded the [[Sigma Chi]] chapter there, and [[Harvard Business School]].{{Cite web|url=http://www.inc.com/magazine/20030101/25049.html|title=The 10 Secrets of a Master Networker|date=2003-01-01|website=Inc.com|access-date=2016-02-18}} == Career == After graduating from Harvard, he joined [[management consulting]] firm [[Deloitte]] as an entry level analyst, and rose through the company's [[marketing]] department to eventually sit on the executive committee as [[chief marketing officer|CMO]], and was nominated as a [[Partner (business rank)|partner]].{{cite web|url=http://money.cnn.com/2006/05/15/magazines/business2/welllikedsuccess/|title=You don't have to be well liked to succeed|date=2006-05-16|publisher=money.cnn.com|accessdate=2016-05-09}} At age 32, he was hired by [[Starwood Hotels and Resorts Worldwide|Starwood Hotels]] to be their chief marketing officer (CMO), and was reportedly the youngest CMO in the Fortune 500. In 2000, he left Starwood to found YaYa Media, an entertainment and marketing company, which he sold to investment company American Vantage in 2003. Ferrazzi left YaYa Media to found Ferrazzi Greenlight, a Los Angeles-based strategic [[consulting firm]], and its associated Greenlight Research Institute. The firm and Institute focus on the importance of positive relationships to business success.{{cite web|url=http://knowledge.wharton.upenn.edu/article/keith-ferrazzi-relationships-crucial-success/|title=Keith Ferrazzi â€“ Why Relationships Are Crucial to Success|date=2014-02-19|publisher=wharton.upenn.edu|accessdate=2016-05-09}}[[File:Ferrazzi with Kekulov.jpg|thumb|Keith Ferrazzi (right) with Alexey Kekulov in Moscow]] Ferrazzi has been described as a master "networker" by [[Inc.
