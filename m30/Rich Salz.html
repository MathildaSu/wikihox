xml:space="preserve" bytes="3228"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Rich Salz|timestamp=20140610154647|year=2014|month=June|day=10|substed=yes|help=off}} {{BLP sources|date=January 2013}} [[File:Rich Salz Sept 2009.jpg|thumb|Rich Salz in Oxford (Sept. 2009)]] '''Rich Salz''' is a Principal Engineer at Akamai Technologies. His previous position was technical lead for the XML appliance products at [[IBM]].http://xmlsummerschool.com/faculty2009/richsalz/ He came to IBM when he was [[Chief Security Officer]] of [[DataPower]], which was acquired by IBM in 2005. He has made numerous contributions to recent work on [[XML]]{{Citation needed|date=February 2007}} and [[SOAP]] specifications,{{Citation needed|date=February 2007}} particularly involving security. For many years, he spelled his name 'Rich $alz' (see [[Talk:Rich Salz]] for an explanation) and was an early contributor to the [[free software movement]]. In 1986 he replaced [[John P. Nelson]] as editor of the original "[[moderated newsgroup|moderated]]" [[Usenet]] group for free source code, mod.sources{{cite newsgroup | title = Many newsgroup postings | author = | date = | newsgroup = mod.sources | id = | url = http://groups.google.com/group/mod.sources | accessdate = 2006-11-18}} (later renamed to comp.sources.unix{{cite newsgroup | title = Many newsgroup postings | author = | date = | newsgroup = comp.sources.unix | id = | url = http://groups.google.com/group/comp.sources.unix | accessdate = 2006-11-18}} ). This [[newsgroup]] was a primary distribution medium for high quality free software before Internet access became available to the public. Salz posted early releases of [[Patch (Unix)|patch]], [[localtime]], [[perl]], [[Concurrent Versions System|cvs]], [[Elm (e-mail client)|elm]], and the first free [[Tar (file format)|tar]] program. He passed the job of editor to [[Paul Vixie]] in 1991.{{Citation needed|date=February 2007}} He also wrote free software, including porting [[AberMUD]] to [[UNIX]] in 1989 and the original release of the well-respected and still-used [[InterNetNews|INN]],https://www.isc.org/software/inn/ which became the de facto software for running [[Usenet]] [[news server]]s, for a few years.{{Citation needed|date=February 2007}} ==External links== * Rich Salz's [http://www.ibm.com/developerworks/blogs/page/soma weblog] ==References== {{Reflist|2}} {{MUDs|state=collapsed}} {{Persondata | NAME = Salz, Rich | ALTERNATIVE NAMES = | SHORT DESCRIPTION = Free software programmer | DATE OF BIRTH = | PLACE OF BIRTH = | DATE OF DEATH = | PLACE OF DEATH = }} {{DEFAULTSORT:Salz, Rich}} [[Category:Year of birth missing (living people)]] [[Category:Living people]] [[Category:MUD developers]] [[Category:Free software programmers]] [[Category:Usenet people|$alz, Rich]] {{compu-bio-stub}} {{unix-stub}} {{Business-bio-stub}} {{MUD-stub}}
