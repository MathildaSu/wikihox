xml:space="preserve" bytes="6224"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Gina Din|timestamp=20150717085239|year=2015|month=July|day=17|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Orphan|date=July 2015}} '''Gina Din''' (born 23 October 1961) is [[East Africa|East Africa's]] leading [[Strategic communication|Strategic Communications]] specialist,http://www.ginadin.com/about.php with business interests spanning the whole of the African continent and beyond. Through her company, the Gina Din Group,http://www.ginadin.com she has built strong and lasting relationships with some of the region's leading brands, including [[Safaricom|Safaricom Ltd.]], [[Kenya Commercial Bank|Kenya Commercial Bank Ltd.]], [[Kenya Airways|Kenya Airways Ltd.]], [[Kenya Red Cross Society]], amongst many others.http://www.ginadin.com/about.php She has also recently been credited with pioneering the march into the Energy Sectorhttp://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=207159515&ticker=U8P1:GR&previousCapId=62604221&previousTitle=CAMAC%2520ENERGY%2520INC for Kenyan women. [[File:Gina Din.JPG|thumb|Gina Din]] ==Early Life and Education== ===Early life=== Gina Din was born in [[Nanyuki]], [[Kenya]], where her parents, Malek and Shamsu Din owned the Sportsman's Arms Hotel. She is the 4th child with 3 older sisters.http://www.standardmedia.co.ke/article/2000022356/i-love-my-life ===Education=== She attended the [[London School of Journalism]] where she graduated with a Bachelor's Degree in Journalism. ==Career== Gina Din started her career at [[Barclays Bank (Kenya)|Barclays Bank of Kenya]] as the Head of Communications at the age of 22. After working at the Bank for 14 years, Gina left to start her own company, Gina Din Corporate Communications, now the Gina Din Group.http://www.ginadin.com The company has grown to be one of the leading [[Public Relations]] and [[Communications]] companies in [[Africa]]. Gina is the founder and Group Executive Chair of the Gina Din Group and has over 29 years of service and experience in providing state of the art media and communication strategy to corporates, governments and NGOs in Africa and beyond. The Gina Din Group has over the years filled a market niche by providing comprehensive crisis management, corporate communications strategy as well as major events planning for many blue chip and international companies. It is an affiliate of Weber Shandwick, a highly-awarded global PR firm. ==Business Ventures and Investments== She is a Director at [http://www.erinenergy.com/home/default.aspx Erin Energy Kenya] which is part of Erin Energy, listed on the [[New York Stock Exchange]]. She also sits on the board of various organizations including The Africa Yoga Projecthttp://www.africayogaproject.org/pages/board The Africa Yoga Project and the James Jordan Foundation.http://www.jamesjordanfoundation.com/ James Jordan Foundation ==Humanitarian Initiatives== Gina Din has been involved in numerous humanitarian initiatives as the [[Goodwill Ambassador]] for the [[Kenya Red Cross Society]]http://www.redcross.or.ke/index.php/goodwill-ambassador and through her own foundation, the Gina Din Foundation.http://www.ginadinfoundation.org/initiatives Through the [[Kenya Red Cross Society]], she helped spearhead the [[Kenyans for Kenya|Kenyans for Kenya Inititive]] that saw 1 billion [[Kenyan shillings|Kenyan Shillings]] raised to tackle famine in the country. She also headed the Chagua Peace Campaignhttp://www.standardmedia.co.ke/thecounties/article/2000098548/gina-din-group-crowned-2013-public-relations-industry-champions in 2008 that saw all presidential candidates in Kenya come together to declare a peaceful election period in the country in 2013. As the chairperson of the Gina Din Foundation, Gina Din has empowered youth and women by linking them to critical resources. Under the auspices of this foundation, in October 2013 Gina launched ‘At the Table with Gina Din’, a TV show that is an avenue to join young high potential Africans with high achievers who work in the same field. The show has a segment called The University Table Talks, a youth-lead series set at rotating universities and is available through Google+ Hangout. The aim of the series is to empower students to engage a diverse audience using an interactive, real time format that enables a global conversation about enterpreneurship, innovation and collaboration. ==Honours and Awards== Gina Din has won multiple Global Public Relations Awards including the [[Stevie Awards]] and the Superior Achievement in Branding and Reputation (SABRE) Awardshttp://aptantech.com/tag/gina-din-corporate-communications/ with her company winning over 100 Public Relations Awards since its inception in 1997. She has also been named by the New African Magazine as one of 100 Most Influential Africans in Businesshttp://newafricanmagazine.com/100-most-influential-africans-business and also received the award of the Moran of the Burning Spear.http://allafrica.com/stories/200312151217.html ==Personal life== Gina Din is married to Christopher Burugu Kariuki, a pilot, and they have two children, Natalya Din-Kariuki, 24, and Naythan Din-Kariuki, 19.http://www.businessdailyafrica.com/Why-I-think-I-m-a-great-mum-and-wife/-/1248928/2666174/-/wl5y6dz/-/index.html ==External links== * [http://www.ginadin.com Official Website] ==References== {{Reflist}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Din, Gina}} [[Category:Leading women in Kenya]] [[Category:List of people by nationality]] [[Category:List of people by occupation]] [[Category:List of women founders]] [[Category:List of women in business]] [[Category:List of foundations]] [[Category:List of Kenyans]] [[Category:List of Africans]] [[Category:Businesspeople]] [[Category:List of women in media]] [[Category:1961 births]] [[Category:Living people]]
