xml:space="preserve" bytes="3811"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Chunati|timestamp=20150711195841|year=2015|month=July|day=11|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Multiple issues| {{refimprove|date=December 2011}} {{orphan|date=February 2012}} }} '''Chunati''' is a small village that is prominent for having a wide range of educational institutes, high rate of literacy and hundreds of years of spiritual traditions. It is located next to the Asian Highway (old Arakan Road) between the port city [[Chittagong]] and tourism town [[Cox's Bazar]] in [[Bangladesh]]. The distance from Chittagong is about 70 km. == History == Chunati is placed in a setting of [[hillock]]s, [[Valley|dales]] and [[meadow]]s encircled with homesteads and natural beauty. According to United Service Organization, the total population of the village was around 6,300 in 2010 with 75% male and 25% female. The local people are involved in farming and small businesses. The small businesses are picking up and have been showing a positive response in micro economy and financial stability of the village. Most of the highly educated people do jobs in private and public sectors, thus live away from the village. The noted sages of earlier domicile have enhanced the cultural flow that was immensely nurtured by yet more resourceful progenies who aptly and amply contributed to their proud legacy for the posterity endowment of cross cultural synthesis epitomizing and regenerating the main stream of the noble heritage. The inhabitants here are always traditionally bounded but susceptible transformations; they tend reshape modernizing it in their own way. Chunati is endowed with a literacy rate of 80%. 45% of which are [[madrasa]], college and university graduates. Among them are 20 PhD holders. The dwellers are predominantly madrasa education oriented and mostly end up in achieving high degrees. Among the educational institutions of Chunati, “Hakimia Aliah Madrasa” occupies the prime position because of its university level and traditional aspect education. Apart from the Aliah Madrasa, it houses a girls’ college, one women madrasa, a high school, several primary schools and a multitude of [[Maktab]]s and Furqaniahs. These institutes provide educational support to meet rising challenges in the job market. Chunati’s culture is based on development of creative faculty. All of the people are almost from similar cultural backgrounds and they are well-satisfied about it. They have no complains about someone else’s cultural aspects and practices and so they all enjoy each other’s cultural events and occasions together, with visions to make it bigger and better. The dwellers are as well very much entertaining; they hold stage performances and present self-composed nuptial songs, which are equally amazing and soothing. Apart from cultural activities, the dwellers actively take part in sports and recreational activities like regular picnics and family gatherings in amazing places around the village. The population of Chunati is a harmonious blend of conservativeness and spirituality. It has always remained a coveted abode for outsiders.
