rock]] [[recording artist]],{{cite web|title=Artist: Jenn Vix |url=http://mp3clans.xyz/artist-jenn-vix/|work=MP3clans.xyz|accessdate=23 December 2015}} [[vocalist]], [[lyricist]], [[songwriter]], arranger, [[Producer (music)|producer]], [[recording engineer]] and [[remixer]], who has ventured into other genres including [[alternative rock]], [[trip hop]], [[darkwave]], modern [[new wave]] and [[Electro-industrial|electro - industrial]].{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix – Songs, Playlists, Videos and Tours – BBC Music|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/83087f44-72e0-4a2c-859b-7908095b6a68|work=[[BBC Music]]|accessdate=23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix - Electronic, Singer-Songwriters, Gothic, Trip-Hop, Female Vocalists |url=http://www.last.fm/music/Jenn+Vix|work=[[Last.fm]]|accessdate=23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix - Alternative, Electronic, Rock |url=http://thenoise-boston.com/2009/04/jenn-vix-feature291/|work=Music New England|accessdate=23 December 2015}} ==Early life and background== Though she was born in [[Providence, Rhode Island]], she spent her teen years in [[New York City]], doing her middle schooling at [[New York City Department of Education|Simon Baruch 104]]. Actor [[Jon Cryer]] was also at this school with her, and Vix appeared in a school play with him, "[[Bye Bye Birdie]]", under the musical direction of Dr. Robert Sharon. She was active in the choral department, and was featured in an octet of female singers, titled "Beauty Shop". This group played shows all over [[NYC]]; at hotels, banks, and other public functions. ==Career== In 1984, Vix performed in a one-off band, singing back-up, along with singer [[Nicole Willis]] from The Soul Investigators, [[Ad-Rock|Adam Horovitz]] of [[The Beastie Boys]], Phil Painson and David Strahan. Her first solo release was "Jenn Vix",{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix Discography - (Jenn Vix) |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/jenn-vix-mw0000310436|work=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=23 December 2015}} a self-titled album of 8 tracks on her own label, Umbrella, which was officially released in January 1995, through a [[New York]] based distributor.{{cite web|title=Album: Jenn Vix |url=http://mp3clans.xyz/album-509a8d7b-f244-49f9-b56d-04b02b57e874/|work=MP3clans.xyz|accessdate=23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix's self-titled debut album |url=https://www.reverbnation.com/jennvix|work=[[ReverbNation]]|accessdate=23 December 2015}} Later on it was sold on [[CDBaby]] which is an online music store specializing in the sale of [[CDs]], [[Vinyl records|vinyl record]] and music downloads from independent musicians to consumers. In 1997 she was one of the first artists on that site. She got a positive response when her album was reviewed in [[Rolling Stone Magazine]].{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix on Rolling Stone Magazine|url=http://www.rollingstone.com/music/pictures/jenn-vix-photos-20001207|work=[[Rolling Stone Magazine]]|accessdate=23 December 2015}} The Second release was "Hope Springs Nocturnal".{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix - Hope Springs Nocturnal |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/hope-springs-nocturnal-mw0000009201|work=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=23 December 2015}} Officially released in October 1998; incorrectly listed as 2000 on [[AllMusic]]. It is a longer release of 18 tracks. She went on to release albums back to back that have gained popularity. The Third release was titled "3" and it has 10 tracks. It was released on Nov 1, 2003{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix Discography - 3 |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/3-mw0000322491|work=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=Musician: Jenn Vix' Third Album, "3" |url=http://thenoise-boston.com/2009/04/jenn-vix-feature291/|work=Music New England|accessdate=23 December 2015}} to rave reviews - "Vix’s voice is a wonderful instrument on its own; warm and embracing." In 2010, Jenn Vix released a single track titled "Vampires",{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix's Vampires |url=http://sonichits.com/artist/Jenn_Vix|work=Sonic Hits|accessdate=23 December 2015}} and it was exclusively made available at [[CDBaby]]. It's now available at all retail and streaming sites. On January 24, 2012, the fourth release of a single track, titled "In the House of Dark Shadows", was released. It is a collaboration track with [[Reeves Gabrels]], former guitarist for [[David Bowie]] and [[Tin Machine]], and current [[guitarist]] of [[The Cure]].{{cite web|title=Jenn recorded 'In The House of Dark Shadows' with guitarist Reeves Gabrels; of David Bowie, Tin Machine, and The Cure|url=http://groovesharks.org/artist/Jenn-Vix|work=Groovesharks.org|accessdate=23 December 2015}} In 2012, Vix released a single track titled “Sick” on March 14,{{cite web|title=Muzoic Album release Sick - Single by Jenn VIX|url=http://muzoic.net/release/album/jenn-vix-sick-single|work=Muzoic.net/|accessdate= 23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=Sick |url=http://cocatalog.loc.gov/cgi-bin/Pwebrecon.cgi?v1=7&ti=1,7&Search_Arg=vix%20jenn&Search_Code=NALL&CNT=25&PID=oYxbMOOvShh2Z6G-HuM-KVnCvZ6&SEQ=20151223070019&SID=1 |work=US Copyright Catalog|accessdate= 23 December 2015}} and then a year later she released a sixth single titled "Speed Of Light", it was the second collaboration with [[Reeves Gabrels]]. The release date was April 22, 2013.{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix and Reeves Gabrels "Speed of Light"|url=http://blog.horizonvumusic.com/?p=9814|work=Horizon VU Music|accessdate=23 December 2015}} She then released a couple of single tracks by collaborating with other renowned artists. Next came her seventh release, a single track collaboration with [[Andy Anderson (drummer)|Andy Anderson]], former drummer of [[The Cure]], [[Iggy Pop]] and [[Hawkwind]], and [[guitarist]] Mark Montalto. The track title is "Eyes Roll Back." The official release date was June 29, 2015. This was originally pre-released on [[Bandcamp]] on March 13, 2015.{{cite web|title=Review: Jenn Vix And Andy Anderson – 'Eyes Roll Back'|url=http://www.intravenousmag.co.uk/2015/06/review-jenn-vix-and-andy-anderson-eyes.html|work=INTRAVENOUS MAGAZINE|accessdate=23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=SINGLE REVIEW: Jenn Vix, "Eyes Roll Back"|url=http://popdose.com/single-review-jenn-vix-eyes-roll-back/|work=Popdose.com|accessdate=23 December 2015}} It blends post-punk guitars and drums with light and sensual synthesizers. Her eighth release was a double single, titled "F*ck, Rinse, Repeat" and "Burn."{{cite web|title=Review: Jenn Vix & Dirk Ivens - 'F*ck.Rinse.Repeat' / 'Burn' (Single)|url=http://www.intravenousmag.co.uk/2013/06/review-jenn-vix-dirk-ivens.html|work=INTRAVENOUS MAGAZINE|accessdate=23 December 2015}} Both tracks are a collaboration with [[Dirk Ivens]], Belgian [[electro-industrial]] pioneer from the bands [[Absolute Body Control]], Dive, and [[The Klinik]]. Vix‘s ninth release was a single track titled "I Don't Trust You". This is yet another collaboration with the former drummer of [[The Cure]], [[Andy Anderson (drummer)|Andy Anderson]].{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix Teams with The Cure's Andy Anderson on New Single 'I Don't Trust You', Out 7/14|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Jenn-Vix-Teams-with-The-Cures-Andy-Anderson-on-New-Single-I-Dont-Trust-You-Out-714-20150630|work=Broadway World|accessdate=23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=Review: Jenn Vix And Andy Anderson – 'I Don't Trust You'|url=http://www.intravenousmag.co.uk/2015/06/review-jenn-vix-and-andy-anderson-eyes.html|work=INTRAVENOUS MAGAZINE|accessdate=23 December 2015}} (Originally pre-released on [[Bandcamp]] on June 17, 2015, then officially released on June 29, 2015. "I Don't Trust You" opts for a dark synthpop meets [[Trip hop|trip-hop]] approach "Which frame Vix's [[Annie Lennox]] style vocals very nicely. It is a much more complex, intriguing and engaging track that more appropriately shows off the depth of Vix's songwriting skills." The Tenth release was a single track, titled "Hands On My Heart (Waiting)." It was released on August 29, 2015, and it is available exclusively on [[Bandcamp]]. Her eleventh release was a 5 track EP titled "Strange Buildings."{{cite web|title=E.P. REVIEW: JENN VIX (Featuring John Ashton), "Strange Buildings"|url=http://popdose.com/e-p-review-jenn-vix-featuring-john-ashton-strange-buildings/|work=Popdose.com|accessdate=23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix - "Strange Buildings"|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/jenn-vix/|work=[[MTV]]|accessdate=23 December 2015}} It features two collaboration tracks, "The Woman with No Fear" and "Weirdo", with the original guitarist of [[The Psychedelic Furs]], [[John Ashton (musician)|John Ashton]]. It was released on [[Bandcamp]] on November 16, 2015, and then officially released on November 17, 2015.{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix and John Ashton - Woman With No Fear|url=http://www.teamrock.com/features/2015-12-06/classic-rock-s-tracks-of-the-week-53|work=TeamRock|accessdate=23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix and John Ashton – ‘The Woman With No Fear’|url=http://www.gigslutz.co.uk/video-premiere-jenn-vix-and-john-ashton-the-woman-with-no-fear/|work=Gigslutz|accessdate=23 December 2015}}{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix - The Woman With No Fear (Feat. John Ashton)’|url=http://thesoundofconfusionblog.blogspot.in/2015/12/jen-vix-woman-with-no-fear-feat-john.html|work=TheSoundOfConfusion|accessdate=23 December 2015}} There are also several unofficially released single tracks on [[YouTube]], [[Myspace]], [[Reverb Nation]] and [[Soundcloud]], including "Wave Of Sound", "In Your Sleep", "The Poisoner", "There", "Rover", "Pretty Knives", "Snake", Electrocution Man" and "Show Me The Sun" featuring former [[Adam and the Ants]] and [[Bow Wow Wow]] drummer [[Dave Barbarossa]], aka Dave Barbe. There is also a cover version (tribute) of "Mouth To Mouth," by [[The Glove]]. Vix has also contributed vocals and music tracks for Bob Robertson's singing skeleton group, Sindy Skinless and the [[Decomposer (album)|Decomposers]], featured on [[YouTube]]. In January 2016, Jenn Vix performed the tracks "Strange Buildings", "Unlocked" and "The Woman With No Fear" live for [[WBRU]]'s Home BRU'd, accompanied by Paul LF and [[John Ashton (musician)|John Ashton]]. {{cite web|author=*Please enter your name. |url=http://www.wbru.com/home-brud-session-jenn-vix/ |title=Home BRU’d Session: Jenn Vix | 95.5 WBRU |website=Wbru.com |date=2016-01-25 |accessdate=2016-06-27}} ==Awards and recognition== Vix's debut release was reviewed in the [[Kurt Cobain]] memorial issue of [[Rolling Stone Magazine]], #683, on June 2, 1994, prior to its official release. She has been a member of [[Broadcast Music Inc.]] since 1994. Her track "Devils Chasing Angels" from her 1995 debut album was featured on a [[CMJ]] New Music Monthly CD compilation in February 1995,{{cite web|title=Jenn Vix - CMJ New Music Monthly|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=IS0EAAAAMBAJ&source=gbs_all_issues_r&cad=1|work=[[CMJ]]|accessdate= 23 December 2015}} VOL. 18, alongside tracks by [[Throwing Muses]], [[The Stone Roses]] and more. There was a full page ad in the magazine and a short review of her track. She was on the cover of [[Boston]], MA, Noise magazine, and was the feature story; feature 291, April 22, 2009. The video for "Vampires" was animated by cartoonist and writer, [[Scott Bateman]]. In September 2014, Vix collaborated on a single track with [[guitarist]] [[Marco Pirroni]], formerly of [[Adam and the Ants]], [[Rema-Rema|Rema Rema]] and [[The Models]]. The track is a cover version of [[Shirley Collins]]' "Turpin Hero" and it is featured on the digital download of the compilation album "Shirley Inspired", official UK releases date 8 June, 2015. She has also contributed 3 royalty free tracks to the [[PBS]] ([[New England]]) series "Hit and Run History", featuring Andrew Giles Buckley. The series is on the Boston PBS Channel 2 (WGBH) official website, and it has appeared on [[Rhode Island]] [[PBS]] channel 36.
