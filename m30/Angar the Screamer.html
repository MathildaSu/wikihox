Sonokinesis |}} '''Angar the Screamer''' ('''David Alan Angar''', also known as '''Scream''') is a [[fictional character]], a [[Marvel Comics]] supervillain. == Publication history == Angar first appeared in ''[[Daredevil (Marvel Comics)|Daredevil]]'' v1, #100 (June 1973), and was created by [[Steve Gerber]], [[Gene Colan]], and [[John Tartaglione]]. Angar also appeared in issues #101 (July 1973), and #105-107 (November 1973-January 1974) of ''Daredevil''. The character subsequently appeared in ''[[Marvel Premiere]]'' #25 (October 1975), ''[[Iron Fist (comics)|Iron Fist]]'' v1, #1-2 (February-March 1976), 5-7 (June-September 1976), ''[[Spider-Woman (Jessica Drew)|Spider-Woman]]'' v1, #34-35 (January-February 1981), 50 (June 1983), ''[[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] Spotlight'' #26 (December 1989), 28-29 (January-February 1990), ''Marvel Comics Presents'' #97 (1992), ''[[Nick Fury]]: Agent of SHIELD'' v2, #33-35 (March-May 1992), ''[[Captain America]]'' v1, #411-414 (January-April 1993), and ''[[Avengers Unplugged]]'' #4 (April 1996). Angar was killed in ''[[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]] Annual '97''. Angar returned a few years later, as an energy being named '''Scream''', and appeared in ''Thunderbolts'' #49-56 (April-November 2001), and 58-59 (January-February 2002) Angar the Screamer received an entry in the ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]'' #1. ==Fictional character biography== David Angar was born in [[San Francisco, California]]. He became a [[hippie]] and a radical social activist, who volunteered for an experiment that would give him superhuman powers. [[Moondragon]] provided a crooked lawyer named Kerwin J. Broderick with a machine built on [[Titan (Marvel Comics)|Titan]]. The machine subjected Angar's vocal cords to a bombardment with ''hypersound''. As a result, Angar could scream very loudly and cause people to hallucinate. Moondragon intended for Angar to be an ally against the mad Titan [[Thanos]], but Broderick hired Angar as an assassin. In his first appearance, Angar tried to kill [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics)|Daredevil]] and [[Black Widow (Natalia Romanova)|Black Widow]]. Angar was defeated and would fight many Marvel superheroes over the years. He tended to attack crowds of civilians for little or no reason.Daredevil Vol.1 #100 He later entered a relationship with the similarly powered [[Songbird (comics)|Screaming Mimi]].Avengers Spotlight #28 In a robbery, he was shot, and as he died in her arms, she screamed for over an hour until her larynx was destroyed.Thunderbolts Annual 1997 She was then found by [[Baron Zemo|Baron Helmut Zemo]], who had [[Fixer (comics)|Fixer]] rebuild her voicebox and give her new powers, after which she joined the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]] as Songbird. The Fixer also took Angar's body and experimented upon his larynx.Thunderbolts Vol.1 #59 The Fixer's experiments resurrected Angar himself as the abstract sound being '''Scream''',Thunderbolts Vol.1 #54 who became part of the [[Redeemers (comics)|Redeemers]].Thunderbolts Vol.1 #49 Scream showed no emotions or intelligence and only obeyed orders. When the Redeemers fought [[Graviton (comics)|Graviton]], most of the team was killed and Scream was dispersed. He managed to restore himself through Songbird's energy and went on a rampage, until he had Songbird disperse him for good. Songbird and the Redeemers were unaware of Scream's true identity as Angar the Screamer. Songbird did notice something familiar about Scream, but only realized his true identity when Scream restored himself and took Angar's appearance again. Thunderbolts Vol.1 #58 == Powers and abilities == As Angar the Screamer, Angar could scream at a superhuman level, deafening and even causing permanent hearing damage to those around him. He could also induce hallucinations in humans with his voice. These hallucinations usually were disturbing and violent in nature. Angar was immune to his own powers, and could make his victims lose all memory of his attacks.''Spider-Woman'' #35 As Scream, Angar was a being made out of "solid sound", allowing him to exist without food or sleep. He didn't need to breathe, but he needed air as a [[transmission medium]] to exist. He was immune to physical harm and could even reintegrate himself when dispersed. Scream could fly and manipulate sound around him. He could disorient opponents or use sound as a concussive weapon. Despite his ability to manipulate sound, Scream could not talk and seemed to be a mindless creature for most of his existence. ==In other media== ===Television=== * David A.
