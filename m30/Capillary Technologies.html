Established in 2008, Capillary is present in [[India]], [[Singapore]], [[Malaysia]], [[Greater China]], [[United Kingdom]], [[South Africa]], [[United States of America]], [[Dubai]], [[Australia]] and [[New Zealand]] . ==History== Capillary was started by Krishna Mehra, Aneesh Reddy and Ajay Modani in 2008. The three co-founders are alumni of [[Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur|Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur]] (IIT-KGP). Before starting Capillary, Aneesh has worked at [[ITC Limited]]; Krishna with [[Microsoft Research]] and Ajay with [[Danieli]]. Incubated in IIT Kharagpur, Capillary began its operations in Kolkata and moved to Bangalore in May, 2009. Capillary got its first client, Indus League brand John Miller in April, 2009. In September 2009, Capillary received a funding of USD100,000 as the winners of QPrize[http://www.siliconindia.com/shownews/Capillary_Technologies_secures_100000_in_seed_funding-nid-62005-cid-100.html "Capillary Technologies secures $100,000 in seed funding"] Silicon India 2009, a global business plan contest conducted by [[Qualcomm]]. By September 2010, Capillary’s [[solution]]s were live in 2000 stores across India. From 10 employees in 2009 and 30 employees in 2010, Capillary has grown to a 220+ strong team (as of July 2013).[http://www.rediff.com/getahead/slide-show/slide-show-1-achievers-interview-with-capillary-tech-founders/20111104.htm "The amazing success story of an Indian garage start-up"] Rediff News The company has more than 140 major brands worldwide, reaching over 70 million consumers across 10000+ stores. ==Funding== Capillary started with a funding of 1,00,000 [[Indian rupee]]s by the co-founders themselves.
