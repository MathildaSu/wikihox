xml:space="preserve" bytes="11567"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Ariel Hyatt|timestamp=20140312010835|year=2014|month=March|day=12|substed=yes|help=off}} {{notability|date=March 2014}} {{advert|date=January 2014}} {{Infobox person | name = Ariel Hyatt | image = ArielSeatedByArielHyatt.jpeg | alt = | caption = | birth_date = | birth_place = [[New York City]] | organization = Ariel Publicity | education = [[Clark University]] (BA 1993) | known_for = | occupation = Marketing and [[social media]] specialist | website = }} '''Ariel Hyatt''' is an American music publicist, author, and social media strategist.{{cite news |url=http://www.wired.com/underwire/2009/03/sxsw-bands-spli/ |title=SXSW: Social Networking Rocks — But Only for Some Bands |last=Van Buskirk |first=Eliot |date=March 21, 2009 |publisher=''[[Wired Magazine]]'' |accessdate=2010-07-03}} She is the president and founder of Ariel Publicity, a social media PR firm based in [[New York City]], and Cyber PR, her online brand. She authored the book ''Music Success in 9 Weeks''{{cite news |url=http://www.knowthemusicbiz.com/index.php/BIZ-BLOG/BIZ-BLOG/Review-of-Ariel-Hyatt-s-Music-Success-in-Nine-Weeks-by-Carla-Lynne-Hall.html |title=Review of Ariel Hyatt's Music Success in Nine Weeks by Carla Lynne Hall |last=Hall |first=Carla Lynne |date=December 15, 2009 |publisher=KnowtheMusicBiz.com |accessdate=2010-07-03}} and co-wrote the book ''Musician's Roadmap to Facebook and Twitter.'' ==Early life== Hyatt grew up in New York City and graduated from the [[Dwight School]]. In 1989 she attended [[Clark University]] and graduated with a degree in theater in 1993.{{cite web |url=http://www.berkshiresweek.com/032003/default.asp?filename=ArielHyattfeature&adfile=ads9 |title=Cool: She makes a living promoting her favorite bands |author=Rogovoy, Seth |date=30 March 2003 |publisher=Berkshires Month |accessdate=10 April 2012}} ==Career== Hyatt started her career as a traditional publicist working for the fashion industry, but later transitioned into music publicity.{{cite news |url=http://miccontrol.com/#/micschool/branded-music-ariel-hyatt/ |title=Branded Music - Part 5 of 5 - Ariel Hyatt |last= |first= |date=September 17, 2010 |publisher=www.miccontrol.com |accessdate=2010-07-03}} In 1994 she began working as an assistant in [[Boulder, CO]] at What Are Records?, a small indie record label representing bands including [[The Samples]]. In 1995 she became PR Director at Small Axe Concert Promotions, and in March 1996 she became Publicity Director at [[Fox Theatre (Boulder, Colorado)]], where she stayed until 2000. She also worked at publicity firm KSA. Her musical clients have included [[Lee Scratch Perry]], [[Kelly Richey]], [[George Clinton (musician)|George Clinton]], [[The Nitty Gritty Dirt Band]], [[Derek Sivers]] of [[CD Baby]], and Project Logic, which featured [[John Popper]] of [[Blues Traveler]] and [[DJ Logic]].{{cite news |url=http://musiciancoaching.com/tag/ariel-hyatt/ |title=Getting Press and Blog Attention with Ariel Hyatt |last=Coaching |first=Musician |date=December 17, 2009 |publisher=Musician Coaching.com |accessdate=2010-07-03}}{{cite news |url=http://popwreckoning.com/2010/02/12/interview-with-ariel-hyatt-of-ariel-publicity/ |title=Interview with Ariel Hyatt of Ariel Publicity |last=Starks |first=Thomas |date=February 12, 2010 |publisher=popwreckoning.com |accessdate=2010-07-03}} Hyatt currently writes for Music Think Tank, an online music industry magazine and blog.{{cite web |url=http://www.musicthinktank.com/blog/ariel-hyatt-7-questions-for-a-real-live-music-supervisor-sar.html |title=7 Questions For A Real Live Music Supervisor Sarah Gavigan of Get Your Music Licensed |last=Hyatt |first=Ariel |date=March 28, 2012 |publisher=Music Think Tank |accessdate=April 1, 2012}} Hyatt serves on the board of the [[Sweet Relief Musicians Fund]], a non-profit organization that provides monetary aid to ill professional musicians. She also serves on advisory boards for [[South by Southwest|SXSW]] Accelerator,{{cite web |url=http://sxsw.com/music/accelerator/advisory_board |title=Advisory Board |publisher=[[South by Southwest]] |accessdate=April 1, 2012}} SoundCtrl,{{cite web |url=http://www.soundctrl.com/blog/2010/04/07/early-bird-tickets-for-new-music-seminar-nyc/ |title=Early Bird Tickets for New Music Seminar NYC |date=April 7, 2010 |publisher=SoundCtrl |accessdate=April 1, 2012}} and the [[New Music Seminar]].{{cite web |url=http://playitloudmusic.wordpress.com/interviews/social-media/interview-with-ariel-hyatt-founder-of-ariel-publicity-a-social-media-pr-firm-and-author-of-“music-success-in-nine-weeks”/ |title=Interview with Ariel Hyatt, founder of Ariel Publicity (a social media PR firm) and author of "Music Success in Nine Weeks" |last=Bethune |first=Aaron |publisher=Play it Loud Music |accessdate=April 1, 2012}} ==Ariel Publicity== In 1996 Hyatt asked Summer Trembley to create a logo for her new company, and founded Ariel Publicity in [[Boulder, Colorado]]. The company specializes in independent musicians,{{cite news |url=http://www.ontherecordpodcast.com/pr/otro/podcast-post.aspx?id=2261 |title=How to Achieve Social Media Rock Star Status with Ariel Hyatt |last=Schwartzman |first=Eric |date=November 16, 2009 |publisher=On The Record Online |accessdate=2010-07-03}} and has represented over 1,700 artists.{{cite news |url=http://rockstarlifelessons.com/340/interview-with-ariel-hyatt-indie-music-publicist-and-author-of-music-success-in-nine-weeks/ |title=Interview with Ariel Hyatt, Indie Music Publicist and Author of Music Success in Nine Weeks |last=Lynne Hall |first=Carla |date=July 2009 |publisher=Rock Star Life Lessons |accessdate=2010-07-03}} In 2000 she published her first article, "How to Be Your Own Publicist".{{cite web |url=http://schedule.sxsw.com/2012/events/event_MP11864 |title=Beethoven + Social Media = Crowdfunding Patronage |publisher=[[South by Southwest]] |accessdate=2012-04-01}} ==CyberPR== In 2007 she met Corey Denis, former Director of Online Marketing at What Are Records and Director of Digital Marketing at IODA, who introduced her to [[Twitter]] and other social media platforms. In January 2007 Hyatt launched CyberPR, an online business venture that exists as a separate brand from Hyatt Publicity.{{cite news |url=http://www.bluegrassjournal.com/2010/05/12/ariel-publicity-founders-book-music-success-in-nine-weeks-tips-tools-on-music-marketing-in-digital-age/ |title=Ariel Publicity founder’s book "Music Success In Nine Weeks" Tips & Tools on music marketing in digital age |last=Nile Media |first=Lotos |date=May 12, 2010 |publisher=BluegrassJournal.com |accessdate=2010-07-03}} CyberPR not only represents music industry clients, but also books and authors. For Cyber PR, Ariel developed software that focuses on getting clients reviewed in blogs, websites and in podcasts. Ariel Publicity and Cyber PR have an official YouTube channel called Sound Advice, on which interviews, discussions, and training videos are regularly uploaded, almost all of which feature Hyatt herself. At its most popular, this channel had over 20,000 subscribers.{{cite web |url=http://www.ourstage.com/blog/2010/3/9/qa-with-ariel-hyatt |title=Q&A with Ariel Hyatt |date=March 9, 2010 |publisher=[[Ourstage]] |accessdate=April 1, 2012}} Starting in 2011, [[Middle Tennessee State University]] has offered an official master class based on the publicity principles developed by Ariel Hyatt and Cyber PR, including hands-on work with PR campaigns. Due to the popularity of this class, an advanced section has since been added.{{cite web |url=http://news-from-mtsu.blogspot.com/2010/09/115-musicians-sept-28-master-class.html |title=Musicians' Sept. 28 'Master Class' Targets Social Media As Moneymaker |date=1 April 2012 |publisher=[[Middle Tennessee State University]] |accessdate=10 April 2012}}{{cite web |url=http://www.murfreesboropost.com/students-get-hands-on-training-cms-30679 |title=Students get hands-on training |date=1 April 2012 |publisher=Murfreesboro Post |accessdate=10 April 2012}} ==Published works== In 2008, Hyatt published her first book, ''Music Success in Nine Weeks'', designed to help musicians use social media and other internet based technologies to expand their fan base. The book was critically successful; a review in ''[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]]'' magazine stated that Hyatt "provides compelling advice on how to connect with those who can help advance your career, [garner] you more fans, gigs, rehearsal space, private shows, interns, and, eventually, money."{{cite web |url=http://www.nodepression.com/profiles/blogs/book-review-ariel-hyatt-music |title=Book Review: Ariel Hyatt - Music Success in Nine Weeks (Ariel Publicity, 2009) |date=September 25, 2010 |publisher=[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]] |accessdate=April 1, 2012}} The book is currently in its third edition. In 2011, Hyatt co-wrote and published her second book, ''Musician's Roadmap to Facebook and Twitter'' with Carla Lynne Hall. This release focuses entirely on popular social media services [[Facebook]] and [[Twitter]], and is meant to help musicians optimize their use of these services to further their careers. ==Public appearances== Some of the events Hyatt has spoken at include [[South by Southwest]], the [[CMJ Music Marathon|CMJ]] SoundCtrl, ASCAP’s I Create Music, The Future of Music, [[Canadian Music Week]], APRA’s Song Summit in [[Sydney]], You Are In Control ([[Reykjavík]]), The ECMAs, [[NARAS]], and The Taxi Road Rally.{{cite news |url=http://www.abn.org.au/site/article/featured-podcast-music-industrys-pr-maven-ariel-hyatt |title=Featured Podcast: Music Industry's PR Maven, Ariel Hyatt |last= |first= |date= |publisher=Australian Businesswomen's Network |accessdate=2010-07-03}} == References == {{reflist}} ==External links== * {{official website|http://arielpublicity.com/}} *[http://vimeo.com/14472609 Video: Ariel Hyatt Interview at Bigsound] (2009) *[http://www.accidenthash.com/2009/10/31/music-success-in-nine-weeks/ Video: Interview by C.C.
