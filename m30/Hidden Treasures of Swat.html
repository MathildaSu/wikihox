The book is limited to the Swat Valley's archaeological sites, some of these sites are more than 3000 years old and having great histories behind them, Site [[Barikot]] [[Ancient]] [[Bazira]] was Captured by [[Alexander the Great]] in spring of 326 BCE. [[Barikot]] is the present day name of the ancient "[[Bazira]]", which was besieged by [[Alexander the Great]]. Other sites are related various [[Dynasties]] such as [[Grecian]], Parthian, [[Kushan]], [[Sassanid]], and [[Mauryan]]. The book contains brief histories concern to these dynasties. Buddhism and [[Battagram District#Hindu Shahi dynasty|Hindu Shahis]] have occupied many pages in the book owing to a deep relation between Swat Valley and [[Buddha]].
