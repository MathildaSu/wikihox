xml:space="preserve" bytes="1754"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=De L'Assomption Boulevard|timestamp=20161016020952|year=2016|month=October|day=16|substed=yes|help=off}} {{unreferenced|date=October 2016}} '''De L'Assomption Boulevard''' (French: ''Boulevard de l'Assomption'') is a main north-south street in the [[Montreal]] boroughs of [[Mercier–Hochelaga-Maisonneuve]], [[Rosemont–La Petite-Patrie]] and [[Saint-Léonard—Saint-Michel|Saint-Léonard]]. There is also the [[Assomption (Montreal Metro)|Assomption metro station]] south of [[Sherbrooke Street]] East. {| class="wikitable" ! colspan="2" |De L'Assomption Boulevard |- |Direction |North-south |- |Starts at |Hochelaga Street |- |Ends at |Jean-Talon Street |- |Length |3.6 km |- |Designated |December 3, 1951 |- |Formerly names |Rue Poulin, 45e Avenue |} == Description == The boulevard is 3.6 kilometres long and starts at its intersection with [[Hochelaga Street]] and ends at its intersection with [[Jean Talon Street|Jean-Talon Street]]. The metro station is situated at the corner with Chauveau Street. [[File:Boulevard de l Assomption.jpg|alt=An STM bus waits outside the Assomption metro station|thumb|An STM bus waits outside the Assomption metro station]] The boulevard got its name in 1951 in honor of the proclamation of the [[Assumption of Mary|Dogma of the Assumption of Mother Mary]] a year earlier by [[Pope Pius XII]]. {{Streets in Montreal}} [[Category:Streets in Montreal]]
