xml:space="preserve" bytes="2725"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=GRADES|timestamp=20150115112641|year=2015|month=January|day=15|substed=yes}} {{Infobox musical artist | name = GRADES | background = DJ_producer | origin = [[Croyden]], England | years_active = {{start date|2013}}-present | label = {{Unbulleted list|One More Tune Records, [[Warner Music Group]]}} | website = {{URL|https://soundcloud.com/gradesofficial}} }} '''Grades''' is an [electronic dance music] producer and DJ from London. Signed to the [[Warner Music Group]] imprint One More Tune, his music encompasses elements of [[2-step garage|2-step]] and [[House music|house]]. ==Career== GRADES’ first original track "Freedom" was released in April 2014 and was featured by [[Annie Mac]] in her Free Music Mondays download series. He followed the release of “Freedom” with a remix of [[Lana Del Rey|Lana Del Rey’s]] “[[West Coast (song)|West Coast]]” ) which reached number 3 at Hype Machine . Grades’ second single “Owe It To Yourself” was premiered in June by [[Mixmag]] who compared him to artists such as [[Bondax]] and [[Kaytranada]] Later in the year, Grades joined [[Blonde (musical group)|Blonde]] on the Eton Messy tour which included at London date at Birthdays , and also remixed [[Bastille (band)|Bastille’s]] ) “Torn Apart” which featured on the “Vs. (Other People’s Heartache pt. III)” mixtape. His third single “Crocodile Tears” was premiered by [[VICE|Vice’s]] music channel Noisey.
