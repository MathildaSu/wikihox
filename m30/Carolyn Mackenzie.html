She graduated from [[Carleton University]] with a Bachelor of Journalism.{{cite web | last = Cornblum | first = Marcy | title = Celebrity Spaces: Carolyn MacKenzie | publisher = Toronto Sun | date = July 21, 2014 | url = http://www.torontosun.com/2014/07/21/celebrity-spaces-carolyn-mackenzie}} ==Career== MacKenzie began her career as host of [[Rogers TV]] ''Toronto Living''. She then moved to [[Sydney, Nova Scotia]] where she worked as a videographer, reporter, camera operator and editor for ATV/CTV News. MacKenzie moved to [[Barrie]], Ontario and worked as an anchor for a new television station, New VR. In 2005, MacKenzie moved back to Toronto where she joined [[Global Television Network]]. The following year, she won an [[Edward R. Murrow Award (Radio Television Digital News Association)|Edward R. Murrow Award]] for excellence in journalism for her coverage of transit inaccessibility. MacKenzie has covered Queen Elizabeth's Jubilee Visit, reported on Toronto terror suspects, and achieved the "Best Spot News Story" for reporting on a home attack in [[Vaughan]], Ontario.{{cn|date=February 2015}} In 2013, MacKenzie switched from anchoring Global's weekend news, to anchoring its weekday 11:00 pm news.{{cite web | last = Armstrong | first = Denis | title = Global Anchor Says J-School Program "Bang On" | publisher = Carleton University | date = May 2014 | url = http://carletonnow.carleton.ca/may-2014/global-anchor-says-j-school-program-bang-on/}} On June 1, 2015, Mackenzie moved to Glabal Television's [[The Morning Show (Canadian TV series)|The Morning show]].{{cite web | title = Global News Toronto Announces New Anchor Line-up | publisher = Broadcaster | date = May 14, 2015 | url = http://www.broadcastermagazine.com/news/global-news-toronto-announces-new-anchor-line-up/1003625439/}} ==Personal life== MacKenzie and her firefighter husband Chris have two children. They live in Etobicoke, a suburb of Toronto.
