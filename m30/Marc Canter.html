xml:space="preserve" bytes="8926"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Marc Canter|timestamp=20150704085652|year=2015|month=July|day=4|substed=yes}} {{BLP sources|date=October 2014}} {{original research|date=October 2014}} [[File:Me-closeup.jpg|thumb|180px|Marc Canter]] '''Marc Canter''' is CEO of Broadband Mechanics, which produces People Aggregator, a social networking tool with source available (but not under an open source license).[http://blog.broadbandmechanics.com/2008/04/keeping-up-with-the-blogjones-mid-april-08 Marc Canter explains that People Aggregator source is available, but not under an open source license] Previously, he was a founder of the company that became [[Macromedia]]. His [[blog]], Marc's Voice, frequently critiques other Internet luminaries and competitors, such as [[Mark Zuckerberg]].[http://blog.broadbandmechanics.com/2007/09/open-letter-to-mark-zukerberg Marc’s Voice » Blog Archive » Open letter to Mark Zuckerberg] Known for his boisterous appearances at technology conferences, Canter makes no secret of his left-leaning politics, identifying himself as a "[[red diaper baby]]" at the 2005 [[Gnomedex]] meeting in Seattle.[http://www.flickr.com/photos/factoryjoe/178618619/ Red Diaper Canter sur Flickr : partage de photos !][http://blog.broadbandmechanics.com/2006/07/honesty-courage-and-american-politics Marc’s Voice » Blog Archive » Honesty, courage and American politics] A son of community leader Miriam Canter -- eponym of Chicago's Canter School -- and well-known election and civil rights attorney David Canter, he was raised in the liberal but exclusive Hyde Park-Kenwood neighborhood near the University of Chicago. Canter is also a contributor to many [[open standards]] efforts and advocates for end-user controlled digital identities and content - being a co-founder of the "Identity Gang",http://www.identitygang.org/ Identity Gang and a co-signer of the Social Web Users' Bill of Rights.{{cite web|url=http://opensocialweb.org/2007/09/05/bill-of-rights/|publisher=Opensocial.org|title=A Bill of Rights for Users of the Social Web|date=September 5, 2007}} He has consulted with global corporations including [[PCCW]] and [[Intel]] and has written on the [[multimedia]] industry, [[Micropublishing|micro-content publishing]] and [[social networking]].{{cite web | title=MicroContent Musings | url=http://www.sivas.com/microcontent/musings/ | accessdate=2007-01-05 }} ==Business activities== Canter is the founder and CEO of Broadband Mechanics a digital lifestyle aggregator (DLA) company.{{cite web | title=Welcome to Broadband Mechanics | url=http://broadbandmechanics.com/index.html | accessdate=2007-01-05 }} Broadband Mechanics builds tools and environments to enable [[online communities]]. It targets products at a broad user base with the hope that everyday people can make better use of Internet technologies. Broadband Mechanics provides strategic consulting and architectural design work to its clients, such as [[1UP.com]], [[Tribe.net]], [[aSmallWorld]], and [http://www.alwayson.goingon.com/ AlwaysOn Network]. Canter has been involved in various [[Startup company|startup]]s in the formulative stages of their product development and design. He was an early participant in Tribe.net and helped develop its "tribes" system, the first social network 'groups' technology.{{cite news | author = Andrew Leonard | title = You are who you know | url = http://dir.salon.com/story/tech/feature/2004/06/15/social_software_one/index.html?pn=1 | work = Salon | publisher = Salon.com | id = | pages = | page = 2 | date = 2004-06-15 | accessdate = 2007-01-05 | quote = A posting on Tribe.net had led me here. I found out about the event after joining a "tribe" called "social software intellectuals" -- originally created by Marc Canter }} He consulted for [[Ruckus Network]] and [http://jahshaka.org Visual Media]. Broadband Mechanics has also collaborated with [[Avid Technology]].{{cite web | title=Broadband Mechanics: Our Clients | url=http://broadbandmechanics.com/clients.html | accessdate=2007-01-05 }} Broadband Mechanics in 2006 released StructuredBlogging,[http://structuredblogging.org StructuredBlogging] an attempted standard for microcontent. It has released [[Perl]] and [[PHP]] libraries and plug-ins for [[WordPress]] and [[Movable Type]]. StructuredBlogging has established [[Database schema|schemas]] for events, reviews, lists, media (audio and video), people and group showcases. ==History in the technology industry== Canter co-founded [[MacroMind]] in 1984 and began developing for the newly launched [[Apple Macintosh]].{{cite web | title = Macromedia - The Story | url=http://www.adobe.com/macromedia/story/ | accessdate = 2007-01-05 }}{{cite news | title = Online repository | work = San Diego Union-Tribune (online version) | pages = 1 | publisher = Knight-Ridder News service | date = 2005-04-04 | url = http://www.signonsandiego.com/news/computing/personaltech/20050404-9999-mz1b4site.html | accessdate = 2007-01-05 }} MacroMind became [[Macromedia]] in 1991. He was part of the team that created the first multimedia player, the first cross-platform authoring system and the world's leading multimedia platform. Marc was considered one of the founders of multimedia,{{cite book | last = Packer | first = Randall | title = Multimedia: From Wagner to Virtual Reality, Expanded Edition | publisher = [[W. W. Norton & Company|Norton]] | year = 2001 | isbn = 0-393-32375-7 | quote = Marc Canter, who developed the first commercial multimedia authoring system, was a chief catalyst...}} and has been a speaker, developer and evangelist in the industry for over a decade. Prior to MacroMind, he worked for [[Bally-Midway]] programming music for videogames. He claims to have programmed one of the earliest licensed music ("Peter Gunn" for Spyhunter).{{cite web | title=LinkedIn: Marc Canter | url=http://www.linkedin.com/in/marccanter | accessdate=2007-01-05 }}{{cite web | url = http://joi.ito.com/archives/2002/09/20/product_placement_on_the_sims.html | title = Product Placement on the Sims! | accessdate = 2007-01-05 | author = Joi Ito | last = | first = | coauthors = | date = 2002-09-20 | year = | month = | work = Joi Ito's Web | publisher = | pages = | language = | archiveurl = | archivedate = | quote = }} He started his career after college when he went to New York City to help his friends build a music studio called "Noise New York." During this time Canter learned about laserdiscs, laser light shows, [[NAPLPS]], pro audio and video equipment, and a then-new technology called [[videodisc]]s. Canter was also involved in one of the first known cases of a [[computer virus|virus]] being distributed via commercial software.{{cite newsgroup|title=RISKS DIGEST 6.46|author=Peter G. Nuemann|date=1988-03-18|newsgroup=comp.risks|message-id=|url=http://groups.google.com/group/comp.risks/browse_thread/thread/3340b564f2b9da7a/14e449aaa88cfe20|accessdate = 2007-01-05 }} According to the March 16, 1988 edition of the ''[[Toronto Star]]'', several MacroMind products shipped with virus infected media. Analysis later revealed that Canter's computer was infected with the virus while he was working on training material for the software products. Canter and [[J.D.
