xml:space="preserve" bytes="2274"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Bill Clinton haircut controversy|timestamp=20150203152912|year=2015|month=February|day=3|substed=yes}} {{merge to|Presidency of Bill Clinton|discuss=Talk:Presidency of Bill Clinton#Proposed merge with Bill Clinton haircut controversy|date=February 2015}} The '''Bill Clinton haircut controversy''', usually known as "'''Hairgate'''", was a 1993 controversy surrounding a [[haircut]] given to U.S. President [[Bill Clinton]] on board [[Air Force One]].{{cite news |last=Friedman|first=Thomnas|date=21 May 1993|title=Haircut Grounded Clinton While the Price Took Off|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1993/05/21/us/haircut-grounded-clinton-while-the-price-took-off.html|newspaper=[[New York Times]]|access-date=03 Feb 2015}} On 18 May 1993, a [[Belgian American]] hairstylist [[Cristophe (hairstylist)|Cristophe]] gave Clinton the $200 haircut while the plane was sitting on the tarmac at [[Los Angeles Airport]]. Two runways were shut down for an hour, but the media reported much more significant delays.Katy Steinmetz, "[http://content.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,1977252_1977334_1977256,00.html Hairgate]," ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]. [[Nigel Hamilton (author)|Nigel Hamilton]] notes that every newspaper in America seized on the story, and that it was quickly dubbed "the most expensive haircut in history".[[Nigel Hamilton (author)|Nigel Hamilton]], ''[http://books.google.com.au/books?id=wJmUbbMCh6UC&pg=PA120 Bill Clinton: Mastering the Presidency]'', p. 120. Hamilton suggests that the fiasco "typified the problems of the White House under its new management, or mis-management."Hamilton, ''Bill Clinton'', p. 119. The following day, a new controversy broke, the [[White House travel office controversy]], also known as "travelgate". ==References== {{reflist}} [[Category:1993 in American politics]] [[Category:Clinton administration controversies]] [[Category:1993 controversies]]
