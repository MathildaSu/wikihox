It was initially used as the [[Malacca City Council|Malacca Historical City Municipal Council]] building. The Kite Museum was officially opened September 1995 by then [[List of Chief Ministers of Malacca|Malacca Chief Minister]] [[Mohd Zin Abdul Ghani]] in conjunction with the Regional Malay Customs seminar.http://www.virtualmuseummelaka.com/layang.htm ==Architecture== The museum is located at the upper floor of the building. The building also houses the [[People's Museum]] and [[Beauty Museum]] at the ground floor and top floor respectively. ==Exhibitions== The museum displays various aspects of kites, such as communication, use as weapons, sport, leisure activities, how different countries make kites, different materials to make kites, role of kites in the life of people etc. It also displays the Wau Gallery, Traditional Games, Top Spinning Gallery and Datuk Wira Haji Md Borhan bin Yaman Gallery. ==Opening time== The museum opens everyday from 9.00 a.m. to 5.00 p.m.{{cite web|url=http://www.perzim.gov.my/en/harga-tiket/|title=PERZIM - Harga Tiket|work=PERZIM}} ==See also== {{commons category|Kite Museum}} * [[List of museums in Malaysia]] * [[List of tourist attractions in Malacca]] ==References== {{reflist}} {{Museums of Malaysia}} {{Malaysia-museum-stub}} [[Category:Museums in Malacca]] [[Category:1995 establishments in Malaysia]] [[Category:Museums established in 1995]] [[Category:Kites]] {{Coord|2|11|30.1|N|102|14|57.2|E|display=title}}
