xml:space="preserve" bytes="3483"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Pioneers press|timestamp=20161005214935|year=2016|month=October|day=5|substed=yes|help=off}} Founded by publisher/editor Jessie Duke {{cite web|url=http://jessie-duke.tumblr.com/ |title=Hard Fifty Duke |publisher=Jessie-duke.tumblr.com |date=2016-03-31 |accessdate=2016-10-05}} on a farm outside the town of Leavenworth, Kansas, Pioneers Press {{cite web|url=http://pioneerspress.com/pages/about-us |title=About Us – Pioneers Press |publisher=Pioneerspress.com |date= |accessdate=2016-10-05}} is a publishing house focusing "on survival and sustainability on the farm and in the city, in addition to health, gender, sexuality, social justice and food movements, and literary works by up-and-coming authors." {{cite web|url=http://pioneerspress.com/pages/about-us |title=About Us – Pioneers Press |publisher=Pioneerspress.com |date= |accessdate=2016-10-05}} Since the press's launch in the summer 2012, Pioneers Press has release titles that have made the best-seller lists of various independent bookstores, including [[Powell's Books]]' #1 best-selling small press title of 2013, 2014, and 2015 ''The Do-It-Yourself Guide to Fighting the Big Motherf*ckin' Sad'').{{cite web|url=http://news.adamgnade.com/post/137689682581/wearepioneerspress-the-powells-books-on |title=ADAM GNADE — wearepioneerspress: The Powell’s Books on |publisher=News.adamgnade.com |date=2016-01-20 |accessdate=2016-10-05}} Recent published authors include Bart Schaneman, Trace Ramsey, Matt Gauck, Olivier Matthon, Julia Eff, and Craig Kelly. In 2013, Duke was awarded a Rocket Grant by the the Charlotte Street Foundation and the University of Kansas Spencer Museum of Art, with funding provided by the [[Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts]]{{cite web|url=http://www.pitch.com/news/article/20564584/pioneers-press-attempts-a-radical-experiment-in-literature-and-living |title=Pioneers Press attempts a radical experiment in literature and living | Kansas City Pitch |publisher=Pitch.com |date=2014-08-12 |accessdate=2016-10-05}}. The funded project, the Hard Fifty Farm Zine Mobile, is a touring small-press library and portable educational facility based out of a converted horse trailer, focusing on the empowerment of independent publishing. Pioneers Press announced in 2016 a team-up with San Diego-based record label [[Three One G]] ([[The Locust]], [[Head Wound City]], [[the Blood Brothers]]) and released its first collaborative book, ''Locust House'', in March of that year.{{cite web|url=http://threeonegrecords.tumblr.com/post/140042013127 |title=Three One G and Pioneers Press team up to form |publisher=Threeonegrecords.tumblr.com |date= |accessdate=2016-10-05}}. In the summer of 2016 Duke was awarded an Innovative Leader scholarship to attend [[Yale]]'s Summer Publishing Course {{cite web|url=http://publishing-course.yale.edu/2016-ypc-scholarship-winners/ |title=2016 YPC Innovative Leader Scholarship Winners — Yale Publishing Course |publisher=Publishing-course.yale.edu |date= |accessdate=2016-10-05}}. == References == [[Category:publishers]]
