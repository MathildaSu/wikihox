xml:space="preserve" bytes="4200"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Albert Mensah|timestamp=20140310162700|year=2014|month=March|day=10|substed=yes|help=off}} {{BLP sources|date=March 2014}} '''Albert Mensah''' is a [[Ghanaian]] born motivational speaker, business coach, and author. Mensah speaks and writes about embracing change in order to succeed, increasing work morale and productivity, and enhancing team commitment to youth and corporate audiences alike. Mensah's books include "When the Drumbeat Changes Dance A Different Dance" (2000), "OK Means Opportunity Knocking (Make the Most of Every Chance Encounter" (2005), and "Secrets of Effective Goal Making" (2008). {{cite web|title=Albert Mensah - Big Speak|url=http://www.bigspeak.com/albert-mensah.html|publisher=Big Speak|accessdate=24 February 2014}} == Early life and Career == Albert grew up in Abesewa, [[Ghana]] and he is an [[Ashanti]]. In 1978 he left Ghana for [[America]]. Matriculating onward to receive a Bachelor's degree in English from [[Western Maryland College]], Mensah also has academic prestige from the [[Dale Carnegie School]] of Public Speaking. After graduating from Western Maryland College he went on to have a successful Fortune 500 sales career. Later his passion for the sport of soccer lead him to open a sporting goods store. Finally, he earned the Certified Speaking Professional certificate {{cite web|title=Albert Mensa - 2014 MLK Symposium|url=http://www.prweb.com/releases/2014/01/prweb11494388.htm|publisher=PR Web|accessdate=24 February 2014}} and has grown his public speaking business well. In 1999, Albert Mensah placed second in the Toastmasters International World Championship of Public Speaking contest held in [[Chicago]], [[USA]]. Although the award usually takes a 5-year period to attain, Mensah managed to attain it within a 4-year period. This victory made him one of the first [[African]]s, and Ghanaians to ever attain this feat. He is rated one of the top seven percent of professional speakers internationally. Mensah has appeared in the lineup to speak with [[Harry Belafonte]] at the [[University of Michigan]]. Mensah has worked with large and international companies such as [[Costco]], [[John Deere]], [[Microsoft]], [[Starbucks]], and [[Boeing]], he continues to speak to audiences of all sizes for inspiration and change. Deemed as the "Ambassador of Opportunity", Mensah speaks to audiences today while participating in several podcasts and radio shows as well. He is also a consult for companies on the verge of corporate change, organizational transformation, and a keynote speaker too. == Books == Albert Mensah's writing of inspirational books aim to help readers clarify their dreams, get clear on their goals, grab every opportunity, and innovate, not imitate. The book 'When The Drum Beat Changes Dance A Different Dance' - takes a deeper look at dreams and how everything begins with one, realistic goals, a certain passion to move forward, and action. 'Wake Up!' - Holds 3 steps to a wonderful life, 7 beliefs of high achievers, how to cope with disappointment, 10 strategies to refocus efforts, and how to change self-sabotaging behavior. 'OK Means Opportunity Knocking' - Shows the thin line between activity and action, shaking off fear, choosing a dream team partner, and rewriting the "Golden Rule". ==References== {{reflist}} == Albert Mensah ==
