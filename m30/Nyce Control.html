Products are sold indirectly through Tier 1 [[Multiple system operator|MSOs]], and are also available through home automation ecosystems such as [[Control4]].{{cite news|title=NYCE signs Supply Agreement with Hitron|url=http://www.nycecontrol.com/?p=2658|date=August 31, 2012}}{{cite news|title=Contact Sensors Are the Secret Sauce in Many Home Control Applications|url=http://www.electronichouse.com/article/contact_sensors_are_the_secret_sauce_in_many_home_control_applications/|date=May 3, 2013}} Describing its control and sensor products as the "smallest Zigbee devices in the world ... with the lowest possible power consumption, smallest physical size and simplest installation",{{cite web|url=http://www.hometheater.com/content/honey-i-shrunk-zigbee-sensors|title=Honey, I Shrunk the Zigbee Sensors!}} the goal of all NYCE products is to be “invisible” in consumers' lives.{{cite news|title=NYCE Product for Sale On-line in Control4 Marketplace|url=http://www.nycecontrol.com/?p=2614|date=May 11, 2012}} A large market for NYCE is home security packages offered through broadband providers, where NYCE supplies wireless security sensors. NYCE sensors are also used to reduce energy consumption via lighting automation, sensing room occupancy and accordingly turning lights on and off.{{cite news|title=Here’s how to make your house smarter. Hitron’s customer base is the Tier 1 [[Multiple system operator|MSOs]] market including: Comcast, Rogers, Time Warner, Bright House, Comporium and Cox Cable. * October 2011 NYCE signs an Authorized Supplier Agreement with [[Control4]] granting NYCE access to sell its wireless ZigBee sensor and control products in Control4’s on-line Marketplace * 2010 Company founded.
