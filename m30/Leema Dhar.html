She is considered as one of the young women novelists under 25 years of age{{cite news |title= A Few Must Read Books Of Contemporary Indian Women Novelist Who Are Below 25 |last=Menon |first=Manini|url= http://www.womensweb.in/2016/02/indian-women-novelists/|publisher=Women’s Web. |location=Mumbai, India |date= 20 February 2016}}.As on February 2016 she has written seven books{{cite news |title= CITY GIRL OUT WITH HER NRW FICTION |last=Kumanr |first=K Sandeep|url= http://www.leadstartcorp.com/media-corner/leadstart-people/the-committed-sin/|publisher= [[Hindustan Times]]|location=Allahabad, India |date= 24 April 2016}}. She writes columns and articles in various magazines and news papers{{cite news |title=It’s An Apple, Either Way… |first=Leema |last=Dhar|url=http://youthaction.in/2015/08/its-an-apple-either-way/ |publisher=Youth Action. |location=Indore, India |date= 13 August 2015}}. ==Personal life== She was born on 22 December 1993, in [[Allahabad]], India. She finished her schooling and ISC [[Indian School Certificate]] examination from [[St. Mary's Convent Inter College]]. Her creative passion bloomed in her childhood days{{cite news |title=An Eternal Rest |first=Leema |last=Dhar|url=http://www.smccjallahabad.org/StudentGallery/KidsImage.aspx?photoid=TWpjMA== |publisher=Student Gallery. |location=Allahabad, India |date= April 2011}}, she left her All India Engineering/Architecture Entrance Examination rank in 2011, to become an engineer. And she pursued humanities in her [[Graduation]] in Psychology & English Literature. Currently, she is doing her final year [[Master of Arts]] in English Literature from [[University of Allahabad]]. ==Career== Leema Dhar’s first anthology of Hindi poems कुछ लफ्ज़ नक़ाब में(2007) was published when she was in her teen(9th standard). Her second book and the first anthology of English poems For The Hundred Tomorrows2010) at the age of 16. Her debut novel, Till We Meet Again (August 2012) was a romantic story of teenagers’ nuances of love, the bouts of hesitation, and a lifetime of romance. It went on to become popular soon after. A girl, who avenges her mother’s rape, when she was just eight weeks’ foetus, was the theme of her third novel The Girl Who Kissed the Snake, deals with the psychological and emotional trauma of a mother and daughter{{cite news |title= Review: The Girl Who Kissed the Snake|last=Dhar |first=leema|url= http://www.merinews.com/article/review-the-girl-who-kissed-the-snake/15887602.shtml|publisher=Merinews.com|location=New Delhi, India |date=5 July 2013}}. MOM AND I Love a Terrorist{{cite news |title= Review: Mom And I Love A Terrorist |last=Dhar |first=leema|url=http://www.merinews.com/article/review-the-girl-who-kissed-the-snake/15887602.shtml|publisher=Merinews.com|location=New Delhi,India |date=11 November 2012}} was her second fictional work, which is full of love and thrill, and yet has a touch of romance in it. She has dealt with the topic of terrorism in this novel.
