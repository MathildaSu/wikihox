[[Download]] }} ''Elysian Shadows'' is an upcoming [[Homebrew (video games)|homebrew]] 2D/3D [[role-playing game]] for [[Dreamcast|Sega Dreamcast]], [[Microsoft Windows]], [[OS X]], [[Linux]], [[iOS]], [[Android (operating system)|Android]], and [[Ouya]]. It is being marketed as a "[[History of video game consoles (eighth generation)|Next Generation]]" 2D RPG, as it fuses aspects of [[History of video game consoles (fourth generation)|16-bit era]] console RPGs with modern advanced rendering techniques, dynamic 2D and 3D perspectives, positional and dynamic audio, rigid body physics, and particle simulators.{{cite web|url=http://elysianshadows.com/game/information/|title=Official Website |publisher=elysianshadows.com |accessdate=2014-05-26}} The game is best-known as the focus of the "[[#Adventures in Game Development|Adventures in Game Development]]" [[YouTube]] webseries, where fans have been given a behind-the-scenes look at the development of '''Elysian Shadows''' since its inception in 2007.{{cite web|url=http://youtube.com/gyrovorbis|title=GyroVorbis' YouTube Channel |publisher=youtube.com |accessdate=2014-05-26}} The team is currently promoting the game in preparation for a [[Kickstarter]] crowd-sourcing campaign during Q2/Q3 2014.{{cite web|url=http://elysianshadows.com/kickstarter|title=Official - Kickstarter Info Page |publisher=elysianshadows.com |accessdate=2014-05-26}} == Setting == Limited information has been revealed regarding the storyline of Elysian Shadows, although the developers have been vocal in insisting that the game was originally conceived as a storyline concept and that plot and character progression would be the central focus of the RPG.{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K56mndHzpQc|title=Adventures in Game Development Chapter 24 |publisher=youtube.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2014-04-09}} === World and Story === [[File:Elysian Shadows Screen Shot.jpg|thumb|Screenshot of the protagonist, Julien, standing in Loren's Market.]] Elysian Shadows takes place in a land known as Asphodel, playing host to a diverse set of biomes, including forests, caves, mountains, jungles, plains, tundra, and a variety of ancient ruins. The societies of Asphodel are caught in constant conflict between magic and technology, as the religious sects have been bestowed with the gifts of magic, while the intellectual sects are forced to turn to science and technology in their day-to-day lives. Upon uncovering a long-lost artifact in an ancient ruin, players find themselves thrust into the middle of this rising conflict to maintain the status quo.{{cite web|url=http://nichegamer.net/2014/04/discover-a-world-separated-by-magic-and-technology-in-elysian-shadows/|title=Discover a World Separated by Magic and Technology in Elysian Shadows |accessdate=2014-05-26 |publisher=nichgamer.com | date=2014-04-20}} In addition to a lush overworld, Asphodel also plays host to a variety of different underground ruins scattered about the lands. These ruins are excavated by "diggers" and scholars seeking to uncover their ancient secrets and hidden treasures. While the backstory of these ruins has not yet been revealed, they will play a major role in the plot progression of Elysian Shadows.{{cite web|url=http://elysianshadows.com/game/information/|title=Official World of Elysian Shadows|publisher=elysianshadows.com |accessdate=2014-05-26}} Various pieces of concept art for the ruins and their inhabitants have been posted by the team to social media sites, and the developers have announced during interviews that at least one of these ruins will be survival-horror themed, inspired by [[Resident Evil]] and [[Ocarina of Time]]'s Shadow Temple.{{cite web|url=http://twodashstash.com/2014/05/indie-stash-cast-falco-girgis-tyler-rogers-talk-elysian-shadows-developing-dreamcast-answer-questions/|title=Indie Stash Cast: Falco Girgis and Tyler Rogers Talk Elysian Shadows, Developing for the Dreamcast, and Answer Your Questions |accessdate=2014-05-26 |publisher=twodashstash.com | date=2014-05-20}} === Main characters === While the complete party has never officially been revealed, various party members have been released at one point or another through episodes of "[[#Adventures in Game Development|Adventures in Game Development]]," or on the team's official social media pages.{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/media/set/?set=a.410087505701567.88557.201764123200574&type=3|title=Facebook - Party Concepts |accessdate=2014-05-26 |publisher=facebook.com | date=2012-06-21}}{{cite web|url=http://youtube.com/gyrovorbis|title=GyroVorbis YouTube Channel|accessdate=2014-05-26|date=2008-08-07}} * '''Julien''' is the main protagonist of Elysian Shadows. He is revealed to be a digger living in Loren, uncovering artifacts for the local museum. His name was reportedly inspired by the team's favorite band, [[Julien-K]]. * '''Eryn''' is the female protagonist of Elysian Shadows. She also lives in Loren and runs the local museum, studying ancient artifacts with her father. She has been said to be a strong female lead, inspired by Lucca of [[Chrono Trigger]] and [[Lara Croft]] of [[Tomb Raider]]. * '''Angel (name not known)''' has been teased as a third party member during an episode of [[#Adventures in Game Development|Adventures in Game Development]]. Nothing has been revealed of her backstory, but her sprite has been shown displaying a prominent pair of wings. == Style and Influences == === Visuals === [[File:Elysian Shadows.png|thumb|left|3D Perspective and Dynamic Lights in Loren's Church.]] Graphically, Elysian Shadows uses mostly [[pixel art]] in the style of 16-bit RPGs, with slightly higher resolutions. The team has cited console RPGs such as [[Chrono Trigger]], [[Secret of Mana]], and [[Phantasy Star]] as artistic inspiration; however, unlike other games of the genre, Elysian Shadows features advanced lighting techniques such as [[shadow mapping]], [[normal mapping]], and [[specular highlights|specular highlighting]].{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iMsB17loGcY|title=Adventures in Game Development Chapter 21 |publisher=youtube.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2014-01-13}} The environments are also rendered in full 3D, which allows the game to dynamically change between 2D orthographic and 3D perspectives during cutscenes or other in-game events.{{cite web|url=http://elysian-shadows.wikia.com/wiki/Elysian_Shadows|title=Unofficial Elysian Shadows Wiki |accessdate=2014-05-26 |publisher=wikia.com | date=2014-03-27}} According to the official website, the team's mission for the visuals of ES is {{quote|"[..] to push the boundaries of pixel art by creating diverse, lush environments for players to explore with beautifully lit scenes and advanced rendering effects that could not have been possible on older hardware."|Elysian Shadows Team|[http://elysianshadows.com/game/information/ Elysian Shadows Official Website, Game Information]}} === Audio === Similar to the graphical style, the audio of Elysian Shadows is also a fusion of old and new. Background music dynamically changes in intensity to match the player's environment, and sound effects are positional, in full 3D. The soundtrack uses vintage electronic music hardware with modern synthesizer software, resulting in songs with a distinct 16-bit chiptune-style mixed with a variety of instruments, choirs, and electronic sounds.{{cite web|url=http://elysianshadows.com/game/information/|title=Official - Music, 3D positional & dynamic |publisher=elysianshadows.com |accessdate=2014-05-26}} In his blog posts and during [[#Adventures in Game Development|Adventures in Game Development]], composer [[Connor Linning]] has sampled various beats and sounds from popular [[Sega Genesis]] and [[Super Nintendo]] games, including [[Sonic the Hedgehog]], [[Harvest Moon]], and [[Donkey Kong Country]].{{cite web|url=http://www.connorlinning.com/2014/01/26/elysian-shadows-soundtrack-development-blog-2/|title=Elysian Shadows Soundtrack Development Blog 2 |accessdate=2014-05-26 |publisher=ConnorLinning.com | date=2014-01-26}} === Gameplay === Elysian Shadows' gameplay has drawn influence from a wide variety of games, many of which aren't even RPGs. The RPG aspects of Elysian Shadows have been said to be primarily inspired by 16-bit [[Squaresoft]], [[Enix]], and [[Sega]] RPGs, while the adventure aspects of the game have been primarily influenced by the [[Legend of Zelda]] series, with an emphasis on environmental interaction and rewarding overworld exploration. Additionally, the game includes [[Platform game|platformer]] gameplay mechanics such as dashing and jumping not usually found in top-down RPGs. The team believes these mechanics will open exploration up into the third dimension and will make combat more interesting, citing the speed and agility of the [[Megaman X]] series. The team has referred to the battle engine as a marriage between the [[Secret of Mana]] and the [[God of War]] series.{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/elysianshadows/photos/pb.201764123200574.-2207520000.1401124606./730020550374926/?type=3&theater|title=Combat Engine Facebook Post |publisher=facebook.com |accessdate=2014-05-26}} During a podcast interview, the team mentioned that the recurring theme of scattered underground ruins in the world of Asphodel was inspired by the [[Megaman Legends]] series, and that their composer's primary source of inspiration comes from the survival horror genre and franchises like [[Resident Evil]] and [[Silent Hill]]. == Development == Development on Elysian Shadows is widely regarded to have started Oct 7, 2007, as this is when the first Chapter of the "[[#Adventures in Game Development|Adventures in Game Development]]" series was uploaded to [[YouTube]].{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iimLO5UkWYg|title=Adventures in Game Development Chapter 1 |publisher=youtube.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2007-08-07}} Going by date, the game has been in development for almost 7 years, and this number is often cited by critics regarding the game's lengthy development cycle.{{cite web|url=http://www.micromania.es/noticias/elysian-shadows-7-anos-desarrollo-indie|title=Elysian Shadows: 7 años de desarrollo indie |publisher=micromania.com |accessdate=2014-05-08 |date=2014-04-09}} The team has publically dismissed the "7 year" estimate during interviews, as it is claimed that they were just kids in their parents' attics at the time, with no development experience, and much of the time has been spent growing into their roles as game developers. In addition to time spent on the actual game, the team also cites time spent developing their own engine, tools, and multiplatform library, further bloating this figure. They have also taken several extended breaks throughout development, as many of their members are attending graduate school and work full-time jobs.{{cite web|url=http://coreelementspodcast.blogspot.com/2014/05/ce-61-elysian-shadows.html|title=Core Elements 61: Elysian Shadows|publisher=http://coreelementspodcast.blogspot.com/ |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2014-05-09}} === Publisher === On 26th May 2014, the team shared on their Facebook page that developers of [[Pier Solar]] Water Melon Co has contacted the team about possibly publishing Elysian Shadows.https://www.facebook.com/elysianshadows/photos/a.261525073891145.61859.201764123200574/748968801813434/?type=1 The press release did not say that a deal has been finalized, merely discussed the possibility. === Sega Dreamcast Support === Elysian Shadows began as an an RPG exclusively for the [[Sega Dreamcast]], but the engine was quickly ported to many more platforms as the [[#Adventures in Game Development|Adventures in Game Development]] [[YouTube]] series rose in popularity. With the inclusion of advanced shading techniques in Elysian Shadows' visuals, the team has stated many times that the game will be pushing the console to its limits.{{cite web|url=http://dreamcast-talk.com/forum/viewtopic.php?f=53&t=4814&start=50|title=Dreamcast-Talk Elysian Shadows Discussion Thread |publisher=dreamcast-talk.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2012-07-24}} The limited resources on the Dreamcast have often been the target of many jokes throughout the [[#Adventures in Game Development|Adventures in Game Development]] series, as the team has stated that the development builds frequently run out of [[texture memory]]. They have also joked that players may have to manually free memory resources allocated to scripts when the framerate drops. While the [[Sega Dreamcast]] is no longer the sole-focus of the Elysian Shadows project, the lead engine developer, [[Falco Girgis]], insists that Elysian Shadows continues to support the aging console, calling it his "quest" to release a game for the platform he grew up developing for.{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6JN90aCRuQI|title=The Week in Geek - Episode 5|publisher=youtube.com |accessdate=2014-05-28 |date=2014-05-19}} === Technology === The majority of the technology behind Elysian Shadows has been developed in-house by the team, including the Engine, Toolkit, and platform-independent driver back-end.{{cite web|url=http://elysianshadows.com/game/information/|title=Official - Powering the Future of the 2D RPG|publisher=elysianshadows.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2014-04-09}} ==== ESGamma ==== ESGamma is the proprietary engine developed in-house to power Elysian Shadows. It is written in [[C++11]] and links against the [[#LibGyro|LibGyro]] API to achieve platform independence. The engine boasts full 3D dynamic lighting, 2D and 3D dynamic cameras, 2D rigid-body physics, advanced particle effects, bump and specular maps, and shadow mapping. It is fully extensible through the [[Lua (programming language)|Lua scripting language]], which allows the Elysian Shadows team to create and script custom contents and events for the world of Elysian Shadows in a manner similar to Behavior Scripts in the [[Unity3D]] engine.{{cite web|url=http://elysianshadows.com/game/information/|title=Official - ESGamma|publisher=elysianshadows.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2014-04-09}}{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LiI59XlCeK0|title=Adventures in Game Development Chapter 20|publisher=youtube.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2012-04-08}} ==== ESToolkit ==== ESToolkit or "ESTk" is a 2D level editing and creation tool developed by the Elysian Shadows team to create the environments for Elysian Shadows. It is developed in [[C++11]] with the [[Qt (software)|Qt framework]] and is said to share a significant amount of code with ESGamma. ESTk offers advanced editing features for developing environments using tilesheets.{{cite web|url=http://elysianshadows.com/game/information/|title=Official - ESToolkit|publisher=elysianshadows.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2014-04-09}} It also contains an entity/component editor that was influenced by the [[Unity3D]] editor. One of its most well-known features is the ability to give flat tiles "elevation" information within the levels, allowing the team to create 3D levels for Elysian Shadows using only 2D assets.{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ecjtXlo89K0|title=Elysian Shadows Live Stream #3|publisher=youtube.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2013-05-25}} ==== LibGyro ==== LibGyro is a platform-independent multimedia API written in C, providing interfaces for rendering, device input, audio, asset management, linear algebra, and system management. These interfaces are implemented as statically-linked libraries on various platforms, including [[Dreamcast|Sega Dreamcast]], [[Microsoft Windows]], [[OS X]], [[Linux]], [[iOS]], [[Android (operating system)|Android]], [[Ouya]], which are then linked against the ESGamma engine, allowing it to remain truly platform-independent through compile-time and link-time [[Polymorphism (computer science)|polymorphism]].{{cite web|url=http://elysianshadows.com/game/information/|title=Official - libGyro|publisher=elysianshadows.com |accessdate=2014-05-26 |date=2014-04-09}} It has been demonstrated at various times throughout the "[[#Adventures in Game Development|Adventures in Game Development]]" series that the linear algebra library implementation for at least the x86 and [[Dreamcast|Sega Dreamcast]] platforms are assembly-optimized to take advantage of [[SIMD]] instructions for vector and matrix processing. LibGyro has enabled the Elysian Shadows team to easily move the game to various platforms without ever requiring engine rewrites or modifications.{{cite web|url=http://www.linkedin.com/in/falcogirgis|title=Falco Girigs LinkedIn - libGyro Project|publisher=linkedin.com |accessdate=2014-05-26}} === Adventures in Game Development === [[File:AiGD.jpg|thumb|Elysian Shadows Team releasing ESTkv1.5.2 in AiGD Chapter 20]] "Adventures in Game Development" is an ongoing [[YouTube]] [[video blog]] series uploaded to GyroVorbis's Channel, giving viewers a behind-the-scenes look at the life of indie game developers, by chronicling the development of the Elysian Shadows 2D RPG from start to finish. The majority of the videos are filmed in the style of a reality television series, with limited post-production and editing effects, and a raw, uncut presentation. The series is well-known for its adult humor, caffeine abuse, lack of sleep, and dysfunctional antics. Despite this fact, many of the videos dive into great technical detail and the series is viewed by many as being partially educational.{{cite web|url=http://www.destructoid.com/i-m-into-adventures-in-game-development-273218.phtml|title=I'm into Adventures in Game Development|accessdate=2014-05-26|publisher=destructoid.com|date=2014-04-14}} While the series has historically been mostly programming-oriented, the latest Chapters attempt to follow all aspects of game development, including coding, art, music, and design. ====Accidental Success==== While the series has been highly successful as a game development video blog on YouTube, with over 9k subscribers as of May 2014, the team has admitted that this success was purely accidental in a recent podcast interview. Chapters 1-3 were apparently recorded with the purpose of being end-game unlockable content, inspired by the developer videos at the end of [[God of War]]. The team was shocked to find a positive reaction to the original videos on YouTube, and they decided to continue documenting their development progress on YouTube, transforming the series into a [[video blog]].{{cite web|url=http://coreelementspodcast.blogspot.com/2014/05/ce-61-elysian-shadows.html|title=Core Elements 61: Elysian Shadows|publisher=http://coreelementspodcast.blogspot.com/ |accessdate=2014-05-08 |date=2014-05-09}} ====Chapter Summaries==== Adventures in Game Development is released periodically as a series of "Chapters," with "Adventures in Game Development Chapter 25" being the latest release. {| class='wikitable' |- !
