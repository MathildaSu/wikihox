Billed as "the greatest album of Christmas music clucked by a human chicken ever," it was originally released in 2002. ==Background== Keysser, a software services manager at [[Fordham University]] in [[New York City]],[http://www.fordham.edu/campus_resources/fordham_it/about_us/software_services__i/contacts_25118.asp “Fordham IT,” Fordham University] recorded 14 minutes of material in ten Christmas season songs on ''Happy Clucking Holidays''. He replaced the lyrics with chicken clucking, and used a variety of background musical arrangements. Keysser has performed chicken-inspired Christmas music twice on the “Is This Anything?” segment of the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]'', clucking “Joy to the World” on December 17, 2002, and “Deck the Halls” on December 17, 2003.[http://lateshow.cbs.com/latenight/lateshow/wahoo/index/php/20031217.phtml “Is This Anything?” Late Show with David Letterman, CBS] ==Reception== ''Happy Clucking Holidays'' has received somewhat wary reviews. The ''[[Atlanta Journal-Constitution]]'' commented, "You just haven't been annoyed until you've been annoyed by Dirk Keysser clucking out the melody to 'Little Drummer Boy'."[http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=AT&p_theme=at&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0FF1DCADE4B5DB6B&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM “The Holiday Sounds,” November 28, 2003, Atlanta Journal-Constitution (fee access required)] The ''[[Contra Costa Times]]'' remarked, “You have not known fear and hatred until you have heard a man clucking like a chicken to 'Silent Night' - with feeling."[http://www.anecdotage.com/index.php?aid=16451 “Happy Clucking Holidays,” Anecdotage.com] More positive feedback came from ''[[USA Today]]'' which noted, “The gaping hole in Christmas catalogs for carols sung in a chicken voice is filled by this eclucktic 14-minute collection.” [http://www.usatoday.com/life/music/news/2003-12-16-holiday-music-guide_x.htm “Making a holiday album list,” USA Today, December 18, 2003] And [[Jack Cafferty]], on the [[CNN]] program ''American Morning'', commented the recording had value “because we're really lacking in Christmas carols sung by a chicken.” [http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-19816097_ITM “The Spirit of Regifting,” CNN (transcript), December 25, 2003 (library card access required)] ==Future releases== Following the release of ''Happy Clucking Holidays'', Keysser announced plans to release [[poultry]]-inspired albums based on the classical music compositions of [[Maurice Ravel]] and [[Sergei Prokofiev]].[http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4159/is_/ai_n12748569 “Storyville,” The Independent on Sunday, December 14, 2003] To date, however, ''Happy Clucking Holidays'' remains the only recording featuring Keysser’s chicken music. ==Track listing== #"[[Deck the Halls]]" – 1:13 #"[[Rudolph the Red-Nosed Reindeer]]" – 0:59 #"[[Have Yourself a Merry Little Christmas]]" – 2:38 #"[[Carol of the Bells]]" – 0:48 #"[[Silent Night]]" – 2:02 #"[[God Rest Ye Merry Gentlemen]]" – 1:26 #"[[Little Drummer Boy]]" – 2:03 #"[[Ode to Joy]]" – 0:39 #"[[Winter Wonderland]]" – 2:30 #"[[Joy to the World]]" – 0:32 ==References== {{Reflist}} [[Category:2002 Christmas albums]] [[Category:Dirk Keysser albums]]
