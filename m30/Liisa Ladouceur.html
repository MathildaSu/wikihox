xml:space="preserve" bytes="2368"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Liisa Ladouceur|timestamp=20150118055810|year=2015|month=January|day=18|substed=yes}} {{unreliable sources|date=January 2015}} '''Liisa Ladouceur''' writes about music and unpopular{{clarify|date=January 2015}} culture, and is the author of two books, ''Encyclopedia Gothica'' (2011, [[ECW Press]]) [http://www.worldcat.org/title/encyclopedia-gothica/oclc/707327955&referer=brief_results WorldCat book record and ''How to Kill a Vampire: Fangs in Folklore, Film and Fiction'' (2013, ECW Press). [http://www.worldcat.org/title/how-to-kill-a-vampire-fangs-in-folklore-film-and-fiction/oclc/829738917&referer=brief_results WorldCat book record] She regularly talks about [[Goths|goth]] music and [[subculture]], [[vampire]]s and other such dark arts on radio and television, where she brings shadowy subjects to light, and was the keynote speaker for ''Popular Culture Association of Canada'' in 2012. Ladouceur started all this with a community TV show in high school and a [[Do it yourself|DIY]] zine in university, and has since been widely published. She’s a former music columnist for ''[[Eye Weekly]]'', [[CBC Radio]]’s ''[[Definitely Not the Opera|DNTO]]'' and ''[[Rue Morgue (magazine)|Rue Morgue Magazine]]'', and currently writes about good records and interesting people for ''[[The Huffington Post|Huffington Post]] Canada'', ''[[The Grid (newspaper)|The Grid]]'' and [[Society of Composers, Authors and Music Publishers of Canada|SOCAN]]’s ''Words and Music'', and curates playlists for [[Songza]]. She has appeared on [[M3 (Canadian TV channel)|MuchMoreMusic]], ''[[George Stroumboulopoulos Tonight]]'', ''[[Kevin Newman (journalist)|Kevin Newman]] Live'', and [[CBC News Network|CBC Newsworld]].
