He is best known for founding [[Blippar]], a [[visual search]] and [[augmented reality]] (AR) company based in the United Kingdom, of which he is the CEO.{{cite web|url=http://www.wsj.com/video/blippar-aims-to-be-the-future-of-search-engines/837454C0-96CA-40E2-B09D-1913A140425C.html|title=Blippar Aims to Be the Future of Search Engines|date=July 11, 2014|work=wsj.com}} __FORCETOC__ ==Early life and education== Born in India, Mitra left home as a teenager to work and live in a slum in New Delhi.{{cite web|url=http://www.thesundaytimes.co.uk/sto/business/Tech_and_Media/article1536951.ece|title=The $1bn unicorns - The Sunday Times|work=thesundaytimes.co.uk}} He co-founded his first startup, womeninfoline.com, an internet portal for women, which eventually went public on the Indian stock market.{{cite web|url=http://www.standard.co.uk/business/business-news/a-blip-on-every-screen-thats-the-aim-of-realworld-browser-blippar-9761784.html|title=Growth capital: A blip on every screen: that’s the aim of ‘real-world browser’ Blippar|work=London Evening Standard}} Mitra then relocated to the United Kingdom to continue his education, earning a master's degree from the [[London School of Economics]]. ==Career== Following his graduation, Mitra continued his entrepreneurship by co-founding several startups, including U.K. insurance provider [[Swiftcover]] where he served as Head of Innovation.{{cite web|url=http://www.postonline.co.uk/post/analysis/1898609/pstf-swiftcover-brand-boosted-insurance-iphone-app|title=PSTF: Swiftcover brand boosted by non-insurance Iphone app|work=Post}} In 2007, Swiftcover was acquired by [[AXA|AXA Insurance]].{{cite web|url=http://www.postonline.co.uk/post/news/2233780/former-swiftcover-exec-reames-departs-axa|title=Former Swiftcover exec Reames departs Axa|work=Post}} While working for [[AXA|AXA Insurance]], Mitra met Omar Tayeb, and the two were inspired to create Blippar after sharing a joke in a pub about imagining the [[Elizabeth II|Queen of England]] coming to life from a £20 note.
