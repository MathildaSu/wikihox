[[New York Shipbuilding Corporation]] }} [[Captain (United States)|Captain]] '''Allen Bevins Reed''' (April 3, 1884 – February 28, 1965){{cite web|url=http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=81686049|title=Capt Allen B. Reed Sr|publisher=Find a Grave|accessdate=2012-01-16}} was a [[United States Navy|U.S. Naval officer]] whose 30-year span of ship-commands began on a patrol [[gunboat]] fighting pirates and [[Moro people|Moro]] rebels in the [[Philippines]] at the turn of the 20th century and ended as the [[plank owner]] [[commanding officer]] of the newly commissioned [[heavy cruiser]] {{USS|New Orleans|CA-32|}} in the 1930s. In the latter years of his naval career, Reed was attached to the office of the [[Chief of Naval Operations]] where his assignments included Director of Fleet Maintenance Division, Assistant to the Chairman of the [[U.S. Maritime Commission]] and navy [[Liaison officer|liaison]] to the Office of Production Management before he went on retired inactive status in 1941, three months before the [[attack on Pearl Harbor]]. ==Early life and education== [[File:USNA First Class Cadet Petty Officers 1904.jpg|thumb|left|200px|USNA First Class Cadet Petty Officers, 1904. A.B. Reed, seated, front row, center]] [[File:USNA Gymnastics Team 1904.jpg|thumb|right|200px|USNA Gymnastics Team, 1904. A.B. Reed, seated, second from right; H.E. Kimmel, seated, second from left]] Allen Bevins Reed was born in [[Liberty, Missouri]], on April 3, 1884, the oldest of four children of [[Clay County, Missouri|Clay County]] [[Sheriff]] James French Reed (1852-1909) and Fannie G. (Wymore) Reed (1857-1936), married on March 28, 1883.[http://files.usgwarchives.net/mo/clay/newspapers/marriage215gnw.txt The Liberty Tribune, March 30, 1883] He was named for his grandfather, Captain Allen Grigsby Reed (1812-1899). Graduating from [[Liberty High School (Liberty, Missouri)|Liberty High School]] in 1900, Allen B. Reed received an appointment to enter the [[United States Naval Academy]] at [[Annapolis, Maryland]] from Rep. [[John Dougherty (Missouri politician)|John Dougherty]] (D-Missouri) of the 3rd [[congressional district]]. Reed entered the Academy on September 22, 1900, as one of 93 fourth class [[officer cadet|naval cadets]],[https://books.google.com/books?id=I-jc3TVTHiYC&printsec=frontcover&output=reader&source=gbs_atb_hover&pg=GBS.RA1-PA86 Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1901, p. 86] as [[midshipman|midshipmen]] were known until July 1, 1902 when the rank reverted to the traditional "midshipman".[http://www.usna.edu/admissions/history/1900.htm A Brief History of the United States Naval Academy] During the summers of 1901 and 1902 he made training cruises on the {{USS|Indiana|BB-1|}} and [[USS Severn (1898)|USS ''Chesapeake'']]. There were 71 third class [[officer cadet|naval cadets]] in 1901-1902,[https://books.google.com/books?id=I-jc3TVTHiYC&printsec=frontcover&output=reader&source=gbs_atb_hover&pg=GBS.RA2-PA87 Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1902, p. 87] 67 second class [[midshipmen]] in 1902-1903[https://books.google.com/books?id=0M1eMpPtLgcC&printsec=frontcover&output=reader&source=gbs_atb_hover&pg=GBS.PA91 Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1903, p. 91] and 62 first class [[midshipmen]] in 1903-1904.[https://books.google.com/books?id=0M1eMpPtLgcC&printsec=frontcover&output=reader&source=gbs_atb_hover&pg=GBS.RA1-PA98 Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1904, p. 98] While at [[United States Naval Academy|Annapolis]], Reed was on the track, gymnastics and football teams.The Lucky Bag, 1904, p. 69 Reed's fellow graduates of the [[United States Naval Academy|Naval Academy]] class of 1904 included future [[Admirals]] [[William Halsey, Jr.|William S. "Bull" Halsey, Jr.]], commander of [[Pacific Ocean Areas (command)|South Pacific Area]] and later [[United States Third Fleet]] during [[World War II]] and [[Husband E. Kimmel]], [[commander]] of [[United States Pacific Fleet]] at the time of the [[attack on Pearl Harbor]]. Completing his academic studies at the [[United States Naval Academy|Naval Academy]], Reed received his [[warrant officer|warrant]] as a [[passed midshipman]]. On January 25, 1904 he was detached from [[United States Naval Academy|Annapolis]] "to home and ready for sea".National Archives and Records Administration, "Transcript of Service of Allen Bevins Reed, Commander (T) USN", March 20, 1920[http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive-free/pdf?res=F40C10FC34591B728DDDAA0A94DC405B838CF1D3 N.Y. Times, April 23, 1903] At that time, [[passed midshipman|passed midshipmen]] were required to successfully complete two years sea duty before being commissioned as an [[ensign]]. On March 21, 1904, Reed reported for duty aboard the school ship [[USS Mohican (1883)|USS ''Mohican'']].[https://books.google.com/books?id=0M1eMpPtLgcC&printsec=frontcover&output=reader&source=gbs_atb_hover&pg=GBS.RA2-PA48 Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1905, p. 48] On April 8, 1904, ''Mohican'' was assigned as station ship at the [[Naval Station]], [[Olongapo]], [[Subic Bay]], [[Luzon]], [[Philippines]] and one month later sailed from [[Mare Island Naval Shipyard]] via [[Honolulu]], [[Guam]], and [[Cavite]] for her new station, arriving February 4, 1905. Reed served on ''Mohican'' variously as W division and signal officer before he was detached on April 17, 1905 to be [[executive officer]] of {{USS|Paragua|1888|6}}.[https://archive.org/stream/registercommiss20deptgoog#page/n50/mode/1up Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1906, pp. 42] ==U.S. Naval service== ===Asiatic fleet=== [[File:Midshipman Allen B. Reed.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Midshipman Allen B. Reed in 1904]] In 1905, as a 21-year-old passed midshipman, Reed was given the coveted assignment of second-in-command of a vessel, the 30-man [[gunboat]], {{USS|Paragua|1888|6}}, [[Ensign]] Charles S. Kerrick, commanding.[https://archive.org/stream/registercommiss20deptgoog#page/n195/mode/1up Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1906, pp. 188] ''Paragua'' was part of the [[Philippine Department#US Navy and USMC|Philippine Squadron]] of the [[Asiatic Fleet]][https://books.google.com/books?id=f2A7AQAAIAAJ&printsec=frontcover&output=reader&source=gbs_atb_hover&pg=GBS.PA388 Annual Report of the Secretary of the Navy, 1905, p. 388] that along with other ships in the "[[mosquito fleet]]" of mostly seized ex-Spanish gunboats and steam yachts, patrolled the [[Philippine Islands]] primarily south of [[Mindanao]] in the [[Sulu Archipelago]] to intercept [[pirates]], [[gunrunners]], [[slave-traders]] and support [[U.S. Army]] and local [[Philippine Constabulary]] ground forces during the [[Moro Rebellion]] of 1899-1913. During the [[Third Sulu Expedition]] in May 1905, the gunboat transported the provisional company of the US Army's 17th Infantry to Pata Island on an expedition to kill or capture a [[Moro people|Moro]] chieftain whose tribe had been robbing other islanders to the point that the [[panglima]] in charge of the island had requested the army's assistance. Midshipman Reed volunteered to accompany the expedition ashore and acted as signal officer to the gunboat using "wig-wag" flags and controlled the gunfire on a part of the firing line at the [[Battle of Pata Island on May 13, 1905]]. During the fighting the rifle of a soldier standing by Reed became jammed and the midshipman gave him his sidearm to fire. Reed was commended by the army company commander in his report to [[Major General]] [[Leonard Wood]], who in his report commended ''Paragua'' for its assistance in the expedition. Promoted to ensign on February 2, 1906, Reed assumed command of ''Paragua'' that same day from Kerrick. On December 14, 1906, Reed detached from ''Paragua'' to the [[protected cruiser]] {{USS|Baltimore|C-3|}}, cruising Asiatic, Philippine and Australian waters before returning to the [[Brooklyn Navy Yard|New York Navy Yard]] on April 26, 1907.[http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive-free/pdf?res=F70717FE385A15738DDDAE0A94DC405B878CF1D3 New York Times, "Baltimore Home from Far Off Seas", April 27, 1907][http://babel.hathitrust.org/cgi/pt?id=mdp.39015036626235;view=image;seq=470;num=200;q1=%22allen%20b.%20reed%22;start=1;size=10;page=search Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1907, p. 200] ===Pacific fleet=== Reed was assigned to the protected cruiser {{USS|Charleston|C-22|}} of the [[United States Pacific Fleet|Pacific Fleet]] Second Squadron Third Division, from June 10, 1907 to April 18, 1908 as W & D division officer and senior assistant [[engineering officer]].[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn85038615/1907-06-12/ed-1/seq-8/;words=A+Reed+B+Ensign?date1=1907&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1908&proxtext=Ensign+A.+B.+Reed&y=4&x=8&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=1 The Times-Dispatch: Richmond, VA, June 12, 1907][https://books.google.com/books?id=DsYcAQAAIAAJ&printsec=frontcover&output=reader&source=gbs_atb_hover&pg=GBS.PA205 Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1908, p. 205] ''Charleston'' sailed along the [[West Coast of the United States|west coast]] from [[Mexico]] to [[British Columbia]] on exercises and fleet maneuvers until entering [[Mare Island Naval Shipyard]] in March 1908 to prepare for transfer to the [[Asiatic Fleet]]. Ensign Reed detached from ''Charleston'' on April 18, 1908 to the protected cruiser {{USS|Albany|CL-23|}}, of the [[United States Pacific Fleet|Pacific Fleet]] Second Squadron Fourth Division, as senior engineer, reporting for duty on April 19, 1908 at Mare Island where ''Albany'' was berthed.[http://babel.hathitrust.org/cgi/pt?id=mdp.39015036626235;view=image;q1=albany%20mayo;start=1;size=100;page=root;seq=750;num=206 Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1908, p. 206] Reed was promoted to [[lieutenant (naval)|lieutenant]] on February 2, 1909, bypassing [[lieutenant (junior grade)]]. In October 1909, he was detailed to escort visiting [[Imperial Japanese Navy]] Captain [[Isamu Takeshita|Takeshita Isamu]], commanding the [[armored cruiser]] [[Japanese cruiser Izumo|''Izumo'']], that had set out from [[Sasebo, Japan]] on September 20, 1909, to participate in the [[Portola Festival]] at [[San Francisco, California|San Francisco]]. [http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn85066387/1909-10-13/ed-1/seq-16/;words=Lieutenant+Reed?date1=1908&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1909&proxtext=LIEUTENANT+REED&y=6&x=12&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=12 The San Francisco Call, October 13, 1909] [https://books.google.com/books?id=QJAmAQAAIAAJ&pg=RA6-PA12&lpg=RA6-PA12&dq=portola+festival+uss+albany&source=bl&ots=jKY8_OiDpt&sig=QeUkwgQ6Nx-x_UJ6UMbVg9lU9d0&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwiylubmw7fOAhVQ7GMKHeohAS0Q6AEIPzAG#v=onepage&q=portola%20festival%20uss%20albany&f=false Our Navy, the Standard Publication of the US Navy, Vol. 3, 1909]The previous month, ''Albany'' and another cruiser had visited [[Santa Monica, California]] as part of dedication ceremonies for the new [[Santa Monica Pier|Santa Monica Municipal Pier]] which coincided with [[California Admission Day]]. Visitors toured the [[warships]] and four companies of bluejackets, two from each cruiser, marched in a parade from the [[Santa Monica, California]] city hall to the new pier.[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn85042462/1909-09-10/ed-1/seq-8/;words=cruisers+Albany+cruiser?date1=1908&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1909&proxtext=cruiser+albany&y=7&x=15&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=9 The Los Angeles Herald, September 10, 1909] ===Mare island=== When ''Albany'' returned to Mare Island on April 28, 1910, following patrol duties in Mexican and Nicaraguan waters [http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn85066387/1910-04-29/ed-1/seq-9/;words=ALBANY+Nicaragua?date1=1910&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1910&proxtext=albany+nicaragua&y=4&x=21&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=1 The San Francisco Call, April 29, 1910] Reed detached from the cruiser and assumed shore duty at Mare Island, where he served from May 4, 1910 to June 25, 1912 as assistant inspection officer.[https://books.google.com/books?id=cJNaAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA244&lpg=PA244&dq=navy+register+1910&source=bl&ots=i4vzOxvscp&sig=amx0CLYMkJeszbKxNXy6flXBHYQ&hl=en&sa=X&ei=MtNHUJmbMMTbigKttYHwBA&sqi=2&ved=0CDwQ6AEwAQ#v=onepage&q=%22245%20lieutenant%20allen%20b.%20reed&f=false Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1911, p. 244-245] While at Mare Island, Reed had temporary duty assignments from March 8–11, 1911 on {{USS|Yorktown|PG-1|6}} and on {{USS|Cincinnati|C-7|}} from October 7–11, 1911 as [[engineering officer]]. As assistant inspection officer, he was a member of the [[Naval Board of Inquiry]] that convened to investigate the publicized fraud scandal involving naval [[Paymaster]] Arthur M. Pippin. Pippin, his pay clerk and chief yeoman on the receiving ship {{USS|Independence|1814|6}}, among other schemes, were suspected of withholding and embezzling uniforms bought by recruits and selling these and other supplies to civilian merchants.[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn82015415/1911-05-03/ed-1/seq-9/;words=Arthur+M+Pippin?date1=1836&sort=relevance&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1922&proxtext=%22arthur+m.+pippin%22&y=9&x=14&dateFilterType=yearRange&page=3&page=1&index=3 The Hawaiian Star, May 3, 1911, p. 1][http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn85066387/1911-04-21/ed-1/seq-5/;words=Arthur+M+Pippin?date1=1836&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1922&proxtext=%22arthur+m.+pippin%22&y=9&x=14&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=16 The San Francisco Call, April 21, 1911, p. 5][http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn85066387/1911-04-25/ed-1/seq-5/;words=Arthur+M+Pippin?date1=1836&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1922&proxtext=%22arthur+m.+pippin%22&y=9&x=14&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=18 The San Francisco Call, April 25, 1911, p. 5] The board of inquiry found cause to court-martial Pippin and his two associates. They were ultimately convicted and Pippin and his pay clerk received sentences of seven and three years respectively, [[hard labor]] at [[San Quentin]], "the severest punishment ordered by a naval court-martial in many years."[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn84026749/1911-11-03/ed-1/seq-1/;words=Arthur+M+Pippin?date1=1836&sort=relevance&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1922&proxtext=%22arthur+m.+pippin%22&y=9&x=14&dateFilterType=yearRange&page=3&page=1&index=5 The Washington Times, November 3, 1911, p. 1][http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn83030214/1911-11-04/ed-1/seq-10/;words=Arthur+M+Pippin?date1=1836&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1922&proxtext=%22arthur+m.+pippin%22&y=9&x=14&dateFilterType=yearRange&page=2&index=18 The New York Daily Tribune, November 4, 1911] ===Nicaragua=== [[File:USS Denver Nicaragua Landing Force 1912.jpg|thumb|right|200px|USS ''Denver'' ship's landing force under Lt. A. B. Reed rests beside the Corinto, Nicaragua railroad line, 1912]] During the [[United States occupation of Nicaragua]], on June 25, 1912, Lieutenant Reed was detached from Mare Island to the protected cruiser, {{USS|Denver|CL-16|}}, as executive officer.[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn84026749/1912-06-26/ed-1/seq-8/;words=Mare+Denver+Island?date1=1912&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1912&proxtext=denver+mare+island&y=0&x=0&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=11 The Washington Times, June 26, 1912] On July 9, his orders were changed to duty as [[navigator]].[https://books.google.com/books?id=W1YuAQAAIAAJ&pg=PA31&lpg=PA31&dq=uss+denver+reed&source=bl&ots=mnPsgvohEL&sig=FozC3MGsZWWEPiE6J2OF4mZSyYs&hl=en&sa=X&ei=FyZIULPCK6K9iwKz_4HgDw&ved=0CDAQ6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=uss%20denver%20reed&f=false Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1913, pp. 31, 62] ''Denver'' was recommissioned on July 25, 1912 at Mare Island for service with the Pacific Fleet cruising the west coast from San Francisco to [[Central America]] to protect American citizens and property. On August 29, 1912, a landing force of 120 men under the command of Lt. Reed was landed from ''Denver'' at [[Corinto, Nicaragua|Corinto]] to protect the railway line running from Corinto to Managua and then south to Granada on the north shore of Lake Nicaragua. This landing party reembarked aboard ship October 24 and 25th, 1912.[http://www.history.navy.mil/research/library/online-reading-room/title-list-alphabetically/l/list-of-expeditions-1901-1929.html List of Expeditions 1901-1929, Navy Department Library, Navy History & Heritage Command] ===Pacific Torpedo Flotilla=== Reed's next assignment after being detached from ''Denver'' on May 23, 1913 at San Diego, was to the Pacific Torpedo Flotilla tender, {{USS|Iris|1885|6}}, as executive officer and navigator.[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn84026749/1913-05-26/ed-1/seq-12/;words=A+Iris+B+REED?date1=1912&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1915&proxtext=%22a.b.+reed%22+iris&y=8&x=15&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=0 The Washington Times, May 26, 1913][https://books.google.com/books?id=ih8PAQAAMAAJ&pg=PA256137&lpg=PA256&dq=january+1,+1911+register+of+the+commissioned+and+warrant+navy&source=bl&ots=7lGgvFMIdM&sig=To8Eq6yH87zUiKRLl1NA6qR4KeM&hl=en&sa=X&ei=NB5IUJ3MGeHxiwKjnoG4AQ&sqi=2&ved=0CDsQ6AEwAg#v=onepage&q&f=false Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1914, p. 256] One of his crew, John Samuels, a 21-year old sailor who had recently "shipped over for another hitch" (re-enlisted) was heir to a $2 million estate and the richest enlisted man in the navy, but preferred the sea and the $20 a month he would earn for the next four years to the "joys of living ashore".[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn82014682/1913-05-22/ed-1/seq-2/;words=Iris+San+Diego?date1=1913&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1915&proxtext=iris+san+diego&y=0&x=0&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=4 Honolulu Star Bulletin, May 22, 1913] ''Iris'' was based at San Diego and serviced the Pacific Fleet torpedo ships and [[submarines]]. ===Panama=== Reed detached from ''Iris'' on June 3, 1915. He had requested transfer to the recently opened [[Panama Canal]] (1914) where he served from July 8, 1915 to August 1, 1917 as only the second [[Captain of the Port]] at [[Balboa, Panama|Balboa]], the Pacific canal terminus, replacing Commander Henry V. Butler.[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn82014682/1915-07-10/ed-1/seq-11/;words=flotilla+torpedo+Iris?date1=1913&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1915&proxtext=torpedo+flotilla+iris&y=9&x=11&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=0 Honolulu Star Bulletin, July 10, 1915][http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn84026749/1915-06-11/ed-1/seq-6/;words=A+B+REED?date1=1913&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1915&proxtext= The Washington Times, June 11, 1915][https://books.google.com/books?id=JMkcAQAAIAAJ&pg=PA334&lpg=PA334&dq=a.+b.+reed+register+of+commissioned+and+warrant+officers&source=bl&ots=ZF0KLV8uRf&sig=poVE1DjIXYiaeLnT9Rq30QabhSY&hl=en#v=onepage&q=a.%20b.%20reed%20register%20of%20commissioned%20and%20warrant%20officers&f=false Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the Navy of the United States and Marine Corps, January 1917, p. 334] While serving as Port Captain at Balboa, Reed was promoted to [[lieutenant commander]] on August 29, 1916. ===World War I=== Temporarily promoted to [[commander]] on February 1, 1918[https://books.google.com/books?id=qkjmAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA439&lpg=PA439&dq=%22allen+b.+reed%22+navy&source=bl&ots=7yvnuWyltz&sig=hLKOjjCJmiQNFxaFIeV8Vo7o3qI&hl=en&sa=X&ei=sNzpTuGTBeWksQKalI2sCQ&sqi=2&ved=0CEQQ6AEwBQ#v=onepage&q=%22allen%20b.%20reed%22%20navy&f=false The Official U.S. Bulletin, December 26, 1917, p.7] during [[World War I]], Reed served first as [[executive officer]] and later as [[commanding officer]] of the {{USS|Susquehanna|ID-3016|}}, the former German liner, ''SS Rhein'', a [[troop transport]] that made seven transatlantic crossings carrying over 18,000 American troops to Europe and bringing over 15,000 troops home after the [[Armistice]]. Reed was awarded the [[Navy Cross (United States)|Navy Cross]] in 1920 "for distinguished service in the line of his profession as commanding officer of the {{USS|Susquehanna|ID-3016|6}}, engaged in the important, exacting and hazardous duty of transporting and escorting troops and supplies to European ports through waters infested with enemy submarines and mines."[http://valor.militarytimes.com/recipient.php?recipientid=9961 Military Times Hall of Valor, Navy Cross Recipients] ===Washington D.C.=== Following World War I, he was assigned to the [[Bureau of Steam Engineering]] in [[Washington, D.C.]] and permanently promoted to commander on June 3, 1921. In 1923-24 he served as executive officer on the battleship {{USS| Florida|BB-30}}. From June 1924 through July 1925, he was assigned to [[United States Naval Forces Europe]] and commanded the [[Clemson-class destroyer]] {{USS|Worden|DD-288}} which visited numerous ports in the [[Mediterranean Ocean]] before returning to the [[New York Naval Yard]] in July 1925. Thereafter, Reed was assigned to matriculate at the [[Naval War College]] in [[Newport, Rhode Island]] for a year. He was assigned to the Ships' Movement Division of the office of the [[Chief of Naval Operations]] in Washington, D.C. from June 1926 through July 1928 and promoted to [[captain (naval)|captain]] on March 16, 1927. From August 1928 through June 1929 he commanded Division 30, Destroyer Squadron 11, [[Battle Fleet]] from the [[flagship]] {{USS|McCawley|DD-276}} home-port [[Puget Sound Navy Yard]] in [[Bremerton, Washington]] and from July 1929 through May 1930 he commanded Division 45, Destroyer Squadron 11 from the {{USS|Preble|DD-345}} home-port of [[Naval Base San Diego]]. Returning to Washington, D.C. in June 1930, he was assigned to the [[Office of Naval Material|Material Division]] of the office of the Chief of Naval Operations. ===USS New Orleans=== The {{USS| New Orleans|CA-32}} was launched on April 12, 1933 at the [[Brooklyn Navy Yard]], with Reed selected as the first commanding officer of the 876-man [[heavy cruiser]] while the ship was fitting out in 1933. ''Orleans'' was a lead ship in a class of seven "treaty cruisers", that collectively saw extensive service in all major engagements in the [[Pacific Ocean theater of World War II|Pacific theater]] during [[World War II]]. ''New Orleans'' was commissioned on February 15, 1934 at the Brooklyn Navy Yard. Under Captain Reed's command that ended on August 30, 1935, USS ''New Orleans'' made a [[shakedown cruise|shakedown]] [[Transatlantic crossing]] to [[Northern Europe]] and [[Scandinavia]] in May and June 1934, returning to New York on June 28, 1934. On July 5, ''New Orleans'' sailed to rendezvous with {{USS|Houston|CA-30}}, carrying President [[Franklin Delano Roosevelt]], for a nearly 12,000 nmi. cruise through the Panama Canal to Hawaii and an exercise with the United States Airship Macon and her aircraft off the California coast. Among ''New Orlean'''s [[junior officer]] [[plankowner]]s under Captain Reed's command in 1934 were Ensign E.L. Jahncke, Jr., son of the Assistant Secretary of the Navy, and Ensign T.H. Moorer, who as Admiral [[Thomas H. Moorer]] was [[Chief of Naval Operations]] (CNO) from 1967-1970 and [[Chairman of the Joint Chiefs]] of Staff from 1970-1974. ===World War II=== [[File:Captain A.B. Reed, U.S. Navy Retired, liaison officer, Navy, and Office of Production Management.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Captain A.B. Reed, U.S. Navy Ret., liaison officer, Navy, and Office of Production Management, circa 1940]] After his command of USS ''New Orleans'' ended, Captain Reed was ordered back to Washington to serve at the Office of the Chief of Naval Operations as Director of Fleet Maintenance Division[https://archive.org/stream/officialregister38unit#page/54/mode/2up Official Register of the United States, 1938] and on the Joint Economy Board between the Army and Navy[https://archive.org/stream/officialregister1937unit#page/154/mode/2up Official Register of the United States, 1937] Later, he was appointed [[Chairman of the Board|Chairman]] of the [[Committee|Executive Committee]] of the Army and Navy Munitions Board until 1939. Transferred from the active to retired list on June 30, 1939, he nevertheless continued on active duty for two more years. In 1940 and 1941 he was assistant to the Chairman of the [[U.S. Maritime Commission]], [[Admiral]] [[Emory S. Land]] and Navy [[Liaison officer|liaison]] to the Office of Production Management of the [[Council of National Defense]]. After 37 years of service and the attack on Pearl Harbor still three months in the future, Captain Reed transferred to the inactive list on September 5, 1941. During World War II, he worked as an executive with shipbuilding firms in New York, Texas and South Carolina, before retiring in 1946. On September 6, 1944, his wife, Bess M. Reed, sponsored the {{USS|Torsk|SS-423|}} at its launch from the Portsmouth (Maine) Navy Yard.[http://www.usstorsk.org/photoalbum/44launch/tpic441.htm USS Torsk.org website] ==Personal life== Lieutenant Reed married the former Bessie Moorhead of [[Omaha, Nebraska]] on September 25, 1909 at the home of her parents in Omaha and returned to San Francisco on October 10.[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn85066387/1909-10-08/ed-1/seq-6/;words=Lieutenant+Reed?date1=1908&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1909&proxtext=LIEUTENANT+REED&y=10&x=11&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=16 The San Francisco Call, October 8, 1909][http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn99021999/1914-08-26/ed-1/seq-6/;words=Allen+Reed+Lieutenant?date1=1913&rows=20&searchType=basic&state=&date2=1915&proxtext=Lieutenant+Allen+Reed&y=10&x=13&dateFilterType=yearRange&index=0 Omaha Daily Bee, August 26, 1914] The Reeds had a son and three daughters. Allen B. Reed, Jr. (1912-1996) graduated from Annapolis in 1935 and retired more than twenty years later as a captain. During his retirement, Captain Reed was a resident of Washington, DC and lived on Woodley Rd. NW where he and his wife lived from 1930 until his death in 1965.
