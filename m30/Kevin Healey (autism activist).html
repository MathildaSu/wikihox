This campaign attracted support from both members of Parliament and the public, and Healey delivered a petition to the Prime Minister's office signed by 4,000 people in favour of such expanded services and funding.{{cite news|last1=Walker|first1=Adam|title=Staffordshire autism campaign to meet PM in National Autistic Society, which is the largest autism charity in the UK.{{cite web|title=Board of This bullying included not only online harassment and cloning (impersonation) of his Twitter account, which had over 100,000 followers as of June 2015, but also threats to "sever [his] legs" or kill him. Taking into consideration the information that some of those bullying him had learned what neighbourhood he lived in, Healey found those threats credible enough that he did not leave his home for three months. His experiences motivated him to raise awareness about cyberbullying and online harassment of those with autism.{{cite news|last1=Worgan|first1=Jo|title=Kevin Healey and his Anti Bullying Autism Campaign|url=http://www.autismdailynewscast.com/kevin-healey-and-his-anti-bullying-autism-campaign/6427/joworgan/|accessdate=6 November 2015|work=Autism Daily Newscast|date=2014-01-18|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150206044156/http://www.autismdailynewscast.com/kevin-healey-and-his-anti-bullying-autism-campaign/6427/joworgan/|archivedate=2015-02-06}} Healey advocates for making the bullying of a person with a developmental disability a crime; as of 2015 bullying is not a crime under UK law, leaving victims little recourse. ==Recognition== Healey has won awards including Stoke-On-Trent Citizen of the Year in 2006 and Charity Champion of the year in 2007. He was selected to carry the Olympic torch in Crewe on May 31, 2012. A charity Healey founded, Staffordshire Adults Autistic Society (SAAS), received the Queen's Award for Voluntary Service in 2012. As one of several representatives of SAAS, he was invited to a royal garden party on May 22, 2012; at the party, Healey met and spoke with the Queen of England about autism and his work with SAAS.{{cite web|title=Autism Charity Receives the Queen's Award for Voluntary Service During Her Diamond Jubilee Year and Meets Her Majesty at Royal Garden Party|url=http://www.saas.uk.com/autism-news/autism-charity-receives-the-queen-rsquo-s-award-for-voluntary-service-during-her-diamond-jubilee-year-and-meets-her-majesty-at-royal-garden-party-225.php|website=Staffordshire Adults Autistic Society|accessdate=2015-11-06}} ==Personal life== Healey was diagnosed with autism relatively late, at the age of 27.
