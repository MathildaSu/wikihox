xml:space="preserve" bytes="4196"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Klaus Huhn|timestamp=20150718060712|year=2015|month=July|day=18|substed=yes|help=off}} {{notability|date=July 2015}} {{BLP sources|date=July 2015}} [[File:Klaus Huhn bei einer Buchlesung 2011.JPG|thumb|right|300px|Huhn at a book-reading in 2011]] '''Klaus Huhn''' (born 24 February 1928) is a sports journalist and writer, and a former sports administrator.{{cite web|url=http://www.bundesstiftung-aufarbeitung.de/wer-war-wer-in-der-ddr-%2363%3B-1424.html?ID=3603|author1= Klaus Gallinat |author2= |title=Ullrich, Klaus (eigtl.: Klaus Huhn) * 24.2.1928 Sportjournalist|publisher= Bundesstiftung zur Aufarbeitung der SED-Diktatur: Biographische Datenbanken |language= German|accessdate= 15 July 2015}} Huhn worked for the [[Germany|East German]] mass-market daily newspaper, ''[[Neues Deutschland]]'', and was chairman of the Sports Journalists Sub-Association within that country's important [[GDR Union of Journalists|Union of Journalists]]. As a writer he concentrates on the great names from the [[German Democratic Republic|German Democratic Republic (GDR)]]'s sporting history, and writes largely for the "GDR nostaligia" readership.[http://www.focus.de/magazin/archiv/ddr-journalist-klaus-huhn-war-stasi-spitzel_aid_154495.html ''Journalist Klaus Huhn war Stasi-Spitzel.''] In: ''Focus Magazin.'' Nr. 45 (1995), 6. November 1995. He has written several books about the cycling legend [[Gustav-Adolf Schur]], and was employed as the [[ghost writer]] for Schur's autobiography.{{cite web|url=http://www.faz.net/aktuell/feuilleton/buecher/rezension-sachbuch-held-der-beinarbeit-11269834.html|title=Held der Beinarbeit ..... Heilige Sattelzeiten: Gustav-Adolf Schur radelt in seiner Autobiographie über Hölzchen und Stöckchen |author=Michael Reinsch |publisher=[[Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung]] (online)|date=20 March 2001|accessdate=15 July 2015}} The book's objectivity was questioned by one reviewer who described it as as "shameless propaganda". ==Life== Huhn was born, into a [[Communism|Communist]] family, in [[Berlin]] where his father Angest was a clerical worker. Huhn attended [[Gymnasium (Germany)|secondary school]] in Berlin and in [[Saalfeld]]. In 1946 he joined the Deutsche Volkszeitung, the central organ of the [[Communist Party of Germany]] (KPD) in the Soviet occupation zone. .Kristen Benning (1997), ''Die Geschichte des SED-Zentralorgans Neues Deutschland von 1946 bis 1949. Die Waffenlieferanten "im Kampfe gegen die Reaktion und ihre Verwirrungsmanöver"'', Münster: LIT Verlag In 1954 he took a correspondence course in journalism at the [[Leipzig University|Karl Marx University]] in [[Leipzig]] and in 1983 he was at the German College of Physical Culture, aslo in Leipzig, with a doctorate in pedagogy. In 1995 it was claimed, by the German weekly magazine ''[[Focus (German magazine)|Focus]]'' that Kuhn had been a member of the secret police the [[Stasi]]. The magazine claimed that Kuhn had joined the organisation in 1960 taking the code name "Heinz Mohr" and that he received gifts, on the 60th birthday, for his work with the Stasi. ==Further reading== * Hanns Joachim Friedrichs (1994), ''Journalists Lives'', Munich, pp 149 et seq. * Klaus Huhnu nchicken (2011), "Auch dem Papst half ich mal aus der Klemme.
