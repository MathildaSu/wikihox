AV Suraweera,{{cite web|title=A.V. Suraweera, 1930-|url=https://www.loc.gov/acq/ovop/delhi/salrp/avsuraweera.html}} who was a scholar writer, the Director General of the Central Cultural Fund{{cite web|title= Central Cultural Fund|url=http://www.ccf.lk/}} and was earlier Deputy Minister of Culture and Religious Affairs of Sri Lanka as a project planned to launch a governmental funded project to keep the ancient and traditional textual applications safer against the modern non-literal gossipy trends.{{cite web|title=Elu langauge and ancient Sri Lanka|url=http://www.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~lkawgw/elu.html}} But that plan was not fully implemented instead a range of cultural centres was established. In 2006, the project was re-organized as a part of an Eastern Languages Promotion Programme. ==Functions== Currently the organization has archived physically 132 unique Sinhala gramophone text documents, some of them back dated to 1929 published under the lable of "[[His Master's Voice]]" (HMV). However they are not yet published as electronic public domain texts. An instant document is available online named Thilona Sesi Muni Gauthama Swami. All the printed texts are used for educational purposes only. ==References== {{reflist}} [[Category:Non-governmental organizations| ]]
