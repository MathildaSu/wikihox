#WWEDraft|date=July 19, 2016|accessdate=July 20, 2016}} Slater appeared on the July 26 episode of ''SmackDown'', cutting a highly acclaimed [[Glossary of professional wrestling terms#Pipe bomb|worked shoot promo]] addressing the issue, referring to himself as "the hottest [[free agent]] in sports entertainment".{{cite web|title=7/26 SMACKDOWN INSTANT REACTION – Three-Pack of Thoughts on Smackdown Live Week 1 from PWTorch Staff|url=http://www.pwtorch.com/site/2016/07/26/726-smackdown-instant-reaction-three-pack-thoughts-smackdown-live-week-1-pwtorch-staff/|website=Pro Wrestling Torch|accessdate=27 July 2016}}{{cite web|title=5 Reasons Why Smackdown Was Better Than Raw|url=http://www.forbes.com/sites/brianmazique/2016/07/26/5-reasons-why-smackdown-was-better-than-raw/#cd3a5d85e878|publisher=''[[Forbes]]''|accessdate=27 July 2016}} only to receive a ''[[Professional wrestling attacks#Striking spear|Gore]]'' from a returning [[Rhino (wrestler)|Rhyno]] (also a [[kayfabe]] free agent) shortly after.{{cite web|title=WWE News: American Alpha’s SD Debut Set, Slater Still a Free Agent|url=http://411mania.com/wrestling/wwe-news-american-alphas-sd-debut-set-slater-still-a-free-agent/|website=411mania.com|accessdate=July 27, 2016}} On the August 9 episode of ''SmackDown'', Slater failed to win a SmackDown contract when he lost against Rhyno. Also at the draft, the [[WWE Women's Championship|WWE Women's Champion]] and [[WWE Tag Team Championship|WWE Tag Team Champions]] were drafted to ''Raw'' leaving ''SmackDown'' without a championship for the divisions. Subsequently, the [[WWE SmackDown Women's Championship|SmackDown Women's Championship]] and [[WWE SmackDown Tag Team Championship|SmackDown Tag Team Championships]] were announced and Slater asked for a title shot. A stipulation was added to the tournament that if Slater could find a partner and win the tournament for the inaugural tag team championship, Slater would be offered a ''SmackDown'' contract. Rhyno agreed to be his partner, Slater and Rhyno proceeded to win their quarterfinal match over the returning [[The Headbangers]] ([[Mosh (wrestler)|Mosh]] and [[Thrasher (wrestler)|Thrasher]]), their semi-final over [[The Hype Bros]] ([[Zack Ryder]] and [[Mojo Rawley]]), and the finals at [[Backlash (2016)|Backlash]] over [[The Usos]] (Jimmy and Jey), becoming the inaugural champions.{{cite web|last1=Burdick|first1=Michael|title=Heath Slater & Rhyno def.
