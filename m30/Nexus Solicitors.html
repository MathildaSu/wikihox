xml:space="preserve" bytes="6915"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Nexus Solicitors|timestamp=20140128234238|year=2014|month=January|day=28|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Multiple issues |advert = November 2011 |refimprove = November 2011 |orphan = April 2010 |notability = November 2011 }} '''Nexus Solicitors''' is full-service [[law firm]] based in the [[North West England]]. It offers services in the areas of [[corporate finance]], commercial, property, employment, insolvency, dispute resolution, [[intellectual property]] and media and [[entertainment law]]. Nexus has offices in [[Manchester]] and [[Knutsford]]. It has over 40 employees, including 9 partners and 24 other lawyers. ==Introduction== Nexus’ main office is located in [[Albert Square, Manchester]] having a prime position over-looking Manchester’s Town Hall. It also has an office based in [[Knutsford]] which mainly deals with private client matters. The firm is recognised by Legal 500 as a tier 4 firm in relation to the areas of insolvency, employment, [[intellectual property]], sport and media.{{cite web|url=http://www.legal500.com/firms/4928-nexus-solicitors/offices/8948-manchester |title=> Nexus Solicitors > Manchester, ENGLAND > What we say |publisher=The Legal 500 |date=2011-09-21 |accessdate=2011-11-14}} Nexus Solicitors also has a sister firm, Westwood Solicitors. Westwood Solicitors are specialist personal injury lawyers and are located at the same premises. ==History of the firm== Nexus Solicitors was founded in 2000. The 5 founding partners of the firm were Tony Brook and Christopher Pugh, previously litigators at Berrymans Lace Mawer; Jamie Lloyd and Steve Isherwood, previously corporate practitioners at Beachcroft Wansbroughs; and Desmond O'Driscoll previously from Manchester firm Turner Parkinson. The aim was to create a small but full-service firm acting for owner-managed businesses across the North West.{{cite web|author=18 September 2006 |url=http://www.thelawyer.com/nexus-solicitors/121939.article |title=Nexus Solicitors | Profiles |publisher=The Lawyer |date=2006-09-18 |accessdate=2011-11-14}} Four of the founding partners remain at Nexus, Isherwood having departed in 2004. In 2005 Nexus’ turnover grew from £1.9 million to £2.44 million, an increase of 28 percent.{{cite web|author=Kevin Feddy |url=http://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/news/business/s/170/170823_nexus_to_expand.html |title=Nexus to expand | Manchester Evening News - menmedia.co.uk |publisher=Manchester Evening News |date=2005-08-23 |accessdate=2011-11-14}} In 2006 it was reported the firm’s annual turnover was circa £2.7 million and was continuing to grow. In June 2007, Nexus doubled the size of its office space in Albert Square in order to accommodate growing staff numbers.{{cite web|author=12 November 2007 |url=http://www.thelawyer.com/hill-dickinson-team-defects-to-nexus/129871.article |title=Hill Dickinson team defects to Nexus | News |publisher=The Lawyer |date=2007-11-12 |accessdate=2011-11-14}} In November 2007, Nexus recruited the [[Hill Dickinson]] Corporate and Insolvency restructing team led by Andrew Tonge. Nexus opened its [[Knutsford]] office in 2008, which is headed up by Graham Walker, a former partner at Horwich Farrelly. Walker is best known for a ground-breaking divorce case in 2001 which established the principle that, in long-standing marriages, the partner’s business interests as well as their personal finances, should be taken into account in determining settlements.http://www.crainsmanchesterbusiness.co.uk/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20080805/FREE/281234011/1071/breaking/-/-/nexus-picks-divorce-lawyer-to-head-up-knutsford-office Growth in the last 10 years has seen the firm triple in size with a headcount of over 40 including 9 partners. Nexus continues to recruit and appointed Jessica Enderby head of non-contentious IP in September 2009.{{cite web|url=http://www.lawgazette.co.uk/node/52702 |title=New associate: Jessica Enderby at Nexus Solicitors |publisher=The Law Gazette |date=2009-10-13 |accessdate=2011-11-14}} ==Main practice areas== Corporate and commercial, business recovery and insolvency, Landlord & Tenant, dispute resolution, employment, [[intellectual property]], IT and telecommunications, media and entertainment, real estate development. ==Main industry sectors== Nexus' core clients include national and international retailers, owner-managed businesses, manufacturers, many Banks and Private Equity corporations, global investors, and other companies such as venue management giant SMG, who operate the [[Manchester Evening News Arena]]. ==Recent matters== Nexus’ acted for [[David Sullivan (publisher)|David Sullivan]], [[David Gold (businessman)|David Gold]] and Ralph Gold in the £50m sale of red-top newspapers [[the Daily Sport]] and [[Sunday Sport]] in 2007.{{cite web|author=8 August 2007 |url=http://www.thelawyer.com/finers-advises-mobile-porn-kings-on-sport-acquisition/127765.article |title=Finers advises mobile porn kings on Sport acquisition | News |publisher=The Lawyer |date=2007-08-08 |accessdate=2011-11-14}} Nexus acted for [[Michael Appleton]], ex [[Manchester United]] footballer, in his claim which resulted in an award of £1.5 million being made for the loss of his career.{{cite web|author=Mike Slingsby |url=http://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/news/s/1002/1002735_appleton_wins_damages.html |title=Appleton wins damages | Manchester Evening News - menmedia.co.uk |publisher=Manchester Evening News |date=2007-03-23 |accessdate=2011-11-14}} Nexus advised on the £5m sale of Multidesign Holdings Limited to [[Mott MacDonald]] Group.
