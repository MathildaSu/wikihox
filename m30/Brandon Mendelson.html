Mendelson is an advocate for breast cancer awareness, public education, and the [[United States Armed Forces|American Armed Forces]]. He is also the author of the book, ''Social Media Is Bullshit'', published by [[St. Martin's Press]] on September 4th, 2012.{{cite web|last=Glaser |first=Mark |url=http://www.pbs.org/mediashift/2012/09/mediatwits-56-iphone-5-not-insanely-great-social-media-is-b-s-258 |title=Mediatwits #56: iPhone 5 Not Insanely Great; Social Media Is B.S. |work= Mediashift |publisher=PBS |date=2012-09-14 |accessdate=2013-07-22}} == Early life == Brandon Mendelson was born on April 25th, 1983 in [[Oyster Bay, New York]].{{cite web|url=http://socialmediaisbs.com/about-the-author-of-social-media-bs-bj-mendelson/ |title=About |publisher=Social Media Is BS |date= |accessdate=2013-07-22}} He is the son of Richard and Paula (''[[née]]'' Cohen) Mendelson. Mendelson transferred to the [[State University of New York at Potsdam]] (SUNY) in 2004 and graduated in 2006 with a [[Bachelor's degree]] in Political Science and a minor in Pre-Law. At Potsdam, he established the Oscar Cohen Memorial Scholarship, which was named in honor of his grandfather. Mendelson met Amanda Morin at SUNY Potsdam. They were married on June 6th, 2008."[[Recession may set mood for baby bust]]". Los Angeles Times. 2008-12-10. Retrieved 2013-07-22. B.J. and Amanda moved to South Glens Falls, where they lived with Amanda's parents. In the Summer of 2012, the couple divorced. == Career == === Advocacy === In 2008, Mendelson established a [[Twitter]] account to bring awareness to the fight against breast cancer. He was drawn to this issue through the health crises of both of his grandmothers.{{cite web|url=http://www.crunchbase.com/person/b-j-mendelson |title=B.J. Mendelson | CrunchBase Profile |publisher=Crunchbase.com |date= |accessdate=2013-07-22}} His account was then placed on the [[Twitter]] Suggested User List in February 2009. When Twitter and Mendelson began promoting the "Kicking Breast Cancer's Butt Across America Tour" on his Twitter account, it resulted in an increase of approximately 5,000 followers a day.{{cite web|author= De La Cruz, Ralph |url=http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/2009-02-12/news/0902110586_1_breast-cancer-tour-web-sites |title=Couple's trip to raise cancer awareness is also a virtual journey |publisher=Sun Sentinel |date=2009-02-12 |accessdate=2013-07-22}} He offered to sell his spot on the Suggested User List to entrepreneur [[Jason Calacanis]] in exchange for the $250,000 Calacanis offered Twitter for a spot on the list. The exchange between Calacanis and Mendelson was blocked by Twitter co-founders [[Ev Williams]] and [[Biz Stone]]. The funds would have been donated to the 1 in 8 Foundation, with whom Mendelson organized the national tour to bring awareness to the fight against breast cancer.{{cite web|author=Fuggetta, Emily |url=http://www.alligator.org/news/local/article_c7a25aa0-ef7a-582e-8543-611479d196e2.html |title=Nonprofit organization raises breast cancer awareness|publisher=The Independent Florida Alligator |date=2009-02-10 |accessdate=2013-07-22}}{{cite web |url=http://techcrunch.com/2009/03/20/hey-twitter-heres-a-way-to-call-jasons-bluff-and-maybe-fight-breast-cancer |title=Hey Twitter, Here's A Way To Call Jason's Bluff And Maybe Fight Breast Cancer |publisher=TechCrunch |date=2009-03-20 |accessdate=2013-07-22}}{{cite web|last=Briggs |first=Bill |url=http://www.nbcnews.com/id/27668152/ |title=Digital gold: Turning techno-junk into cash |publisher=NBC News |date=2008-11-13 |accessdate=2013-07-22}} He considered the national breast cancer awareness campaign and tour unsuccessful, attributing the failure to his initial marketing efforts that relied on social networking.
