xml:space="preserve" bytes="3154"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Jim Tocco|timestamp=20140106045315|year=2014|month=January|day=6|substed=yes}} {{About|the baseball broadcaster|the concert pianist|James Tocco}} '''Jim Tocco''' (born September 3, 1976) is a former [[Minor League Baseball|minor-league]] [[baseball]] announcer who is now working in marketing. Tocco grew up in [[North Olmsted, Ohio]] and attended [[North Olmsted City School District, Cuyahoga County, Ohio|North Olmsted High School]] before moving on to [[Bowling Green State University]], where he graduated with a B.A. in Journalism in 1998. After stints with the [[Charleston RiverDogs|Charleston (SC) RiverDogs]], [[Wilmington Blue Rocks|Wilmington (DE) Blue Rocks]], [[West Virginia Power|Charleston (WV) Alley Cats]], and [[Lansing Lugnuts|Lansing (MI) Lugnuts]] he was most recently the announcer for the [[Montgomery Biscuits|Montgomery (AL) Biscuits]].{{cite web|title=Biscuits lose their voice |url=http://www.wncftv.com/sports/biscuits/27744144.html | date=2008-09-01 |accessdate=2009-02-15}} Tocco founded [[CalloftheGame.com]] in 2001 to serve as a medium for fellow broadcasting professionals to network within the industry {{cite web|title=Lugnuts tab Tocco as new "Voice of the Lugnuts" |url=http://www.lansinglugnuts.com/press_box/press_release_archive/12_19_02.htm |date=2002-12-19 |accessdate=2008-01-30 | author=Lansing Lugnuts}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}} In 2003 and 2004, Tocco served as a guest announcer for [[Major League Baseball|MLB's]] [[Montreal Expos]]. In he 2006 was named the [[Southern League (baseball)|Southern League]] Broadcaster of the year {{cite news|title=Jim Tocco named Southern League Radio Broadcaster of the Year |url=http://www.oursportscentral.com/services/releases/?id=3375122 |date=2006-09-07 |accessdate=2008-01-30 }} In addition to baseball, Tocco's on-air credits include [[Division I (NCAA)|Division I]] [[college football]], [[college basketball|basketball]], [[College hockey|hockey]] and [[College soccer|soccer]] as well as occasional work in the television and print media.{{cite web|title=Biscuits Baseball |url=http://www.biscuitsbaseball.com/radio.html |accessdate=2008-01-31}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}} ==See also== * [[List of Montreal Expos broadcasters]] ==References== {{Reflist}} {{Persondata | NAME = Tocco, Jim | ALTERNATIVE NAMES = | SHORT DESCRIPTION = American baseball announcer | DATE OF BIRTH = 1976-09-03 | PLACE OF BIRTH = | DATE OF DEATH = | PLACE OF DEATH = }} {{DEFAULTSORT:Tocco, Jim}} [[Category:Baseball announcers]] [[Category:1976 births]] [[Category:Living people]] [[Category:Bowling Green State University alumni]] [[Category:People from Cuyahoga County, Ohio]] [[Category:People from North Olmsted, Ohio]]
