xml:space="preserve" bytes="4687"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Ramchandra Siras|timestamp=20150312132656|year=2015|month=March|day=12|substed=yes|help=off}} '''Shrinivas Ramchandra Siras''' (1948 – April 8, 2010) was an [[India]]n [[linguist]] and [[author]]. He was a professor in Aligarh Muslim University in Marathi literature. The director Hansal Mehta’s next film [[Aligarh (film)|Aligarh]] which is yet to be released, is based on his life. == Life == After school in Nagpur Siras studied [[psychology]] and [[linguistics]] at [[Nagpur University]] in India. In 1985 he got his [[PhD]] in [[Marathi language|Marathi]] and a [[Master's degree|master]] in psychology. He finished university studies and became a professor at [[Aligarh Muslim University]] (AMU). As author he wrote several short stories in [[Marathi language]].[http://www.amu.ac.in/FShow.jsp?did=18&temp9=10791 AMUC: Ramchandra Siras] In 2002 he got the literary award by organisation ''Maharashtra Sahitya Parishad''.[http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/lucknow/Gay-prof-was-known-as-a-literary-genius/articleshow/5775959.cms Times of India:Gay prof was known as a literary genius] ===Suspension=== On February 9, 2010 Siras was suspended by AMU for “gross misconduct” after having been ambushed by a local TV channel's camera crew while having sex with a [[rickshaw]] puller. He subsequently won his justice case in [[Allahabad High Court]] on 1 April against his university and got his job as professor back.[http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/Aligarh-Muslim-University-professor-suspended-for-being-gay/articleshow/5585787.cms Times of India:Aligarh Muslim University professor suspended for being gay] The case was amplified by the involvement of students in the secretive taping and ambush of Siras and his lover. ===Death=== But on 7 April 2010, he died in his own apartment in Aligarh.[http://www.ndtv.com/news/cities/online-campaign-for-justice-in-gay-profs-death-19915.php NDTV:Online campaign for justice in gay profs death] Police suspected suicide,{{cite web|url = http://www.ndtv.com/news/india/gay-amu-professor-found-dead-suicide-suspected-19432.php|title = Gay AMU professor found dead, suicide suspected|date = April 7, 2010|publisher = NDTV}} and preliminary results from the autopsy showed traces of poison in his body.
