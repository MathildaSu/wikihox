xml:space="preserve" bytes="3030"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Doug Baldwin (writer)|timestamp=20150709063149|year=2015|month=July|day=9|substed=yes}} {{Notability|date=July 2015}} '''Doug Baldwin''' (born November 12, 1952) is a playwright, actor and copywriter living in [[Portland, Oregon]]. He began his stage career as an actor, with appearances in many New York theatres, including Playwrights Horizons and Manhattan Punch Line, where he acted in a well-reviewed production of ''Room Service''[http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=FB091FFC3E5C12728DDDA80894D9405B8084F1D3&scp=1&sq=Doug+Baldwin&st=p]. He has also appeared in feature films (among them ''[[Zero Effect]]'' and ''[[Music Within]]'') and television shows (''[[Leverage (TV series)|Leverage]]'',''[[Nowhere Man (TV series)|Nowhere Man]]'', ''[[Under Suspicion (TV series)|Under Suspicion]]'', etc.), as well as in a number of national TV commercials. His comedy ''Drawing Down Clio'' won the 1997 [[Oregon Book Award#2.4|Oregon Book Award]] for Drama.{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1310&dat=19971012&id=YNZWAAAAIBAJ&sjid=x-sDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4075,3131742&hl=en|title=Awards ceremony honors Oregon writers|date=October 12, 1997|publisher=''[[Eugene Register-Guard]]''|accessdate=July 9, 2015}} ''The Xenophobe'' was the recipient of a 2000 Oregon Literary Fellowship{{cite web|url=http://www.literary-arts.org/what-we-do/oba-home/fellowships/writers/|title=Past Recipients: Writers|publisher=literary-arts.org|accessdate=July 9, 2015}} and won First Place in the 2001 Writerâ€™s Digest Writing Competition for ''Wrestling with Charlotte''.{{cite web|url=http://www.writersdigest.com/writing-articles/by-writing-genre/science-fiction-fantasy/dying_to_win|title=Dying to win|date=March 11, 2008|publisher=writersdigest.com|accessdate=July 9, 2015}} ''Suicide Table'' is his most recent script, and deals with the theme of luck and the role it plays in the lives of two New York City firefighters vacationing in Reno.
