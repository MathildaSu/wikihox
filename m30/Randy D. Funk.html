Monson]] | start_date1 = {{start date|2013|04|06}}}} '''Randy Dennis Funk''' (born August 1, 1952) has been a [[general authority]] of [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]] (LDS Church) since 2013. He is a member of the [[Second Quorum of the Seventy]]. Funk was born in [[Logan, Utah]], to C. Dennis and Rebecca Funk. He grew up in [[Manti, Utah]]; [[Madison, Wisconsin]]; and [[Smithfield, Utah]]. Funk served an a full-time LDS Church [[missionary (LDS Church)|missionary]] in [[Indonesia]]. Following his mission, he was awarded a bachelor's degree in history from [[Utah State University]] in 1976 and a juris doctorate from the [[University of Utah]] in 1979. Funk married Andrea Clyde in 1976 in the [[Logan Utah Temple]]. They are the parents of six children. Funk spent his professional life as a partner at the [[Denver, Colorado]], law firm of [[Sherman & Howard]], with expertise in land use, public finance, and infrastructure and development. Funk has served in the LDS Church as an [[elder (Latter Day Saints)|elders]] [[quorum (Latter Day Saints)|quorum]] president, [[ward (LDS Church)|ward]] clerk, [[stake (Latter Day Saints)|stake]] [[high council (Latter Day Saints)|high councilor]], Scout leader, executive secretary to [[Denver Colorado Temple]] committee, [[bishop (Latter Day Saints)|bishop]], stake president, local [[Young Men (organization)|Young Men]] president, [[Sunday School (LDS Church)|Sunday School]] teacher, [[area seventy]], and [[mission president|president]] of the [[India]] [[Bangalore]] Mission (2010â€“13). On April 6, 2013, Funk was [[common consent|sustained]] by church members as a member of the [[Second Quorum of the Seventy]].
