Diamond, 2013]] '''James D. ("Jim") Diamond''' is an [[United States|American]] attorney and educator. == Early childhood == Diamond was born June 9, 1959 in [[Brooklyn]], [[New York City]] and grew up in [[Oceanside, New York]], attending [[Oceanside High School (New York)|Oceanside High School]]. == Criminal lawyer == Diamond is a criminal defense lawyer whose practice has included representation of clients in investigations and prosecutions by federal and state law enforcement agencies. He has handled a wide variety of criminal cases ranging from white collar cases to violent crime. Diamond is well known for his defense of the "Greenwich Nanny," Flora Canales,{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2003/06/05/nyregion/metro-briefing-connecticut-stamford-guilty-verdict-in-murder-trial.html |title=Metro Briefing | Connecticut: Stamford: Guilty Verdict In Murder Trial - New York Times |location=Greenwich (Conn) |publisher=Nytimes.com |date= |accessdate=2014-03-26}} and Joseph Kriz.{{cite web|url=http://foxct.com/2013/06/05/connecticut-attorney-talks-historic-ruling-in-fraud-case/ |title=Connecticut Attorney Talks Historic Ruling In Fraud Case | WTIC FOX CT |publisher=Foxct.com |date=2013-06-05 |accessdate=2014-03-26}} Kriz is the lawyer who was sentenced in 2013 by Judge [[Janet C. Hall]] in the United States District Court in New Haven for a multimillion dollar [[mortgage fraud]] scheme.{{cite web|last=Neil |first=Martha |url=http://www.abajournal.com/mobile/article/lawyer_gets_30_months_in_mortgage_scheme_that_involved_diverted_iolta_inter/ |title=Attorney gets 30 months in mortgage case that brought down 2 lawyers and a paralegal |publisher=Abajournal.com |date=2013-06-03 |accessdate=2014-03-26}} Diamond is admitted to practice law in the States of Connecticut, New York and Arizona along with numerous federal courts. Diamond also practices Indian Law and is admitted to practice in two Native American tribal courts: the [http://www.pycourts.org/ Pascua Yaqui Tribal Court] (Arizona) and the [http://www.mashantucket.com/tribalcourt.aspx Mashantucket Pequot Tribal Court] (Connecticut). He holds certification by the National Board of Legal Specialty Certification as a criminal trial specialist. Diamond was recognized by his peers and selected as a “Connecticut Superlawyer”{{cite web|url=http://www.superlawyers.com/ |title=superlawyers.com |publisher=superlawyers.com |date=2014-02-15 |accessdate=2014-03-26}} as appearing in Connecticut Magazine every year between 2007 and 2014.{{cite web|url=http://www.superlawyers.com/connecticut/lawyer/James-D-Diamond/935662e2-5276-4f2f-a9c2-35ebaf873851.html |title=James Diamond Lawyer in Stamford, Attorney CT |publisher=Super Lawyers |date= |accessdate=2014-03-26}} He currently practices with the Stamford, Connecticut law firm of Cacace, Tusch & Santagata.{{cite web|url=http://www.lawcts.com/Bio/JamesDiamond.asp |title=Attorney James Diamond, Cacace, Tusch & Santagata, Stamford, Connecticut |publisher=Lawcts.com |date= |accessdate=2014-03-26}} Diamond was a prosecutor (Assistant State's Attorney) in the Connecticut Division of Criminal Justice{{cite web|author=Division of Criminal Justice |url=http://www.ct.gov/csao/site/default.asp |title=Division of Criminal Justice |publisher=Ct.gov |date=2012-09-27 |accessdate=2014-03-26}} from 1988 to 1994. For one year he was assigned to the Judicial District of Litchfield, working under State's Attorney Frank Maco. For the remainder of his term he was assigned to the Judicial District of Danbury. Diamond is well known for early in his career facing off against noted criminal defense and civil rights lawyer, [[William Kunstler]]. Diamond was awarded "Man of The Year" by Mother's Against Drunk Driving in 1992 for his performance in a vehicular manslaughter prosecution. He is also known for successfully lobbying the Connecticut State Legislature for the passage of a law to enable state prosecutors to leave the prosecutor's office and practice law without violating the state's revolving door lobbying statute. The amendment was signed into law by Governor Lowell Weiker in May 1994.Nov. Sp. Sess. P.A. 94-1 == Advocate for government ethics == Diamond started his career as a government ethics advocate, serving as Executive Director of the N.Y. State chapter of the citizens’ lobbying group [http://www.commoncause.org/site/pp.asp?c=dkLNK1MQIwG&b=8281551 Common Cause] from 1981-1983. There, he championed campaign finance reform and openness in government.{{cite news|last=Barbanel |first=Josh |url=http://www.nytimes.com/1982/01/11/nyregion/an-elusive-carey-goal-fiscal-reports-by-aides.html |title=An Elusive Carey Goal - Fiscal Reports By Aides |location=New York State |publisher=Nytimes.Com |date=1982-01-11 |accessdate=2014-03-26}} Diamond successfully lobbied for an series of amendments to the New York State Open Meetings Law which was signed into law by Governor Hugh Carey in 1982.Chapter 73 of the Laws of 1982 Under Diamond's leadership Common Cause was an important part of the coalition that successfully lobbied for passage of the original New York State returnable deposit beverage law known as the "Bottle Bill,"{{cite web|url=http://www.dec.ny.gov/chemical/57687.html |title=Frequently Asked Questions About the Bottle Bill - NYS Dept. of Environmental Conservation |publisher=Dec.ny.gov |date= |accessdate=2014-03-26}} passed in June 1982.Chapter 200 of the Laws of 1982. == State and local government == Diamond was on the executive staff of the New York State Attorney General, [[Robert Abrams]] from 1985-1988. He drafted public policy papers and speeches for the Attorney General on such topics as community policing, ethics in governmentSee New York Times, May 2, 1986 http://www.nytimes.com/1986/05/02/nyregion/wachtler-outlines-plan-to-fight-court-sexism.html and racial tolerance.See Schenectady Gazette, May 2, 1987, http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1917&dat=19870502&id=eAwhAAAAIBAJ&sjid=eHIFAAAAIBAJ&pg=972,482691 Diamond was an elected member of the City of [http://www.boardofreps.org/ Stamford Board of Representatives] from 2004-2009, representing the City's 11th District for five years. He served as Deputy Majority Leader and Chairman of the Board's Legislative and Rules Committee. As a municipal legislator Diamond championed a ban on gifts to City employees,http://docs.boardofreps.org/Minutes/05min/051114.pdf historic preservation and tax credits for hybrid/efficient automobiles.http://www.boardofreps.org/committees/legrules/2008/reports/reports/080324.pdf Diamond was active in state and local politics for 20 years. While at Albany he served as the General Manager of the college radio station, [[WCDB]]. As General Manager he filed an application with the Federal Communications Commission for a power increase which was approved in 1982.{{cite web|url=http://wcdbfm.com/history.aspx |title=90.9FM WCDB Albany, New York |publisher=Wcdbfm.com |date= |accessdate=2014-03-26}} He earned a Juris Doctor degree in 1988 from [[Brooklyn Law School]]. At Brooklyn he concentrated on criminal law and procedure, studying with such noted scholars as [[Edward R. Korman|Edward Korman]] and [[Susan N. Herman|Susan Herman]]. While at Brooklyn he was a Senior Editor at The Justinian and received The American Bar Association Award For Excellence for a series of articles he published on grand jury reform. Diamond attended [[James E. Rogers College of Law|The University of Arizona James E. Rogers College of Law]] where he was in residence in 2012-2013 in the Indigenous People's Law And Policy Program.{{cite web|url=http://www.law.arizona.edu/depts/iplp/ |title=Indigenous Peoples Law & Policy Program |publisher=Law.arizona.edu |date=2013-11-20 |accessdate=2014-03-26}} Diamond concentrated on litigation in Indian Country{{cite web|author=John Pirro |url=http://www.newstimes.com/policereports/article/Defense-lawyer-has-new-specialty-4724739.php |title=Defense lawyer has new specialty |publisher=NewsTimes |date=2013-08-11 |accessdate=2014-03-26}} and studied with such notable scholars as [[Robert A. Williams, Jr.|Robert A. Williams]], Jr., [[James Anaya|S. James Anaya]], [[Melissa L. Tatum|Melissa Tatum]], and [[Raymond Austin]].{{cite web|url=http://www.law.arizona.edu/depts/iplp/faculty/facultyDetail.cfm?facultyid=251 |title=Indigenous Peoples Law & Policy Program |publisher=Law.arizona.edu |date= |accessdate=2014-03-26}} Diamond is currently working on a doctoral dissertation there and is a candidate for the Doctor of Juridical Science [[Doctor of Juridical Science|(S.J.D.)]] degree, the highest degree awarded in law. His doctoral research examines recent school and mass shootings along with the history of restorative justice in indigenous cultures. == Educator == Diamond has taught law as an adjunct professor at both [[Pima Community College]] in Tucson, Arizona (2012-2013), and at [[Western Connecticut State University]] in Danbury, Connecticut (1990-2005). Diamond splits his time between Norwalk, Connecticut and Tucson, Arizona.
