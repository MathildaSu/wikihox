Development|accessdate=July 5, 2015|work=Department of Government Information| date= March 12, 2015}} She is the creator and chairman of the ''[[Global Officials of Dignity Awards]]'', an international humanitarian awards institution, which aims to bridge the gap of power and influence to the peace and welfare of the human race. She is a former beauty queen who has won pageants since she was in elementary school. She was a girl scout of the year three years in a row as a young girl. She’s also a cultural and humanitarian icon, peace advocate, TV host, entrepreneur, producer, singer, choreographer, songwriter, artist, fashion designer, event coordinator, publicist, endorser and graphic artist. She’s a seasoned diplomat and of royalty from the Royal House of Balo-i in the Philippines. She has been interviewed on major news networks and international television and radio shows on her numerous and outstanding humanitarian and peace endeavors globally. ==Activities== Under her leadership, We Care for Humanity has initiated many innovative and important activities in extending care and help to the poor and the needy, building peace projects, strengthening international relations, promoting education and health for all, and empowering women and developing new peace and humanitarian leaders. She and her team promised to deliver even much stronger humanitarians by bringing local supporters who are also nominees for the 3rd GOD Awards at the United Nation. Arriving last February 26, the We Care for Humanity (WCH) team has already visited the Ash-Shifaa House Mercy, Colombo; Darul Rahmah and An Najm Nursery, Wattala; Islamic Center for Physically Handicapped, Colombo; Barathi Home, Baari Female Home, Punitha Boomi and Udippidy welfare camp in Jaffna. She is involved through her WCH in a number of global issues concerning seven causes – human rights, green environment, world peace, poverty eradication, education, health and wellness as well as empowerment. She was also promoting the development of healthy international relations between nations by shaping humanity through education, health, fitness, wellness, beauty, culture and arts. She led WCH, which had implemented programmes that benefited the disadvantaged and undeserved women, children, youths and senior citizens, had formed many chapters in various countries, including Nigeria, India and the Philippines. She was honorably appointed as Goodwill Ambassador of Acid Survivors Foundation, India (ASFI) in the beginning of January, 2015; ASFI ia a leading NGO in India for prevention of acid burn violence as well as for providing support services to survivors through a network of chapters and partners, by sharing knowledge, expertise and best practices. ASFI acts as a forum for advocacy of acid related causes, endeavours to promote a social environment conducive to elimination of all forms of gender violence and espouses a firm legal basis for prosecution of offenders and prescription of national guidelines for treatment, aftercare and rehabilitation of acid survivors. She was in Nepal recently where she had a one-to-one meeting with Nepalese Prime Minister Sushil Koirala and pledged to give Nepal which a grant of US$500,000 (RM1.87 million) worth of medical supplies and equipment especially to the victims of the earthquake under through her WCH's Global Health Initiative for Humanity (GHIH) project. She is instrumental for an initiative where Handicap News International Welfare Organisation of Pakistan would receive a container full of medical supplies for the disabled; the estimated value of the medical goods will be around $500,000. She collaborate through her WCH with Ravindran Rajappa, founder and president of Patronage and Refugee based in Malaysia on the issues of Human Trafficking and to improve the standard of anti-human trafficking in Malaysia and global level. She initiated through her WCA the internet based livelihood academy in collaboration with Joy Heartwell, founder of Empower People Academy (EPA) based in Los Angeles, California to promote the "Fight Against Hunger" and "Empowerment" programs of WCH, and to revolutionize the online marketing in the Philippines and it aims to change the mindset of the people on how to make money online. ==Beauty queen== * She was a beauty queen in Montleban, as well as crowned as Ms. Rizal, Ms. Southern Tagalog (Region 4) -Philippines, Ms. Luzon, Ms. Asia Galaxy, and Queen of the Philippines – Monet Lu. * She was also a recipient of the Luminary for Fashion & Beauty-Image Award, the Outstanding Filipino-American-PPP, the Gerry Roxas Leadership Award, Peace & Humanitarian Award – SPMUDA and the prestigious Elizabeth Taylor Beauty and Humanitarian Award – Global Independence Day. ==Honorary lecturer== She has lectured as an invited speaker throughout the United States and Asia on issues of peace, humanity, health and education, human trafficking and international trade. ==Honorary awards & appointments== She has received many awards for her work for a more peaceful and humanitarian world, including; * Vice President of International Affairs of National Council of Women of United States (NCWUS) * Minister of Cooperation of Order of Jerusalem * Minister of Home Affairs, Royal Kingdom of Sulu * The World Peace Committee Head Representative for California, USA * Conferment of Doctor of Divinity by the Order of Hagiasmos in Indonesia * The Deputy Ambassador for the United States of International Human Rights Organization in India ==Titles== She holds a number of titles including;
