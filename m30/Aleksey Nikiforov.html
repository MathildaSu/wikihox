xml:space="preserve" bytes="2903"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Aleksey Nikiforov|timestamp=20170520055923|year=2017|month=May|day=20|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Multiple issues| {{BLP unsourced|date=January 2017}} {{tone|date=January 2017}} }} '''Aleksey Nikiforov''' (24 April 1957 in [[Elektrėnai]], [[Lithuanian Soviet Socialist Republic|Lithuanian SSR]], [[Soviet Union]]) is a Lithuanian former professional [[hockey]] player for the [[Dynamo Riga]] team of the [[Russian Elite League]]. He is the father of [[New York Islanders]] prospect [[Vladimir Nikiforov]]. He resides on [[Long Island]] where he still teaches hundreds of adults and children of various skill capabilities how to play the game. == Coaching career == Aleksey currently coaches the PAL Jr. Islanders 16u AAA team. He previously coached the [[New York Junior Bobcats]] of the [[Atlantic Junior Hockey League]] where he boasted great placement for many of his students greatly. He is credited with teaching many Long Island hockey products the game. He teaches a European style of hockey, as opposed to the North American style. This European style focuses on skating speed, puck possession, and puck movement, while the North American style focuses more on [[bodychecking]] and overall physical domination.
