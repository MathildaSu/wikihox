''[[Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences|INFORMS Fellow Award]]'' (2012) |signature = }} '''Ramayya Krishnan''' is an [[Indian American]] [[Management]] and [[Information technology]] expert from [[Pittsburgh]], [[Pennsylvania]]. He is the [[Dean (education)|dean]] of [[Heinz College]],{{cite web |url=http://warp.byu.edu/wp-uploads/asm.byu.edu//2011/03/HeinzCollegeProspectus.pdf |title=H. John Heinz III College |publisher=Carnegie Mellon University |accessdate=December 22, 2012}} and also holds the [[H. John Heinz III]] deanship and is the [[William W. Cooper|W. W. Cooper and Ruth F. Cooper]] Professor of [[Operations research|Management science]] and [[Information systems]] at [[Carnegie Mellon University]].{{cite web |url=http://coursecatalog.web.cmu.edu/hjohnheinziiicollege/ |title=H. John Heinz III College - Course Catalogue |publisher=Carnegie Mellon University |accessdate=December 23, 2012}} Ramayya is also a member of [[Carnegie Library of Pittsburgh|Board of Trustees]] of [[Carnegie Library of Pittsburgh]].{{cite web |url=http://www.carnegielibrary.org/about/trustees/boardbio.html |title=Board of Trustees |publisher=Carnegie Library of Pittsburgh |accessdate=December 23, 2012}} == Early life and education == Ramayya has a [[bachelor's degree]] in [[mechanical engineering]] from the [[Indian Institute of Technology]], [[Madras]], a [[master's degree|master]] in [[Industrial engineering]] and [[Operations research]] and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] in Management science and Information systems from the [[University of Texas at Austin]]. He is an International Research Fellow of the [[International Center for Electronic Commerce]] in [[South Korea]]{{cite web |url=http://www.icec.net/icec_int8.html |title=ICEC Research Fellows |publisher=ICEC |accessdate=December 23, 2012}} and a visiting scientist at the [[Institute for Information Systems]] at [[Humboldt University]] in [[Germany]].{{cite web |url=http://www.ischool.berkeley.edu/newsandevents/events/sem20080505 |title=Lunch seminar: Famayya Krishnan, on the interaction between network structure and query content in an enterprise-wide technical forum |date=May 5, 2008 |publisher=UC Barkeley School of Information |accessdate=22 December 2012}} Also, he was a founding faculty member of the Information Systems Management program.{{cite web |url=http://pr-web-01.qatar.cmu.edu/4821 |title=Carnegie Mellon Qatar - Ramayya Krishnan |publisher=Carnegie Mellon Qatar |accessdate=December 22, 2012}} == Work and publications == He is the co-editor for [[telecommunications]] and [[electronic commerce]] at the ''[[Management Science (journal)|INFORMS Journal]]'' on [[computing]],{{cite web |url=http://isr.journal.informs.org/content/23/1/280.short |title=INFORMS - About Our Authors |publisher=INFORMS |accessdate=December 23, 2012}} and an associate editor for ''[[Operations Research: A Journal of the Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences|Management Science]]''. He co-edited a special issue of ''[[The Technology Interface Journal|Interfaces]]'' on [[E-business]] and is co-editing a special issue of ''Management Science'' on Operations research and E-business. He is the immediate past president of the [[Institute for Operations Research and Management Science]] (INFORMS) Computing Society. He has also served on the [[National Science Foundation]] (NSF) Expert Panel on database and [[expert system]]s and on several program committees of information systems conferences.{{cite web |url=http://www.nsf.gov/od/lpa/diggov.pdf |title=Some Assembly Required: Building a Digital Government for the 21st Century |date=March 1999 |work=Sharon S. Dawes, Peter A. Bloniarz, Kristine L. Kelly, Patricia D. Fletcher |publisher=National Science Foundation |accessdate=December 23, 2012}} Professor Krishnan, with a group of Master's students, led the development of [[ArtsNet]], a World Wide Web-based information service for the arts.{{cite web |url=http://www.cylab.cmu.edu/education/faculty/krishnan.html |title=CyLab - Ramayya Krishnan |publisher=Carnegie Mellon CyLab |accessdate=December 23, 2012}} ArtsNet is offered as a community service by the Master of Arts Management Program at the Heinz College. In December 2003, together with his colleague Deb Dey, he hosted [[Workshop on Information Technologies and Systems]] in [[Seattle, Washington]].{{cite web |url=http://www.aisnet.org/intranet/aiscouncilnet/aiscouncil/ccl2003/WITS2003CFP.pdf |title=Call for Papers - 13th Workshop on Information Technologies and Systems (WITS'03) |date=December 13, 2003 |publisher=AISnet |accessdate=December 23, 2012}} == Research == Krishnan's research interests are mainly based on problems that arise at the interface of technology, business and policy aspects of internet-enabled systems. promoting data access and [[Privacy#Privacy protection|protecting privacy]].{{cite journal |last1=Bhargava |first1=Hemant |last2=Choudhary |first2=Vidyanand |last3=Krishnan |first3=Ramayya |year=2000 |url=http://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/viewdoc/summary?doi=10.1.1.110.3118 |accessdate=December 21, 2012}} His research on these topics is supported by the [[National Science Foundation]], the [[United
