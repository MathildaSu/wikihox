He was one of the early members of [[Earth, Wind & Fire]]. And is perhaps best known for his work with the group, however, he joined the group already having an extensive professional music background. This includes receiving a gold record as a 16 year old for his work with [[Donny Hathaway]] on his iconic '''''Live''''' album.http://www.allmusic.com/album/live-mw0000460498 White, along with brothers [[Maurice White]], [[Verdine White]] and other members of Earth, Wind & Fire, was inducted into the [[Rock & Roll Hall of Fame]] in 2000.{{Cite news|url=http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/earth-wind-and-fire-groove-into-hall-20000302|title=Earth, Wind and Fire Groove Into Hall|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=2017-07-30}}{{Cite web|url=https://www.rockhall.com/inductees/earth-wind-fire|title=Earth, Wind & Fire|website=Rock & Roll Hall of Fame|language=en|access-date=2017-07-30}} He also received a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 2016. ==Discography== * 1969: La La Time – The Salty Peppers (tambourine)White, Maurice; Powell, Herb.
