Florescu |publisher=TCM.com |accessdate=21 August 2010}} has had a career as media entrepreneur and television producer in the United States and Europe. A journalist by training, Mr. Florescu is the son of a notable American Balkan scholar, [[Radu Florescu]] and the grandson of a distinguished Romanian pre-war diplomat.[http://www.lumeamilitara.ro/index.php?mod=articol&idart=49&numar=1/2004§iune=Lumea%20militara%20in%20dialog Lumea Militara - Societatea Scriitorilor Militari] As of 2011, he serves as Chief Executive Officer of Centrade,[http://centradeusa.com/ Centrade] a family-owned, US media company based in [[Houston]]. He is fluent in French and Romanian{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} and comes from one of Romania's oldest families with roots going back more than 600 years.{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} ==Education and Early Life== Mr. Florescu holds a B.A. (Magna Cum Laudae, 1976) in History from [[Boston College]]. In 1976, he was a Massachusetts state finalist for the Rhodes scholarship.{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} From 1974 to 1975, he studied Politics, Philosophy and Economics (P.P.E.) at Oxford University (Campion Hall),[http://www.florescufoundation.org/board-of-directors/ Florescu Foundation] where he served as the first American editor of the university newspaper, "The Cherwell". He is listed in the "Who's Who in the Media and Communications" (multiple years) and "Who's Who in the West" (multiple years) and "Who's Who in the World" (2008).[http://www.marquiswhoswho.com/ Who's Who] Mr. Florescu is divorced and has two boys.{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} ==Career== Florescu served as Chief Executive Officer of David Paradine Television, Sir [[David Frost]]'s Los Angeles-based U.S. television production company.{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1993/07/16/style/chronicle-683793.html |author=Nadine Brozan |title=Chronicle|publisher=''[[The New York Times]]''|date=16 July 1993|accessdate=22 August 2010}} Florescu has produced more than 200 specials, documentaries and interviewshttp://www.manta.com/c/mm20bpl/david-paradine-television-inc for [[Public Broadcasting Service|PBS]], [[HBO]],[Bolt, Robert, "Scenes from Two Lives,Random House, 1998] [[A&E Network|A&E]], [[History (U.S. TV channel)|History Channel]], [[Investigation Discovery|Discovery Times]], [[Disney Channel]], [[CBS Cable]] and other broadcast outlets in the United States and throughout the world. He was Co-Executive Producer of ''Talking with David Frost'', one of the longest-running news and public affairs interview series in PBS history.[http://www.pbs.org/newshour/frost/transcript.html PBS | President George W. Bush Talking with David Frost | Part I of the Interview] Through these productions, Florescu has served as Co-Executive Producer for interviews with every past U.S. President (except President Obama) since [[Richard Nixon]][http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0261639/ David Frost Interviews Richard Nixon (TV 1977) - IMDb] and also with several foreign heads of state including [[Mikhail Gorbachev]] and [[Margaret Thatcher]] and several Middle Eastern leaders.[http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0384398/ One on One with David Frost: George Bush - A President's Story (Video 1998) - IMDb] In a joint venture with ''US New & World Report'' and ''The New York Times'', Florescu produced ''The Next President with David Frost'' in 1988.{{cite news |last1=Frost |first1=David |last1=Greenya |first1=John |date=1988 |title=The Next President |url= |newspaper=U.S. News & World Report |location= |access-date= }} Prior to joining David Frost, Florescu served as Director of Communications for the [[Democratic National Committee]] (DNC). Active for 30 years in democratic politics,{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} he also worked in presidential campaigns of [[Edward Kennedy]]{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} and played a fundraising role in the presidential campaigns of [[John Kerry]]{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} and [[Barack Obama]][http://centradeusa.com/news-2012-11-26 Florescus host Obama event to drive voters to polls] where, with his brother Radu, he ran twice "Romanian-Americans for Obama". From 1982 to 1985, Florescu was Vice President of Boston-based American Program Bureau (APB). In that capacity, Florescu created and served as Executive Producer of the PBS series ''Great Confrontations at the Oxford Union'', a 4-part public affairs debate series that brought together the world's best student debaters with various world figures.[http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/title/223794 BFI | Film & TV Database | GREAT CONFRONTATIONS FROM THE OXFORD UNION (1985)] From 1980 to 1982, Florescu ran Video Productions International (VPI) where he pioneered the use of cable television to broadcast political commercials.{{cite book |last=Armstrong |first=Richard |date=1988 |title=The Next Hurrah: The Communications Revolution in American Politics |url= |location= New York |publisher=William Morrow Publishers |chapter=7 |page= |isbn= |access-date= }} He also served as an adviser to Congressional House Democrats on political use of new technologies,{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} a subject on which Florescu has lectured on at [[Harvard University|Harvard]], [[Stanford University|Stanford]] and [[Yale University|Yale]] Universities.{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} From 1977 to 1979, he was an AP writer based in Paris, France.{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} Thereafter he served as a metro writer for ''The Boston Globe'' before joining the 1980 Kennedy Presidential campaign.{{Citation needed|date=March 2015}} Florescu is involved with the management of media companieshttp://www.chainsaweurope.comhttp://www.kubisinteractive.com/ pioneered together with his brothers immediately after the fall of [[Nicolae Ceausescu]].
