She currently works as a digital editor for Diversified Communications in Portland, Maine and is a certified personal trainer and blogger{{Cite web|url=http://www.katherinerushlau.com/|title=Queen of Fit|last=|first=|date=|website=Katherine Tay|language=en-US|archive-url=|archive-date=|dead-url=|access-date=2017-07-12}} who trains and advises young women, including pageant and fitness competitors. == Early life and education == Rushlau was born in Fort Worth, Texas and was adopted by her parents at birth. She grew up in Dresden, Maine and is a 2011 graduate of Hall-Dale High School{{Cite news|url=http://www.kidsrsu.org/learning-communities/hall-dale-middle-school/|title=Hall-Dale Middle & High School – KidsRSU2|work=KidsRSU2|access-date=2017-07-12|language=en-US}}. Both a writer and reader from a young age, she was influenced by her aunt, a Maine-based author, to pursue a career in writing. She has an interest in health, fitness, and food writing and interned for the Wiscasset Newspaper in Wiscasset, Maine in 2009. She studied journalism and publishing at Emerson College in Boston and graduated early in December 2014. While in school, she worked at the Boston Globe{{Cite web|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/1970/01/12/serp/LRzdrFYs8Li3rVYB2kghyL/story.html|title=- The Boston Globe|last=Rushlau|first=Katherine|date=|website=BostonGlobe.com|archive-url=|archive-date=|dead-url=|access-date=2017-07-12}} Living/Arts and Metro, Boston.com Travel,{{Cite news|url=https://www.boston.com/culture/new-england-travel/2014/04/22/5-reasons-to-visit-wiscasset-maine|title=5 Reasons to Visit Wiscasset, Maine|last=Rushlau|first=Katherine|date=2014-04-22|work=Boston.com|access-date=2017-07-12|archive-url=|archive-date=|dead-url=|language=en-US}} and USA Today{{Cite news|url=http://college.usatoday.com/katy-rushlau/|title=KATY RUSHLAU|date=2014-09-05|work=USA TODAY College|access-date=2017-07-12|language=en-US}}. She moved to Danvers, Massachusetts in January 2015. At the time, she worked for BLR, a healthcare publishing company, as an editor. In November 2016, she moved to Portland, Maine and began working as an editor for an integrative healthcare website at Diversified Communications. She created her blog and business, Katherine Tay—Queen of Fit, in December 2016, documenting a 30 pound weight-loss leading up to the Miss Maine competition in June 2017, where she placed first runner up and received honors for community service and academics. Rushlau is a certified personal fitness trainer through the National Exercise and Sports Trainers Association. == Pageants == Rushlau won Miss International{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/missinternationalscholarshippageant/|title=Miss International Scholarship Pageant|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=2017-07-12}}in August 2013. She had not competed in pageants prior to winning the Calais, Maine-based contest. Winning the pageant qualified her to compete at the state competition in June the following year. Rushlau was second runner-up at Miss Maine 2014 and advanced to the National Sweetheart Pageant{{Cite web|url=http://www.hoopestonjaycees.org/pageant/|title=The National Sweetheart Pageant|website=www.hoopestonjaycees.org|access-date=2017-07-12}} as Miss Maine National Sweetheart in Hoopeston, Illinois that August. She then went on to win the Miss Maine Day title in November 2014. She competed two more times at Miss Maine 2016 and Miss Maine 2017, placing first runner-up in both competitions. == Career == Following her years in pageantry, Rushlau started a personal training and nutrition consulting blog and business, Queen of Fit, where she mentors young women, many of whom are preparing for pageants, fitness, and bikini competitions.
