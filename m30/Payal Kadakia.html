xml:space="preserve" bytes="2951"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Payal Kadakia|timestamp=20160704061700|year=2016|month=July|day=4|substed=yes|help=off}} '''Payal Kadakia''' is the CEO and co-founder of [[ClassPass]], a monthly membership service that lets users attend a sample of fitness classes in certain cities. ==Life and career== Payal Kadakia grew up in [[Randolph, New Jersey]].{{cite web|title=Payal Kadakia|url=http://gothamgal.com/2012/03/payal-kadakia-classtivity-woman-entrepreneur/|publisher=gothamgal`|accessdate=10 February 2016}} In 2005, she graduated from [[MIT]] with a degree in Operations Research.{{cite web|title=Payal Kadakia|url=https://startmit.mit.edu/payal-kadakia/|publisher=MIT|accessdate=10 February 2016}}{{cite web|last1=Arata|first1=Emily|title=I Want Your Job: Payal Kadakia, Cofounder And CEO Of ClassPass|url=http://elitedaily.com/women/want-your-job-payal-kadakia-classpass/1320363/|website=EliteDaily.com|publisher=EliteDailyy|accessdate=10 February 2016}} After college, Kadakia worked as a consultant at [[Bain & Company]].{{cite web|last1=Gregoire|first1=Waylae|title=Payal Kadakia: How She Turned Her Love of Dancing Into a Startup Venture|url=http://nextshark.com/payal-kadakia-how-she-turned-her-love-of-dancing-into-a-startup/|website=Nextshark.com|publisher=Nextshark|accessdate=10 February 2016}} She went on to work at [[Warner Music Group]]. In 2008, while working at Warner, she also founded Sa Dance Company. After spending over an hour searching online for an open ballet class she could take, Kadakia had the idea to create a search engine for fitness classes, similar to [[OpenTable]].{{cite web|last1=Marikar|first1=Sheila|title=How one fitness entrepreneur raised $14 million|url=http://fortune.com/2014/11/03/class-pass/|publisher=Fortune|accessdate=10 February 2016}} In 2011, Kadakia founded Classtivity with her then business partner Sanjiv Sanghavi. In January 2014, Classtivity was rebranded as [[ClassPass]].{{cite web|last1=Carson|first1=Biz|title=How this world-class dancer went from failing twice to a $400 million fitness empire|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/payal-kadakia-went-from-failing-twice-to-a-400-million-fitness-empire-2015-9|publisher=Business Insider|accessdate=10 February 2016}} ==References== {{Reflist|2}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Kadakia, Payal}} [[Category:American women chief executives]] [[Category:Massachusetts Institute of Technology alumni]] [[Category:People from New Jersey]]
