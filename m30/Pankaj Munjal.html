The complete group under Mr. Pankaj Munjal’s leadership reported revenue of INR 2105 Crores (approximately USD 35 million).  A firm believer in innovation and upgradation, over the years he has made sure that any new management concept, any new development in technology and any chance of expansion do not bypass the Hero Group. As Chairman and Managing Director of Hero Cycles Ltd. he has taken the company to new heights. Today, Hero Cycles is the market leader in both standard and special bicycle segments in India, with an overall market share of about 36 per cent. It also exports to various countries in Middle East, Africa, Asia and Europe. Under his able leadership, the company has been growing from strength and is constantly reinventing itself and expanding its portfolio. He is an alumnus of the prestigious Kettering University (formerly General Motors Institute), London Business School & Harvard Business School. == Early life == Pankaj Munjal is the son of Shri. [[Om Prakash Munjal|O.P. Munjal,]] founder of Hero Cycles. He finished his early schooling from Ludhiana, and graduated in Science and training in Automotive Manufacturing at Kettering University (formerly General Motors Institute), Flint in USA. Thereon, he also attended an executive program in Strategic Marketing Management at London Business School & Harvard University. == Career == Mr. Pankaj Munjal took over the executive responsibilities of Hero Cycles Ltd. in 2010 after the Munjal family realignment of group businesses. Prior to this, he headed [[Hero Motors Ltd]]. which he established in 1988 and was the leader in the automotive moped segment with its brand Hero Puch. He has demonstrated his foresighted leadership by transforming Hero Motors from a moped company into a world-class gears & transmission maker which today supplies to BMW Germany and Harley Davidson. He read the winds of change in the automotive market and took a bold decision to change the course from being an OEM to a premium automotive component manufacturer. Over the years, he reinvented Hero Motors from a B2C to B2B company, traveling across Europe and US to bag new customers, successfully adding Bombardier, BMW, Harley Davidson, Husqvarna etc. as customers for Hero Motors’ high-precision gears and transmission systems. In recent years, Mr Munjal has been on an expansion spree and has spearheaded several acquisitions, enhancing the company’s standing both in the Indian and the international market.  Hero Cycles acquired a majority stake in the UK-based cycle distributor, Avocet Sports Limited{{Cite news|url=http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/auto/news/two-wheelers/motorcycles/hero-cycles-acquires-majority-stake-in-uks-avocet-sports/articleshow/48454108.cms|title=Hero Cycles acquires majority stake in UK's Avocet Sports - The Economic Times|newspaper=The Economic Times|access-date=2016-12-08}}, last year, which catapulted the company into the high-value European cycle market. Soon after, the company acquired Firefox Bikes{{Cite news|url=http://www.livemint.com/Companies/v2I8jh8Tv236qS4dDFi06K/Hero-Cycles-acquires-Firefox-Bikes-in-allcash-transaction.html|title=Hero Cycles buys Firefox Bikes for Rs150 crore|last=Raj|first=Amrit|date=2015-09-17|newspaper=http://www.livemint.com/|access-date=2016-12-08}}, India’s largest premium bicycle brand and cemented its position in the fast-growing premium cycling segment. This was followed by the acquisition of a majority stake in BSH Ventures{{Cite web|url=http://www.vccircle.com/news/engineering/2016/03/23/hero-cycles-buys-60-stake-sri-lankas-bsh-ventures|title=Hero Cycles buys 60% stake in Sri Lanka's BSH Ventures|website=www.vccircle.com|access-date=2016-12-08}}, one of Sri Lanka’s leading bicycle manufacturers. On the automotive front, Mr Munjal heads Hero Motors Ltd. to target the global automotive market with gears and transmission components for international customers like BMW, Bombardier, Harley Davidson, Ducati{{Cite news|url=http://auto.ndtv.com/news/hero-motors-to-produce-transmission-components-for-ducati-motorcycles-1472466|title=Hero Motors To Produce Transmission Components For Ducati Motorcycles - NDTV CarAndBike|newspaper=CarAndBike|access-date=2016-12-08}} etc. He is also present in the domestic automotive market with group companies - Munjal Kiriu Industries Pvt. Ltd. and ZF Hero Chassis Systems{{Cite news|url=http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/auto/news/zf-hero-starts-assembling-chassis-for-bmw/articleshow/49185170.cms|title=ZF Hero starts assembling chassis for BMW - The Economic Times|newspaper=The Economic Times|access-date=2016-12-08}} in joint venture with Sumitomo Kiriu{{Cite news|url=http://articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com/2007-12-13/news/27670334_1_hero-motors-managing-director-pankaj-munjal-munjal-kiriu-industries|title=Hero, Sumitomo form JV for disc brake manufacturing - The Economic Times|newspaper=The Economic Times|access-date=2016-12-08}} of Japan and ZF of Germany respectively.     == Growing Hero family == Mr. Munjal, through Hero Cycles, Hero Motors, Munjal Kiriu and ZF Hero manages a community of more than 4000 employees which he affectionately calls the Hero family, as did his father Mr. OP Munjal, the family senior who founded Hero Cycles. The bond of employees working for Hero with the company is legendary in Ludhiana circle with the company and employees standing up unwaveringly with each other in times of business exigencies and personal crisis. Hero scores amongst the top in the number of engaged alumni who choose to return to Hero after pursuing other stints. Mr. Munjal has encouraged a culture where people are free to pursue other interests and opportunities and their talents are always welcome to seek opportunities again with Hero. == Board Memberships and affiliations == He has been Non-Executive Director at Munjal Showa Limited since May 16, 1985. He serves as a Director of Hero Cycles Limited and Hero Motors Limited. He has been the Chairman of the Board at Hero Motors Limited since July 2015. He served as a Director of Hero Honda Motors Limited. == Social welfare == Mr. Munjal is a firm believer of enabling society and the wider community with sustainable methods because these in return become workforce talents, entrepreneurs and ultimately consumers, multiplying the economy as they grow upwardly mobile and create a win-win scenario for everyone.
