xml:space="preserve" bytes="3309"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=KE Software|timestamp=20151013235448|year=2015|month=October|day=13|substed=yes|help=off}} '''KE Software''' is a [[computer software]] company based in Manchester, [[United Kingdom]]. It is fully owned by the [[Axiell Group]] since that company purchased the firm in 2014.[http://www.mynewsdesk.com/axiell/pressreleases/ke-software-becomes-a-part-of-the-swedish-axiell-group-980472 My News Desk] KE Software markets two main products: the Ke EMu Electronic Museum management system, a collections management system for museums; and Vitalware Vital Records Management System.[https://www.kesoftware.com/ke-software/about-ke-software KE Software Corporate Web Page kesoftware.com 'About' accessed 21/09/2015] The first version of Ke EMu was launched in 1997 and uses the Texpress database engine with client/server architecture on a Windows or Unix/Linux server.[https://emu.kesoftware.com/support/documentation/the-database-engine/about-emu-s-database-engine/417-about-texpress About EMu's Database Engine] KE Software has been described as ''...the world’s leading provider of Collections Management systems and services for natural history museums, cultural history museums, art museums, herbaria, botanic gardens, archives and special collections. The company’s clients include the three largest museums in the world.''[http://www.collectionstrust.org.uk/suppliers Collections Trust Suppliers Directory]{{rs|date=October 2015}} Emu is used by a large number of museums and galleries around the world, including the Vancouver Art Gallery,Art Museum Libraries and Librarianship, By Joan M. Benedetti, Scarecrow Press, 1 Jan 2007 p.130 New York Botanical Garden,[http://sciweb.nybg.org/science2/hcol/mtsc/NYBG_Best_Practices.doc New York Botanical Garden Virtual Herbarium Best Practices Guide] Museum of Victoria,[http://www.lookatmedam.com.au/testimonials/museum-victoria Lookatmedam, Testimonials 'Museum Victoria', Media Equation Pty Ltd. ] and Melbourne University Archives.[http://blogs.unimelb.edu.au/archives/farewell-to-christine-elias/ FAREWELL TO CHRISTINE ELIAS June 22, 2015 by University of Melbourne Archives in UMA projects] The program has been described as providing ''...comprehensive museum management (collection management plus other administrative needs for a museum), workflow and project management, flexible metadata, various stats and metrics, and comprehensive web interface with support for mobile devices and kiosks''[http://www.idea.org/blog/2011/04/07/collection-management-systems-museums-and-the-web-2011/ Chris Fincham, head of U.S. operations, Collection management systems: Museums and the Web 2011 7-Apr-2011] ==References== {{reflist}} {{DEFAULTSORT:KE Software}} [[Category:Information technology management]] [[Category:Content management systems]] [[Category:Public records]] [[Category:Software companies]] [[Category:Museum informatics]]
