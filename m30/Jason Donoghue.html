xml:space="preserve" bytes="6450"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Jason Donoghue|timestamp=20140208002519|year=2014|month=February|day=8|substed=yes|help=off}} {{notability|date=January 2014}} {{ref improve|date=January 2014}} {{Infobox person | name = Jason Donoghue | birthname = Jason Morgan Donoghue | birth_date = {{Birth date and age|1977|10|14|df=y}} | birth_place = [[Shrewsbury]], [[Shropshire]], [[England]] | occupation = Actor | years_active = 1995–present }} '''Jason Morgan Donoghue''' (born 14 October 1977) is an English actor, best known for his roles as [[#Shane Swanson|Shane Swanson]] in the [[Channel 4]] [[soap opera]] ''[[Hollyoaks]]'',{{cite news|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/soaps/s13/hollyoaks/news/a202385/donoghue-to-make-Hollyoaks-comeback.htm|title=Donoghue to make Hollyoaks comeback|work=[[Digital Spy]]|publisher=[[Hachette Filipacchi Médias]]|author=Green, Kris|date=10 December 2010|accessdate=29 December 2013}} [[#Dominic Pearce|Dominic Pearce]] in the [[science-fiction]] series ''[[Outlanders]]'' and DI Toby Lawrence in the [[crime thriller]] miniseries ''[[Murder Incorporated]]''. == Early life == Donoghue was born in [[Shrewsbury]] to parents John and Suzanne (née Whitelaw) and when he was three-months-old moved to the [[London Borough of Islington]] where he was brought up alongside his sisters Louise (born 1974) and Emma (born 1980). Donoghue attended [[Ashmount Primary School]] and [[Central Foundation Boys' School]] and initially aspired to join the [[Royal Navy]]. But after landing a bit part in a pantomime when he was fourteen he chose to become an actor and a sixteen he left school after getting a training placement at the [[Royal Academy of Dramatic Art]]. == Television work == Donoghue's first ever role was when he guest starred in two episodes of the [[police procedural]] drama ''[[The Bill]]''. However it was his second screen role that turned him into a household name when he was cast as bad boy [[#Shane Swanson|Shane Swanson]] in the [[Channel 4]] [[soap opera]] ''[[Hollyoaks]]'', signing a four year contract to the show and appearing from 1996 until departing in 2000, when Donoghue quit to pursue new roles. Having now become an accomplished television star Donoghue appeared in various television shows including ''[[Queer as Folk (UK TV series)|Queer as Folk]]'', ''[[Casualty (TV series)|Casualty]]'', ''[[Coronation Street]]'', ''[[Cutting It]]'' and ''[[Doctor Who]]'', along with several performances in stage productions. Following another sixteen–month stint as Shane Swanson on ''[[Hollyoaks]]'' (2005–07) which ended in January 2007 after Donoghue was axed, Donoghue was cast in the role of [[#Dominic Pearce|Dominic Pearce]] in the upcoming [[science-fiction]] television series ''[[Outlanders]]'', making his first appearance as one of the six original protagonists in the series in the [[#Pilot (Outlanders episode)|pilot episode]]. Donoghue garnered much critical success and acclaim for his role as Dominic and as one of the cast has been nominated for several [[BAFTA Awards]]. The role remains his most successful to date. However following the conclusion of the third series in 2009 Donoghue announced he was quitting and that he would depart after the next series. Donoghue departed the show at the conclusion of the fourth series with Dominic being killed off.{{cite news|url=http://www.itv.com/tv/s24/outlanders/'outlanders'-star-dominic-to-be-killed-off-in-shock-twist.htm|title='Outlanders' star Dominic to be Killed Off in Shock Exit|work=[[ITV.com]]|author=James, Oliver|date=23 April 2010|accessdate=29 December 2013}} Shortly after departing ''[[Outlanders]]'' in November 2010 Donoghue announced that he would be returning to ''[[Hollyoaks]]'' in the new year in his third stint on the show. After appearing in three episodes of ''[[Misfits (TV series)|Misfits]]'' Donoghue filmed his first scenes back in the show in December 2010, making his return to the series on 12 January 2011. In January 2012 Donoghue took a break from ''Hollyoaks'' to film several cameo appearances for his character of Dominic in ''[[Outlanders]]'' and his scenes for the film ''[[Hansel & Gretel: Witch Hunters]]'', and returned to the soap opera on 13 July 2012. In September 2013 Donoghue took a second break from ''Hollyoaks'' to perform in a [[Royal National Theatre]] production of ''[[Hamlet]]''. He returned to the show on 25 December 2013. == Awards == As a member of the cast ''[[Outlanders]]'' Donoghue has won a total of four [[British Academy Television Awards]] for [[British Academy Television Award for Best Drama Series|Best Drama Series]]. Donoghue has also been nominated for the [[British Academy Television Award]] twice. At the [[British Soap Awards]] 2000/2011/2013 Donoghue has been nominated for "Sexiest Male" and in 2012 for "Best Actor". At the [[Inside Soap Awards]] 2006/2013 he was nominated for "Best Bad Boy" and won the award at the 2011 Inside Soap Awards. == Filmography == Donoghue made his first film appearance in the 2002 film ''[[Once Upon a Time in the Midlands]]'', and he has gone onto make appearances in a further four films, including ''[[The Football Factory (film)|The Football Factory]]'' (2004), ''[[Kingdom of Heaven (film)|Kingdom of Heaven]]'' (2005), ''[[Attack the Block]]'' (2011) and ''[[Hansel and Gretel: Witch Hunters]]'' (2013). == References == {{Reflist}} == External links == * {{IMDb name|9404000}} * [http://www.itv.com/jasondonoghue/ Jason Donoghue] at [[ITV.com]] {{Persondata | NAME = Donoghue, Jason | ALTERNATIVE NAMES = | SHORT DESCRIPTION = British actor | DATE OF BIRTH = 14 October 1977 | PLACE OF BIRTH = [[Shrewsbury]], [[Shropshire]], [[England]] | DATE OF DEATH = | PLACE OF DEATH = }} {{DEFAULTSORT:Donoghue, Jason}} [[Category:English people]] [[Category:English male stage actors]] [[Category:English male television actors]] [[Category:English male soap opera actors]] [[Category:English male film actors]] [[Category:1977 births]] [[Category:Living people]] [[Category:People from Shrewsbury]] [[Category:People from Shropshire]]
