xml:space="preserve" bytes="4258"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Randy Charles Morin|timestamp=20170719235629|year=2017|month=July|day=19|substed=yes|help=off}} '''Randy Charles Morin''' is a Canadian web publisher and the former chairman of the [[RSS Advisory Board]]{{cite web|url=http://www.cadenhead.org/workbench/news/3388/randy-charles-morin-becomes-rss-advisory |title=Randy becomes Chairman of RSS}}, a group that publishes the [[RSS (file format)|RSS]] 2.0 specification. Randy authored the [http://www.rssboard.org/rss-autodiscovery RSS autodiscovery specification] and contributed to the [http://www.rssboard.org/rss-profile RSS profile]. He also runs the [[TalkSports]] website.{{cite web|url=http://www.espn.com/espn/story/_/id/11752403/athletes-personal-lives-big-business|date=October 14, 2014|accessdate=July 19, 2017|title=Athletes' dating lives are big business|publisher=ESPN}} In 2005, Morin began Rmail, a startup that enabled RSS feeds to be read over email. The site grew to more than 50,000 subscribers, and in April 2007 was sold to the broadcast network [[NBC]].{{cite web |url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/technologyNews/idUKN0147588720070501 |title=NBC Universal looks to feed off Rmail|author=Reuters|date=2007-05-01|accessdate=2008-11-11}} NBC digital media executive George Kliavkoff said the acquisition would enable the company to "make predictive understandings of what they might be interested in and start learning about RSS." NBC rebranded the service as [[SendMeRSS]]. Morin began TalkSports after a 2005 blog post he wrote about hockey player [[Sidney Crosby]] received a comment from a female inquiring whether Crosby had a girlfriend. He explained to [[Drama Scene Live Radio]] in an interview, "Google immediately picked up that new comment and within a few days that page was getting 1,000 hits per day. And I thought, I've got an idea ..."{{cite web|url=http://www.blogtalkradio.com/dramascenelive/2010/08/11/lets-talk-sportsits-not-what-you-know-but-what-u-can-prove|date=August 11, 2010|accessdate=May
