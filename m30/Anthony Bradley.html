Bradley''' is an author and professor. He has written numerous books, including ''Liberating Black Theology'' (2010), ''Black and Tired'' (2010), and ''Aliens in the Promised Land'' (2013). Dr. Bradley is currently associate professor of religious theology at [[The King's College (New York)|the King's College]] in New York City, where he also serves as the chair of the Religious and Theological Studies program. He is also a research fellow for [[Acton Institute|The Acton Institute]].{{cite web|title=Staff Profile: Anthony B. Bradley, PhD|url=http://www.acton.org/about/staff/anthony-b-bradley|publisher=Acton Institute|accessdate=23 January 2015}} Before coming to King's, he was assistant professor of theology at [[Covenant Theological Seminary|Covenant Seminary]] from 2005 to 2009, where he also directed the [[Francis A. Schaeffer Institute]].{{cite web|title="A Reformed Approach to Racial Reconciliation"|url=http://www.worldmag.com/2015/01/a_reformed_approach_to_racial_reconciliation|website=http://www.worldmag.com/|publisher=World Mag|accessdate=23 January 2015}} ==Writings== * Bradley, Anthony B.
