xml:space="preserve" bytes="11163"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=List of Formula One driver numbers|timestamp=20160221153455|year=2016|month=February|day=21|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Formula One|lists}} Since the start of the [[2014 Formula One season|2014]] [[Formula One]] season, drivers have to choose an available starting number before entering their first [[Grand Prix motor racing|Grand Prix]].http://www.autosport.com/news/report.php/id/111796 Drivers carry this number throughout their F1 career. A permanent number cannot be reallocated if the driver associated with that number has driven in the last two seasons. For example, numbers that were used most recently in the 2014 season can be reallocated in 2017, providing that the driver who used the number in 2014 does not return to Formula One in 2016. The FIA have also issued temporary numbers to drivers that are exceptions to this rule. Those numbers are 45 (used by [[André Lotterer]] in one race) and 46 (Used by [[Will Stevens]] in one race). The reigning Formula One World Driver's Champion can elect to use their allocated permanent number or car number 1 during the year following their title. The number 1 was most recently used by [[Sebastian Vettel]] in 2014. The number 17 was used by [[Jules Bianchi]] in 2014 before his crash at the [[2014 Japanese Grand Prix|Japanese Grand Prix]].
