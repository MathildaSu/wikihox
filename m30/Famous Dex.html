He is well known for his songs, which include "Drip From My Walk", "Energy", "Hit 'Em With It", and "2 Times".{{cite web|title=Famous Dex is the Chicago Rapper Who Just Wants to Have Fun|url=http://www.highsnobiety.com/2016/04/22/famous-dex-interview/|website=Highsnobiety|date=2016-04-22|accessdate=2016-08-04}}{{cite news|last=Jefferies|first=David|title=Famous Dex Bio and Career – MTV|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/famous-dex/biography/|newspaper=[[MTV]]|publisher=[[Viacom]]|access-date=2016-08-04}} ==Early life and education== Dexter Gore, Jr. was born on September 6, 1993 in [[Manhattan]], a borough of [[New York City]], [[New York]], to deceased parents Dexter Gore, Sr. (father) and Pamela Winters (mother). He lived in [[Harlem]], one of Manhattan's historical neighborhoods. Dex was also the fourth youngest of twelve children, the fourth of five half-siblings, as well as the youngest of 3½ step-siblings. Seven days before his first birthday in late 1994, Dex's elder sister was gunned down in a [[drive-by shooting]] while exiting a local [[McDonald's]]. After his sister's death, Dex's mother relocated the family to 145th Street & Lenox Ave., Harlem's dangerous, crime-riddled street address. While living inside an apartment, Dex attended Harlem Prep Elementary School. There, he met and befriended classmate and rapper [[Azealia Banks]]. By the end of 1994, his family's apartment complex was foreclosed on a government loan. Also, the building was severely used as a setting on part of the 1995 [[crime drama]], ''[[New Jersey Drive]]'', which details the growing automotive theft rate in [[Newark, New Jersey]]. Enraged, Dex's father left the family to attend the military while the rest flew to [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]]. While ''New Jersey Drive'', directed by [[Nick Gomez]] and produced by [[Spike Lee]], premiered in April, Dex began listening to [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]], but dangerously, his family lived on a top floor of the [[Robert Taylor Homes]] on the [[South Side, Chicago|South Side]] section of Chicago. Unfortunately, Dex's mother financial condition turned out poor; At seven years of age, he was sent out to a Catholic school located in [[Hyde Park, Chicago|Hyde Park]]. In January 1997, Dex's father got fired from his job as a lieutenant of the military. Later, his father was charged with conspiracy, drug and weapon possession and assault; he was sentenced to death and was publicly executed that September. Dex was released from a Catholic school and sent back to the Robert Taylor Homes, but his return was short-lived when the building of the complex was demolished in 1998. After seeing this, he left his family alone and used his mother's lost jewelry to buy himself a house. However, with a population of fostered children on the rise in mid-2000, he reunited with his mother and the two settled down. A year later, his grandmother spent annual time in New York City (Dex's birthplace). Tragically, in 2001, while his grandmother was inside the [[7 World Trade Center]] on the eighteenth row, several jets crashed onto the twin towers of the [[World Trade Center (1973–2001)|World Trade Center]], amid the [[September 11 attacks]]. Dex's grandmother died eight days later after suffering from an "after-burn". In 2004, Dex had a disagreement with his mother over his grandmother's tragic death. At this point, he was kicked out of his mother's house and was never heard from again. That same year, he attended [Irene C.] Hernández Middle School in Chicago, where he befriended female rap artist Kay-Mice, the surrogate sister of [[Da Brat]]. Gore also attended [[Paul Robeson High School (Chicago)|Paul Robeson High School]] in [[Englewood, Chicago]], where he also met fellow hometown rappers [[Lil Durk]] and [[Chief Keef]]. However, with poor grades, he was kicked out and sent back to Catholic school. ==Career== In [[Autumn]] 2011, after spending eight months in a hospital, Dex later [[Drug rehabilitation|rehabilitated]] and decided to become a [[rapper]]. He later developed the alias '''Black Migo Dex''', because of his [[African-American]] and [[Morocco|Moroccan]] cultures as well as his religion to being a [[Muslim]]. In 2013, he received the advanced attention with the respective remixes of Chief Keef's "Kobe" and in 2014, with [[Dej Loaf]]'s "[[Try Me (Dej Loaf song)|Try Me]]". Later on, he released his first single, "Bottle After Bottle". However, after a short period of using his stage name, he decided to rename himself '''Famous Dex'''.{{citation needed|date=August 2016}} Controversy around him aroused in October 2014 when fellow Chicago-bred rapper Young Pappy dissed Dex and Lil Mouse via his viral single, "Killa".{{cite web|title=Young Pappy discography at Discogs|url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/4489969-Young-Pappy|publisher=[[Discogs]]|accessdate=2016-08-07}} May 29, 2015, Pappy was murdered in his hometown during a video shoot. A week after Young Pappy's murder, Dex and a companion released a tribute track in honor of his memory. Then, in 2015, he [[self-released]] his first mixtape, ''Never Seen It Coming''. The mixtape was downloaded below 100,000 times, but its track, "Duh Dummy!", gained popularity.http://indy.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/34702/famous-dex-never-seen-it-coming.html Later that year, he released his second mixtape, ''Dexter's Laboratory''; the title of this mixtape is the same as the critically praised [[Dexter's Laboratory|Cartoon Network original animated television series]].
