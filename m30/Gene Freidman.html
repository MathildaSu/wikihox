Freidman''' is the owner and operator of the largest taxi fleet in New York City. Gene is the sole principal of Taxi Club Management (TCM).{{Cite web|title = About Dispatch Taxi Affiliation Inc Service - Chicago Taxi Service|url = http://www.dispatchtaxi.com/dispatch-taxi-affiliation-inc/|website = DispatchTaxi.com|accessdate = 2015-11-29|language = en-US}} ==Early life and education== Born in the Soviet Union in 1970, Mr. Freidman emigrated from St. Petersburg to the United States as a political refugee in 1976. He attended local public schools in Queens before his admission to Bronx High School of Science, then went on to graduate with a Bachelor of Science in Accounting and Business from Skidmore College in 1992. He received his second degree from Cardozo School of Law, Yeshiva University in New York City and is admitted to practice Law in New York and New Jersey.{{Cite web|title=Homepage - Gene Freidman |url=http://www.genefreidman.com |website=Gene Freidman |accessdate=2015-11-29 |language=en-US |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208094457/http://www.genefreidman.com/ |archivedate=2015-12-08 |df= }} ==Career== Freidman built his father’s taxi fleet into what Freidman calls “the nation’s largest.” He began with 60 yellow cabs, and took the company from a valuation of $2.5 million in 1996 to $120 million in 2009.{{cite web|title = Evgeny Freidman CEO of Taxi Club Management|url = http://www.yellowcabnyctaxi.com/nyc-taxi/evgeny-freidman-ceo-taxi-club-management|website = YellowCabNYC.com|accessdate = 25 October 2015|date = 3 February 2009}} Today his company operates cabs in New York, Chicago, Boston, Philadelphia and New Orleans. In 2013, Freidman reached a settlement with the New York State Attorney General's office and the [[Taxi and Limousine Commission]] over excessive charges to taxi drivers working for three of Freidman's companies. Freidman agreed to pay $750,000 in restitution to drivers and $500,000 in fines.{{cite news|last1=Flegenheimer|first1=Matt|title=Taxi Industry Bids the Bloomberg Era a Not-So-Fond Farewell|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/20/nyregion/taxi-industry-bids-the-bloomberg-era-a-not-so-fond-farewell.html|accessdate=11 June 2017|work=[[New York Times]]|date=19 December 2013}}{{cite news|last1=Donohue|first1=Pete|title=Taxi mogul to pay $1.2 million over allegations he was ripping off cab drivers|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/taxi-mogul-pay-1-2m-alleged-rip-off-article-1.1552626|accessdate=11 June 2017|work=[[New York Daily News]]|issue=10 December 2013}} The Attorney General sued Freidman in April 2015 for failing to live up to the terms of the 2013 settlement and for further violations.{{cite news|last1=Fischer|first1=Ben|title=Schneiderman sues NYC's biggest taxi mogul|url=http://www.bizjournals.com/newyork/news/2015/04/23/ny-ag-schneiderman-sues-evgeny-freidman.html|accessdate=11 June 2017|work=[[New York Business Journal]]|date=April 23 2015}}{{cite web|title=A.G.
