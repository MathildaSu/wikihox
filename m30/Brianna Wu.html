xml:space="preserve" bytes="2260"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Brianna Wu|timestamp=20141013124414|year=2014|month=October|day=13|substed=yes|help=off}} [[File:Brianna Wu.jpg|thumb|right]] '''Brianna Wu''' is an American [[video game developer]], and has worked in politics, as an illustrator, and as a journalist.{{cite news|title=Brianna Wu|url=http://the-magazine.org/author/72|accessdate=12 October 2014|work=The Magazine}} Wu co-founded '''Giant Spacekat''', an [[Independent video game development|indie development studio]], with [[Amanda Stenquist Warner|Amanda Warner]].{{cite news|last1=Starr|first1=Michelle|title=Revolution 60: A game by and about badass women|url=http://www.cnet.com/news/revolution-60-a-game-by-and-about-badass-women/|accessdate=12 October 2014|work=CNet|date=30 July 2014}} Their first game, the sci-fi themed action-adventure game '''''Revolution 60''''', was released in 2014, initially for [[iOS]] devices. Reflecting the all-female development team, the game features an all-female cast. In October 2014, Wu left her home after receiving threats of violence. Personal information about her had previously been made public on the Internet by supporters of [[Gamergate controversy|GamerGate]], an online movement critical of games journalism and associated with the harassment of [[Women and video games|women in video gaming]].{{cite news|last1=McWhertor|first1=Michael|title=Game developer Brianna Wu flees home after death threats, Mass. police investigating|url=http://www.polygon.com/2014/10/11/6963279/brianna-wu-death-threats-police-harassment|accessdate=12 October 2014|date=11 October 2014}} ==External links== *[http://www.revolution60.com/ ''Revolution 60'' website] ==References== {{reflist}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Wu, Brianna}} [[Category:Independent video game developers]] [[Category:Video game development companies]] [[Category:American video game designers]]
