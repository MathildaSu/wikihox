He was selected by the [[Buffalo Sabres]] in the 9th round (169th overall) of the [[1987 NHL Entry Draft]]. Tkachuk played four seasons [[1984–85 WHL season|1984]] - [[1987–88 WHL season|1988]] of major junior hockey with the [[Saskatoon Blades]] of the [[Western Hockey League]], playing 264 games to register 123 goals, 125 assists, and 371 penalty minutes. On February 9, 1988, while playing with the Blades to open the newly constructed [[Saskatchewan Place]], Tkachuk scored the first ever goal on Saskatchewan Place ice, beating [[Brandon Wheat Kings]]' goalie [[George Maneluk]] at 12:35 of the first period.[http://www2.canada.com/saskatoonstarphoenix/sports/story.html?id=0de2e0de-f57d-4a15-b957-40f7d1c28cd3&p=2 Grand opening for Blades in '88] Tkachuk turned professional with the [[1988–89 AHL season|1988–89 ]] [[Rochester Americans]], playing 64 [[American Hockey League]] games in his rookie season.
