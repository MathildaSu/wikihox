Martin''' is an American entrepreneur, founder of the [[Non-profit organisation|non-profit]] CDV – [[Makers of Memories|Children of Domestic Violence Foundation]],{{cite web|author= Jacqueline Cutler |url=http://www.nj.com/entertainment/arts/index.ssf/2014/10/truths_to_liberate_victims_of_domestic_violence.html|title= Truths to liberate victims of domestic violence|publisher= nj.com|date=2014-10-12|accessdate=2014-10-27}} and author of ''Invincible: The 10 Lies You Learn Growing Up With Domestic Violence and the Truths To Set You Free''.{{cite web|author= Brian F. Martin |url= http://www.success.com/article/conquering-negativity-in-10-steps|title= Conquering Negativity in 10 Steps|publisher= Success magazine|date=2014-01-01|accessdate=2014-10-27}} ==Early life & Education== Martin was born in Newark, NJ. His mother worked multiple jobs as a waitress. His father was in jail.{{cite web|author= Marlo Thomas |url= http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/10/03/brian-martin-with-marlo-t_n_5928518.html/|title= Brian Martin with Marlo Thomas|publisher= Huffington Post|date=2014-10-06|accessdate=2014-10-27}}. Martin grew up in a violent home{{cite web|author= Jacqueline Cutler |url=http://media.knssradio.com/a/96661788/being-a-child-of-domestic-violence.htm|title= BEING A CHILD OF DOMESTIC VIOLENCE|publisher= [[KNSS]]|accessdate=2014-10-27}} {{cite web|author= Jacqueline Cutler |url= http://www.nj.com/entertainment/arts/index.ssf/2014/10/truths_to_liberate_victims_of_domestic_violence.html/|title= Truths to liberate victims of domestic violence|publisher= New Jersey On-Line|date=2014-10-06|accessdate=2014-10-27}}.The violence was caused by his mother’s boyfriend. Both Martin’s mother and her boyfriend also came from violent homes but this was something Martin did not know until he was an adult.{{cite web|author= Marlo Thomas |url= http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/10/03/brian-martin-with-marlo-t_n_5928518.html/|title= Brian Martin with Marlo Thomas|publisher= Huffington Post|date=2014-10-06|accessdate=2014-10-24}} He attended Essex County College in Newark, NJ where he graduated with a bachelor's degree and an MBA from Rutgers University{{cite web|author= Brian F. Martin |url= https://www.linkedin.com/profile/view?id=25561555&trk=spm_pic|title= Brian F. Martin: Executive Chairman at BrandShop Digital Commerce|publisher= LinkedIn|date=2014-10-06|accessdate=2014-10-27}}. ==Career== Martin got a job as an assistant brand manager or marketing manager of a large consumer packaged goods company{{cite web|author= Brian F. Martin |url= http://adage.com/article/cmo-strategy/give-a-put-brands-consumers-hands-literally/121254/|title= Give It a Try: Put Brands in Consumers' Hands (Literally)|publisher= Advertising Age|date=2007-10-07|accessdate=2014-10-27}}. In 1999 he started a part time business which became Brand Connections{{cite web|url= http://adage.com/article/talentworks/quit-day-job-start/131219/|title= Don’t Quit Your Day Job To Start Something New|publisher= Advertising Age|date=2008-09-29|accessdate=2014-10-22}}, The company distributed free product samples to people while they were traveling if they forgot something they needed, distributing more than 150 million samples each year in the US and Europe{{cite web|author= Paul F. Nunes and Jeffrey Merrihue |url= http://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/the-continuing-power-of-mass-advertising/|title= The Continuing Power of Mass Advertising |publisher= MIT Sloan Management Review|date=2007-01-01|accessdate=24 October 2014}}{{cite web|author= Maisie McCabe|url= http://www.mediaweek.co.uk/article/1037871/jamie-lindsay-appointed-md-brand-connections|title= Jamie Lindsay appointed MD of Brand Connections|publisher= Media Week|date=2010-10-29|accessdate=2014-10-22}}. Martin sold the company to a private equity firm in 2008 for $150 Million{{cite web|author= Emily Steel|url= http://online.wsj.com/articles/SB121479852948315225|title= Veronis Suhler Stevenson To Announce Stake Deal|publisher= The Wall Street journal|date=2008-06-30|accessdate=2014-10-26}}. He is also the co-founder of several other media companies including Brandshare, Social Media Link and Brandshop{{cite web|author= Susan Slater|url= http://www.virtual-strategy.com/2014/10/15/digital-commerce-can-be-brand-building-and-deliver-great-consumer-experience#axzz3GEsBVJFk|title= Digital Commerce can be Brand Building and Deliver a Great Consumer Experience|publisher= Virtual Strategy Magazine|date=2014-10-15|accessdate=2014-10-26}}{{cite web|author= Brian Martin |url= http://www.crunchbase.com/person/brian-f-martin|title= Brian F. Martin Profile|publisher= Crunchbase|date=2009-10-01|accessdate=2014-10-26}}. In 2007 Martin founded [[Makers of Memories]], which was to become CDV – Children of Domestic Violence{{cite web|author= Julee Morrison|url= http://mommysmemorandum.com/makers-memories-foundation-stop-childhood-exposure-domestic-violence/|title= Makers of Memories Foundation Stop Childhood Exposure to Domestic Violence|publisher= Mommy'S Memorandum|date=2012-10-17|accessdate=2014-10-26}}{{cite web|author= Caroline Abbott|url= http://www.carolineabbott.com/2012/07/domestic-violence-statistics-about-children-for-2011/|title= Domestic Violence Statistics about Children for 2011|publisher= Caroline Abbott|date=2012-07-18|accessdate=2014-10-26}}. He sponsored the first congressional briefing on Capitol Hill to educate policy makers, leaders and the public about the children affected by domestic violence.{{cite web|author= Rep. John Conyers, Jr. (D-Mich.) and Brian Martin|url= http://thehill.com/opinion/op-ed/191773-preventing-domestic-violence-must-begin-with-the-children|title= Preventing domestic violence must begin with the children|publisher= The Hill|date=2011-11-03|accessdate=2014-10-21}} In 2012 Martin & his documentary ''The Children Next Door'' were featured in an episode of [[Dr. Phil (TV series)|Dr. Phil]].{{cite web|author= Dr. Phil|url= http://www.drphil.com/shows/show/1771|title= Shot, Tortured and Left to Die|publisher= CBS|date=2012-03-20|accessdate=2014-10-26}} The award winning documentary was invited to screen in more than 25 film festivals{{cite web|author= Doug Block|url= http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2344458/|title= The Children Next Door|publisher= IMDB|date=2012-10-05|accessdate=2014-10-27}}. In 2013 Martin worked with [[Nickelodeon]] on the Emmy nominated children’s program [[Nick News with Linda Ellerbee]] special ''Family Secrets: When Violence Hits Home''{{cite web|author= THR Staff|url= http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/nick-news-special-shines-light-724362|title= 'Nick News' Special Shines Light on Childhood Domestic Violence |publisher= The Hollywood Reporter|date=2014-08-08|accessdate=2014-10-26}}. In 2014 Martin released his first book, ''Invincible: The 10 Lies You Learn Growing Up with Domestic Violence, and the Truths to Set You Free'' published by [[Penguin Random House]] in October of 2014{{cite web|author= Jacqueline Cutler |url= http://www.nj.com/entertainment/arts/index.ssf/2014/10/truths_to_liberate_victims_of_domestic_violence.html/|title= Truths to liberate victims of domestic violence|publisher= New Jersey On-Line|date=2014-10-06|accessdate=2014-10-27}}, The book reached the [[New York Times Best Seller List|New York Times]],{{cite web|author=The Staff|url= http://www.nytimes.com/best-sellers-books/2014-11-23/advice-how-to-and-miscellaneous/list.html|title= The New York Times Bestseller List |publisher= The New York Times|date=2014-11-23|accessdate=2014-11-25}} [[Los Angeles Times]],{{cite web|author=David Kelly|url=http://www.lapl.org/collections-resources/blogs/central-library/november-23-2014-los-angeles-times-best-sellers|title= November 23, 2014 Los Angeles Times Best Sellers Available at the Library|publisher=Los Angeles Times|date=2014-11-23|accessdate=2014-11-25}} [[USA Today]]{{cite web|url= http://www.usatoday.com/story/life/books/2013/06/21/invincible/2444877/isbn/9780399166570/|title= USA TODAY BEST-SELLING BOOKS|publisher= USA Today|date=2014-10-06|accessdate=2014-10-26}} and [[Publishers Weekly]]{{cite web|url= http://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/print/20141103/64590-this-week-s-bestsellers-november-3-2014.html|title= This Week's Bestsellers: November 3, 2014|publisher=Publishers Weekly|date=2014-10-06|accessdate=2014-10-26}} best seller list.
