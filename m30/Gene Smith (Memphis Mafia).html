[[Benjamin Keough]] | known_for = Member of [[Memphis Mafia]] }} '''Gene Smith''' was a member of the [[Memphis Mafia]] as well as being a life-long friend and cousin of [[Elvis Presley]]. He is often cited in Elvis conspiracy theories. ==Early life== Gene Smith was the son of William Edward Smith and Levalle Smith (Smith being both her [[maiden name]] and married name). Gene's ancestry was primarily a Western European mix: on his mother's side, he was [[Scotch-Irish American|Scots-Irish]], with some [[Normans|French Norman]]; one of Levalle's great-great-grandmothers was [[Cherokee]].{{cite book|last1=Dundy|first1=Elaine|title=Elvis and Gladys (Southern Icons)|date=2004|publisher=University Press of Mississippi|pages=13, 16, 20–22, 26|accessdate=28 September 2014}} Levalle was the sister of Gladys Smith Presley, Elvis' mother.{{cite book|last1=Nash|first1=Alanna|title=Elvis and the Memphis Mafia|date=2012|publisher=Aurum Press|isbn=9781845137595|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=7jDBAgAAQBAJ&pg=PT192&lpg=PT192&dq=junior+smith+and+elvis+cousin&source=bl&ots=Ru9ft24eCf&sig=O8uRWbpO1PH8ebQizWmrvpPsAKw&hl=en&sa=X&ei=ELEoVL_2Mc-coQST0IKYAQ&ved=0CFEQ6AEwCDgK#v=onepage&q=gene%20smith&f=false|accessdate=29 September 2014}} The family was poor enough that Gene dropped out of high school to help support them and took a job at Hall's Grocery as a bicycle delivery boy.{{cite book|last1=Hazen|first1=Cindy|title=Memphis Elvis-Style|date=1997|publisher=John F. Blair, Publisher|isbn=9780895871732|page=26|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=ly8cpb-86bEC&pg=PA180&lpg=PA180&dq=elvis+cousin+gene+smith&source=bl&ots=znxmTol_e8&sig=2qLfdlTXKMwESXAmUCHwQGu8yog&hl=en&sa=X&ei=9aAoVLfSNdCRoQT2vYH4CA&ved=0CB0Q6AEwADge#v=onepage&q=elvis%20cousin%20gene%20smith&f=false|accessdate=29 September 2014}} His older brother was Carroll "Junior" Smith. Junior died in cousin [[Billy Smith (Memphis Mafia)|Billy Smith]]'s bed in 1960.{{cite web|title=Billy Smith, Elvis' cousin and Memphis Mafia member talks candidly to EIN|url=http://www.elvisinfonet.com/interview_billysmith_part1.html|website=Elvis Information Network|accessdate=29 September 2014}} ==Adult life== ===With Elvis=== Smith and Elvis grew up together before the latter was famous, having been born only seven weeks apart. They had a "close friendship" that lasted until 1962, when a misunderstanding had them part ways. Eventually, however, Smith would visit Elvis at Graceland.{{cite news|title=Elvis Presley and the Memphis Mafia|url=http://memphismafia.elvis.com.au/|accessdate=28 September 2014|publisher=Elvis Australia}} Before Elvis started singing professionally, the two worked together at Precision Tool.{{cite news|title=INTERVIEW WITH BILLY SMITH - 12 FEBRUARY 2006|url=http://www.elvis100percent.com/interview120206.htm|accessdate=28 September 2014|publisher=Elvis 100%|date=12 Feb 2006}} After Elvis was popular, however, Smith became a part of the Memphis Mafia and ended up on Elvis' payroll and often traveled with him. One of the roles that Smith performed was that of chauffeur.{{cite web|title=ELVIS' FRIENDS|url=http://www.elvicities.com/~myboymyboy/friends.html|website=Elvicities|accessdate=29 September 2014}} [[Jerry Schilling]] also recounts that Smith acted as gatekeeper to Elvis once many people were clamoring for his attention. Smith adopted a "more businesslike appearance" and vetted those that were trying to get access to Elvis.{{cite book|last1=Schilling|first1=Jerry|title=Me and a Guy Named Elvis: My Lifelong Friendship with Elvis Presley|date=2006|publisher=Penguin|isbn=9781592402311|page=50|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=DjNNT8kiwpgC&pg=PA50&lpg=PA50&dq=gene+smith+elvis+cousin+in+jailhouse+rock&source=bl&ots=BSw8eLWQ2o&sig=Nzk8t7f0Ytq0iWte-Ed1EwhMWdo&hl=en&sa=X&ei=S6QoVJiuJ4vioATF64KQCA&ved=0CD4Q6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=gene%20smith%20elvis%20cousin%20in%20jailhouse%20rock&f=false|accessdate=29 September 2014}} The two were close enough that [[George Klein (disc jockey)|George Klein]] describes their relationship as "so close they had their own private language together that was almost impossible for anyone else to make sense of. Gene wasn't book smart, but he was funny, easygoing and was one of those one of a kind characters Elvis always seemed to enjoy."{{cite book|last1=Klein|first1=George|title=Elvis: My Best Man: Radio Days, Rock 'n' Roll Nights, and My Lifelong Friendship with Elvis Presley|date=2011|publisher=Crown Publishing Group|isbn=9780307452757|page=54|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=HvfQid71l-QC&pg=PA54&lpg=PA54&dq=elvis+cousin+gene+smith&source=bl&ots=OTtoa6Qqcp&sig=Hb3pYVxiB1zVboKt2j3GncpA6v0&hl=en&sa=X&ei=9aAoVLfSNdCRoQT2vYH4CA&ved=0CEsQ6AEwCDge#v=onepage&q=elvis%20cousin%20gene%20smith&f=false|accessdate=29 September 2014}} ===Marriage=== Smith met his wife Louise at a clothing store that she and her mother worked at. Elvis and Smith came in to the store, and both men signed up for credit accounts. Eventually, this led to Smith courting Louise, and when he would pick her up for a date Elvis would also be in tow. Louise recalls they had "no money", and would often find their own entertainment. Eventually the two were "happily married" for 43 years.{{cite book|last1=McDowell|first1=Ronnie|title=Genuine Elvis, The: Photos and Untold Stories about the King|date=2009|publisher=Pelican Publishing|isbn=978-1589806955|pages=13–14|url=http://www.pelicanpub.com/content/9781589806955_ch%201.pdf|accessdate=28 September 2014}} ==Filmography== {| class="wikitable" |- !
