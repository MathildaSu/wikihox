xml:space="preserve" bytes="10443"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Paul Koehler|timestamp=20141213155855|year=2014|month=December|day=13|substed=yes}} {{Infobox musical artist | | name = Paul Koehler | background = non_vocal_instrumentalist | image = Paul Koehler in 2014.jpg | caption = Koehler in 2014 | birth_name = Paul Adam Koehler | birth_date = {{Birth date and age|1983|9|9}} | origin = [[Burlington, Ontario|Burlington]], [[Ontario]], [[Canada]] | years_active = 2000-present | instrument = [[Drums]], [[percussion]], [[guitar]] | genre = [[Post-hardcore]], [[Hardcore punk]] | occupation = Musician, Graphic Designer and Web Designer | label = [[Hopeless Records|Hopeless]], [[Victory Records|Victory]] | associated_acts = [[Silverstein (band)|Silverstein]] | website = [http://www.paulkoehler.net www.paulkoehler.net] | notable_instruments = A custom Udrum drum kit with Meinl Cymbals. }} '''Paul Adam Koehler''' (born September 9, 1983) is a Canadian drummer in the band [[Silverstein (band)|Silverstein]]. He is also involved in several business ventures, most notably a clothing company named Liars! Manifesto. ==Early life== Koehler was born in [[Burlington, Ontario|Burlington]], [[Ontario]]. In 1996, at the age of thirteen, he started playing the drums; primarily self taught, he took various lessons. Koehler plays the drums using [[matched grip]]. He has been playing in bands since he learned to play drums. He started a band with some friends because he wanted to become a famous musician like the bands he was listening to then.{{cite web |url=http://www.silversteinmusic.com/db/paul.html |title=PAUL KOEHLER - DRUMS |accessdate=2008-06-04 |author= Silverstein |date= }} He attended [[Lester B. Pearson High School (Burlington)|Lester B. Pearson High School]] in Burlington, receiving his high school diploma in 2002. Paul then attended [[McMaster University]] to get an engineering degree, but when the band signed with [[Victory Records]] he dropped out to pursue a career in music.{{cite web |url=http://media.www.theconcordian.com/media/storage/paper290/news/2003/10/08/Entertainment/Silverstein.Chooses.Rock.Over.Academics-523396-page2.shtml |title=Silverstein chooses rock over academics |accessdate=2008-06-05 |author= Dalton, Melinda |date=2003-08-03 }} Koehler's influences include [[Gorilla Biscuits]], [[Cave In]], [[Daft Punk]], [[Hayden (musician)|Hayden]], [[Kraftwerk]], [[My Bloody Valentine (band)|My Bloody Valentine]], [[The Cure]], [[The Replacements (band)|The Replacements]] and [[Wilco]].{{cite web |url=http://atominc.blogspot.com/ |title=Favorite Bands of All Time. |accessdate=2008-06-04 |author= Koehler, Paul |date= }} ==Musical career== Koehler is the drummer and founding member for the Canadian rock band [[Silverstein (band)|Silverstein]]. His other bandmates include [[Shane Told]], Bill Hamilton, [[Neil Boshart]](no longer in Silverstein- position now occupied by Paul Marc Rousseau) and [[Josh Bradford]]. He found the rest of the band through the scene.{{cite web |url=http://www.skratchmagazine.com/interviews/int-0407-silverstein.php |title=Silverstein |accessdate=2008-06-04 |author= Pangilinan, Ryan |date= }} Koehler has been with the band since the beginning in February 2000.{{cite news |url=http://www.cbc.ca/arts/music/silverstein.html |title=The Great Unknown |accessdate=2008-06-04 |author= Meyer, Andre |date= 2007-07-10 |work=CBC News }} Koehler's first show with the band was on June 28, 2000 at the Glad Tidings Church in Burlington, Ontario. The line-up for the show was Shane, Josh, Richard McWalter and Noah, who only played that one show with the band. They played five songs during their [[Set list|set]].{{cite web |url=http://phorum.nettwerk.com/silverstein/index.php?topic=119.msg1530#msg1530 |title=First Show Ever |accessdate=2008-06-04 |author= Koehler, Paul |date= 2008-03-02 }} In the music video for "Smile in Your Sleep" on the ''[[Discovering the Waterfront]]'' album, Koehler played Professor Plum for the game [[Cluedo|Clue]], where he is seen holding the rope in the billiard room and then later holding a candle stick in the conservatory. It was filmed in the same mansion as the movie ''[[Billy Madison]]''. Also in the same album for the music video of ''Discovering the Waterfront'', Koehler is the only one not lying down on the ground.{{cite web |url=http://www.straight.com/article-129349/silverstein-sacrifices-for-screamo-s-sake |title=Silverstein sacrifices for screamo's sake |accessdate=2008-06-04 |author= Adams, Gregory |date= 2008-01-24 }} During the recording of ''Arrivals & Departures'', Koehler lived in California with the rest of the band. They recorded the album at Capitol Records, where they were surrounded by other musicians. He is married to Jessica Koehler (née Schnell) and is a self proclaimed to be part of the [[straight edge]] lifestyle and has been since the age of sixteen.{{cite web |url=http://www.unratedmagazine.com/Document.cfm?Page=Articles/index.cfm&Article_ID=562 |title=Interview with Paul Koehler |accessdate=2008-06-04 |author= Biskup, Kristin |date= 2007-11-04 }} ===Activism=== In 2006 Paul went to Capitol Hill to speak about teen suicide. He was joined by two congress members Democrat [[Patrick J. Kennedy]] of Rhode Island and Democrat [[Grace Napolitano]] of California. They asked for government funding for two charities that help prevent teen suicide. He is a [[vegan]] and has been for over 6 years. He enjoys finding new vegetarian food while touring with the band. He was on the ballot for [[PETA]]'s "Sexiest Vegetarian" contest in 2007. In 2007 Brofest a charity for Nicholas Hurlbut hosted by Paul raised $12,301. Nicholas had cancer and later died because of it. ===Companies=== ====Liars Manifesto==== [[Liars! Manifesto]] is a group of friends, Paul Koehler, Danny DelPrincipe and Kenny Fletcher founded in 2006. The three friends design unique apparel for the label, when they are not busy. The company, while still young, states on its website that it has plans for bigger things. Paul is in charge of wholesale orders, advertising and designing the website for the Liars! Manifesto. ====Rock and Roll Design Company==== Rock and Roll Design Company is a website design made by Koehler himself. He creates various web design layouts for various sites many of which are either his or related to his band and clothing line. The websites that give credit to Rock and Roll design are such websites as [[Silverstein (band)|Silverstein]]'s, Brofest (Charity Event), Udrum, Verona Records (Shane Told's Record Company), Invisible Ninjas (Photography) and Paul's Personal Page (The Paul Koehler Network).{{cite web |url=http://paulkoehler.net/ |title=Paul Koehler Net |accessdate=2008-06-04 |author= Koehler, Paul |date= }} He is the [http://www.ymcarocks.com/contact.html Webmaster] for his hometown YMCA, the website of which also has been developed by Rock and Roll Design Company. ====Invisible Ninjas==== The website under the direction of Paul features various portfolios with designs created either by him or his own friends. Paul is listed as a Designer while Silverstein bandmate Billy Hamilton is a Photographer.
