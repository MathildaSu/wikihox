xml:space="preserve" bytes="2804"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Brooke Heller|timestamp=20160812000710|year=2016|month=August|day=12|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Orphan|date=July 2016}} '''Brooke Heller''' is a [[Los Angeles]]-based record executive. As a talent developer, manager and A&R executive, Heller is best known for working with the band [[Neon Trees]].{{cite web |url=http://www.artistdirect.com/artist/brooke-heller/6777022 |website=Artistdirect.com}} ==Biography== Heller was born and raised in [[Berkeley, California]], and later began a career as a student at the [[University of California, Santa Barbara|University of California in Santa Barbara]]. It was during his time in college while pursuing a degree in Psychology that he began to write songs, eventually inking a deal with Warner Chappell and later on becoming a producer and A&R Executive.{{cite web|title=Marissa Renee Signs to Warner Bros Recordds|url=http://rm64.com/2011/01/04/fresh-ink-marissa-renee-signs-to-warner-bros-records/|website=Room Sixty-Four}} == Career == Before becoming an A&R{{cite web|title=WHEELS 2.0 HEADS OUT ON A MIDNIGHT DRIVE|url=http://hitsdailydouble.com/news&id=280956|website=Hits Daily Double}} executive, Heller began his career as a solo artist. He was signed to Artemis Records in 2001 by Daniel Glass (Glassnote Records) and Danny Golberg (Nirvana), releasing a full-length album and his chart-topping hit ‘Coming Home’. His debut single ‘Coming Home’ topped the charts at No.2 in Japan{{cite web|last1=Saraceno|first1=Christina|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/release/coming-home-japan-bonus-tracks-mr0001160621|website=allmusic.com}} in between No Doubt’s ‘Can’t Speak’ and U2’s ‘Elevation’. Heller toured around the country over the next couple years, once for a 5-month stint. Heller then continued on to other outlets in the industry. For 7 years he worked as a composer and music supervisor in film and television for Viacom, VH1, HBO, Showtime, and MTV as composer and music supervisor for multiple shows such as ‘Pimp My Ride’ and the dating show ‘NEXT’. His current development and management company has a full national U.S. and European radio staff who broke the band AWOLNATION{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/275348/awolnation/chart|website=Billboard}} to #1 on Billboard. ==References== {{Reflist}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Heller, Brooke}} {{Improve categories|date=July 2016}} [[Category:American music industry executives]]
