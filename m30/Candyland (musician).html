But in 2014, Ethan Davis officially left the project, therefore transforming Candyland into a solo act, now carried by Martin alone.http://www.youredm.com/2015/04/08/ladies-on-deck-candyland/ ==Early life and beginnings== Josie Martin was born on 17 December 1990 in [[Santa Barbara, California]].http://thedjlist.com/djs/candyland/info/ When she was an infant, she was taken and placed in an infant homeless shelter. This was because her parents were homeless, and unsuitable for the child; with Josie Martin's father being a drug dealer and Martin's mother being a drug abuser who had multiple personality disorder and consistently heard voices.https://m.facebook.com/CandylandDJs/photos/a.225704964126803.62427.183277045036262/1069422456421712/?type=3&source=48&__tn__=E While at the infant homeless shelter, Martin was adopted by a twenty-two year old girl who was working there at the time.https://m.facebook.com/CandylandDJs/photos/a.225704964126803.62427.183277045036262/1069422456421712/?type=3&source=48&__tn__=E As Martin grew up, alongside her younger step-brother, she became very passionate about [[basketball]], and intended on having a career as a basketball player. Though unfortunately, during a summer game in high school, she broke her knee. Subsequently, Martin's plans for basketball had been crushed; and after a short period of depression and confusion, at the age of 16, Martin decided to embark on a musical career as a DJ instead.https://m.facebook.com/CandylandDJs/photos/a.225704964126803.62427.183277045036262/1223616114335678/?type=3&source=48&__tn__=E Josie Martin's first gig was at Ethan Davis' 17th birthday party, which is where the two met.http://dancemusicnw.com/candyland-interview-josie-martin/ From thereon, they decided to collaborate as a duo. A year passed before the duo's first song or official name was created though. {{quotation|When we started we had no intention of making our own music. We were DJing probably about a year before the scene really started to take off. Everyone in town would only let their friends DJ all the good house parties so we decided to just start making our own music so people would ASK us to play.|Candyland (as a duo)http://electricsloth.com/2013/04/interview-candyland-2/}} Soon after, the duo were in search of a name that people could "chant at." In the process, Davis conceived the idea of Candyland; with Martin later commenting that the name "had a good fit" during a recent interview.https://peoplewithcooljobs.com/2015/10/12/josie-martin-candyland-dj/ Candyland's first original track was signed by [[Lazy Rich]] via [[Lazy Rich|Big Fish Recordings]] in 2011. ==Music career== ===2011–2013: Career beginnings and early success=== On 4 July 2011, Candyland's first release, "Hate On This EP," was released via [[Lazy Rich|Big Fish Recordings]]. The ep consisted of the tracks, "Piano Is Sure Neat," the song that caught the attention of the aforementioned record label; and "Hate On This."https://www.beatport.com/release/hate-on-this-ep/390047 Candyland's second release was also an ep entitled, "Animal Instincts." It contained the tracks, "Koyote," "Panda Bear," and a remix of "Koyote" by Alex Mind. The ep was released via Braslive Records on 10 October 2011.https://www.beatport.com/release/animal-instincts/712349 "Animal Instincts" gained support from the DJs [[Dada Life]], Markus Schultz and Far Too Loud.https://m.facebook.com/story.php?story_fbid=267282996635666&id=183277045036262&__tn__=%2As Exactly 14 days later, on 24 October, Candyland released an ep titled, "Starving Cactus" via the record label, Burn The Fire.https://open.spotify.com/album/6AqXF6tvxZdbmpDds13iRd The ep contained the tracks, "Starving Cactus" and "Ode."https://www.beatport.com/release/starving-cactus/817086 On 7 November, Candyland released the single, "Pirates" through Heavy Artillery Recordings. On 5 December, Candyland's remix of Digital Freq and Pyramyth's song, "Together Forever" was featured on the remix ep of the song, marking Candyland's first official remix.https://www.beatport.com/release/together-forever/839322 On 19 December, Candyland returned to Big Fish Recordings for the release of their second ep through that label entitled, "C4/Only Thing I Got." The ep's tracks included, "C4," and "Only Thing I Got." Two additional remixes for "C4" were also included. On 26 December, Candyland released their fourth ep, "Prelude EP" via Play Me Records.http://earmilk.com/2011/12/31/candyland-prelude-ep-play055/ On 10 January 2012, Candyland released their second official remix, which was for Atomic Drop and Patron's single, "Do It Again." The remix was featured on the official remix ep for the track. On 17 February, Candyland released a free download of their collaborative track with [[FTampa]] titled, "Hypnotic," via [[SoundCloud]].https://soundcloud.com/housemusic/hypnotic-by-candyland-ftampa On 1 March, Candyland released a free download of their track with Dirtyrock titled, "Four Loko," also through SoundCloud.http://m.youtube.com/watch?v=1we235YuOrk On 26 March, Candyland released their third official remix, which was of [[Felguk]] and Tim Healey's song, "Rio." The remix was featured on the official remix ep of the track. On 30 April, Candyland was featured on the remix ep for the song, "Jump" by Lazrtag featuring Dell Harris. In June, Candyland won [[Beatport]]'s remix contest for the song, "[[Rattle (song)|Rattle]]" by [[Bingo Players]]. The remix was released via Hysteria Records on 4 June.https://www.beatport.com/release/rattle-candyland-remix/919640 The remix topped the Beatport Dubstep Charts for several weeks. On 4 July, in celebration of their one year anniversary, and having gained 5000 [[Facebook]] fans, Candyland released a free download for their remix of [[Coolio]]'s hit, "[[Gangsta's Paradise]]."http://www.thissongslaps.com/2012/07/gangsters-paradise-candylands-og-remix-bonus/ Later that year, Candyland launched their record label, Sweet Shop Records; with Jay Cosmic being the first artist to sign with label. For [[halloween]], Candyland released a free download of their track, "Killer," with MakO.http://www.edmsauce.com/2012/10/30/candyland-mako-killer-original-mix/ In November, Candyland won Beatport's remix contest for [[Skrillex]] and [[Damian Marley]]'s single, "[[Make It Bun Dem]]." This made Candyland the very first artist to win two remix contests in a row.http://www.ixdaily.com/the-buzz/72f7b1aecbe852b947d118ce48d3b00486ed4539https://www.thebrag.com/music/candyland On 3 December, Candyland joined [[Spinnin' Records]], and released "Castle Of Affair," featuring Peter Dawson. Many unofficial remixes by Candyland were released via SoundCloud that year. In 2013, Candyland performed at sixty-five shows, alongside [[Krewella]] for the Get Wet Tour.https://m.facebook.com/CandylandDJs/photos/a.225704964126803.62427.183277045036262/600277903336172/?type=3&source=48&__tn__=Ehttp://www.lessthan3.com/news/josie-martin-candyland-music-solo On 21 January, Candyland released their ep, "Bring The Rain EP" under Spinnin' Records.http://m.youredm.com/2013/01/21/candyland-bring-the-rain-ep-spinnin-records/ The ep was released exclusively through Beatport, before being available through all outlets. It featured seven tracks: "It's A Shark!," a collaborative work with DJ [[Big Chocolate (musician)|Big Chocolate]]; "Bring The Rain," the ep's title track which features Lexi Forche; "Beneath Myself," which Ethan Davis provides vocals for; "All You Gotta Do," with MakO featuring Maksim; "Castle Of Affair," "Get Wild," and Evan Duffy's piano version of "Bring The Rain."https://www.themusicninja.com/filthy-candyland-bring-the-rain-ep/ One month later, Candyland released a remix for the single, "[[Hello (Stafford Brothers song)|Hello]]" by [[Stafford Brothers]] featuring [[Lil Wayne]] and [[Christina Milian]]. The remix was released with the song's remix ep on 13 February. On 26 February, Candyland's remix for Kill Paris' "Slap Me" was featured on "To A New Earth," a remix compilation by Kill Paris. On 15 March, Candyland was featured on the remix ep for "Stellar" by Daddy's Groove. On 7 May, Candyland released a new song titled, "On The Shore," alongside Clark Kent and Slow Skies.http://www.theuntz.com/news/candyland-and-clark-kent-vs-slow-skies-on-the-shore/ On 8 May, Candyland released a remix of [[Knife Party]]'s single, "LRAD."http://mwr-blog.com/2013/05/08/knife-party-lrad-candylands-og-remix/https://soundcloud.com/candylanddjs/knife-party-lrad-candylands-oghttp://www.thissongslaps.com/2013/05/knife-party-lrad-candylands-og-remix/ The remix was highly approved by Knife Party themselves; where they referred to Candyland's remix as "the best one" during a thread on [[Twitter]].https://m.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=3116356165197&id=1753283057&set=a.1568487029436.46310.1753283057&source=48&__tn__=E On 21 May, Candyland was featured on the remix ep for Topher Jones' song, "Save Me." On 4 June, Candyland independently released the single, "The Secret," with [[Stan SB]], better known as [[Fox Stevenson]]. 100% of the song's proceeds went towards [[One Fund Boston]].http://www.thissongslaps.com/2013/06/candyland-the-secret-feat-stan-sb-original-mix/ Exactly one day later, Candyland was featured on the remix ep for [[Tristam (music producer)|Tristam]]'s song, "The Truth," which was released on 5 June. On 8 July, Candyland returned to Spinnin' Records for the release of their single, "Not Coming Down,"http://earmilk.com/2013/07/09/candyland-zak-waters-not-coming-down/ featuring [[Zak Waters]].http://m.youredm.com/2013/07/09/candyland-zak-waters-not-coming-down-orignal-mix/ On 19 August, Candyland was featured on the remix ep for the song, "[[Legacy (Nicky Romero song)|Legacy]]" by [[Nicky Romero]] and Krewella. On 25 November, Candyland released the single, "Breathless," featuring Michelle Quezada, via their own record label, Sweet Shop Records.http://www.thissongslaps.com/2013/11/candyland-breathless-ft-michelle-quezada/ Over the year, Candyland uploaded a series of free downloads for unofficial remixes they released via SoundCloud. ===2014–2015: Other releases, split up, and Josie Martin's solo career as ''Candyland''=== On 28 March 2014, Candyland released a remix ep for their song, "Not Coming Down" with Zak Waters. The ep was released via Spinnin' Records, and contained remixes by the DJs: 28 Mansions, Adrien Mezsi, Boney, Kaj Melsen, REVOKE and Zak Waters.http://m.youredm.com/2014/04/03/candyland-zak-waters-coming-remixes-spinnin/ On 17 June, Candyland's remix for "Overtime" was featured on [[Cash Cash]]'s remix compilation of the same name.https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/overtime-ep-remixes/id886927240 On 22 July, Candyland released a remix with REVOKE of the song, "Gold"https://soundcloud.com/candylanddjs/gold-candyland-revoke-remix-1 by [[Adventure Club]] featuring [[Yuna (singer)|Yuna]].http://m.youredm.com/2014/07/24/adventure-club-gold-candyland-revoke-remix-free-download/ On 9 September, Candyland released the song, "Find Someone"http://www.theuntz.com/news/candyland-collab-with-breathe-carolina-on-find-someone-in-advance-of-the-friend-zone-tour/ in collaboration with the band, [[Breathe Carolina]],http://www.edmtunes.com/2014/09/candyland-breathe-carolina-find-someone/ right before embarking on their Friendzone Tour with the aforementioned band.http://friendzonetour.com/http://m.youredm.com/2014/09/09/candyland-presents-friend-zone-tour-breathe-carolina-drop-new-track-find-someone/ On 15 September, Candyland released a remix ep for their song with Michelle Quezada, titled, "Breathless."http://www.onlythebeat.com/dubstep/2014/09/19/candylands-new-remix-pack-will-leave-breathless/ The ep was released through Candyland's own record label, Sweet Shop Records, and it featured remixes by: Amenzo, Far Too Loud, Kaj Melsen, Regulators, Synchronice, The Kingdumb and Victor Niglio.http://m.youredm.com/2014/09/16/candyland-breathless-remix-ep-sweet-shop-records/ Soon after, it was announced that Ethan Davis had officially left the Candyland project to pursue other ideas.
