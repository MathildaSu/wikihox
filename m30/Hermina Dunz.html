xml:space="preserve" bytes="2411"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Hermina Dunz|timestamp=20141118091137|year=2014|month=November|day=18|substed=yes|help=off}} {{multiple issues| {{notability|date=April 2013}} {{refimprove|date=April 2013}} }} '''Hermina Dunz''' (February 24, 1898 – June 14, 2008) was an [[Austria]]n [[supercentenarian]] who was the oldest living person in the country of [[Austria]] at the time of her death in 2008 at the age of 110.{{cite news |first=|last=|title=Austria's oldest woman, Hermina Dunz, has died at age 110 |url=http://www.iht.com/articles/ap/2008/06/19/europe/EU-GEN-Austria-Obit-Oldest-Woman.php|work= [[Associated Press]] |publisher=[[International Herald Tribune]] |date=2008-06-19 |accessdate=2008-07-16}} Dunz was born on February 24, 1898, in the city of [[Zagreb]], which at the time was located in the [[Austro-Hungarian Empire]], but is currently the capital city of [[Croatia]]. She settled in present-day [[Austria]] and gained Austrian [[citizenship]]. She never had children. Dunz lived independently for most of her life, only moving to a [[retirement home]] when she was 101 years old. She celebrated her 110th birthday with a party in the city of [[Graz, Austria]], in February 2008. Her party was attended by Graz Mayor [[Siegfried Nagl]], who was quoted as saying at the time that Dunz was in good shape for a woman of her age.
