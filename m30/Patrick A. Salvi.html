Salvi ''' is an American trial lawyer and sports entrepreneur who lives and works in the [[Chicago]] area. He is the managing equity partner at Salvi, Schostok & Pritchard, a personal injury law firm with offices in Chicago and [[Waukegan]], Illinois.{{cite news|last1=Robert|first1=Amanda|title=Busy Lawyers Organize Lives in Different Ways|url=http://www.salvilaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/salvi-daily-law-bulletin2011.pdf|accessdate=16 April 2015|work=Chicago Daily Law Bulletin|date=23 April 2011}}{{cite web|url=http://www.avvo.com/attorneys/60602-il-patrick-salvi-1256602.html |title=Lawyer Patrick Salvi - Chicago Attorney |publisher=Avvo.com |date= |accessdate=2014-03-04}} ==Education== Salvi attended St. Mary’s University in Winona, MN, where he received a B.A. degree in political science in 1975.{{cite web|url=http://www.bestlawyers.com/lawyers/patrick-a-salvi/40152/ |title=Patrick A. Salvi - Best Lawyers Lawyer Profile |publisher=Bestlawyers.com |date= |accessdate=2014-03-04}} He then attended the University of Notre Dame Law School, where he received his J.D. degree in 1978. He has continued his relationship with both schools, serving as a member of the board of trustees at St. Mary’s and as chairman of the Law School Advisory Council. ==Legal career== After receiving his J.D., Salvi worked in a general law practice with his father, Albert S. Salvi, for four years prior to creating the firm of Salvi, Schostok & Pritchard which represents [[plaintiffs]] in cases involving serious [[personal injury]], [[medical malpractice]], and [[wrongful death]]. Under his leadership, Salvi, Schostok & Pritchard has achieved numerous seven- and eight-figure verdicts and settlements over the years on behalf of their clients. In addition to his law practice, Salvi is an adjunct law professor at the University of Notre Dame Law School, where he has taught a course in personal-injury litigation since 2008.{{cite web|title=Patrick Salvi|url=http://law.nd.edu/directory/patrick-salvi/|publisher=University of Notre Dame|accessdate=23 April 2015}} ==Notable cases== Salvi has been involved in a number of high-profile cases including, * [[Jim Dooley]] was a former [[Chicago Bears]] player who died of [[Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis]].
