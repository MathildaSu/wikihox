Rebecca Mary Few Brown |spouse= |issue= }} '''George John Godolphin Spencer-Churchill, Marquess of Blandford''' (born 28 July 1992), styled as the '''Earl of Sunderland''' until 2014, is an [[England|English]] [[Aristocracy (class)|aristocrat]]. He is the heir apparent to the [[Duke of Marlborough (title)|Dukedom of Marlborough]]. He is the son of [[Jamie Spencer-Churchill, 12th Duke of Marlborough]] and his first wife, Rebecca Mary Few Brown. Lord Blandford is also a distant relative of the [[conservative]] wartime prime minister [[Winston Churchill|Sir Winston Churchill]] (first cousin of his [[Charles Spencer-Churchill, 9th Duke of Marlborough|great-great-grandfather]]) and of [[Diana, Princess of Wales]] (his 4th cousin once removed). He is also a descendant of [[Consuelo Vanderbilt]]. As his father has had a checkered past, including serving time in prison for forging prescriptions and road rage, Lord Blandford was announced as the financial beneficiary of his grandfather's estate. His inheritance includes the spectacular [[Blenheim Palace]], a 187-room mansion set on 2,000 acres in [[Oxfordshire]].{{cite journal|title=Magnificent Obsession|url=http://www.vanityfair.com/news/2011/06/blenheim-palace-201106|accessdate=2 April 2015|work=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]|date=June 2011}}{{cite news|last1=Roy|first1=Amit|title=Empire strikes back at 'racist' aristocrat|url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1070813/asp/nation/story_8186676.asp|accessdate=2 April 2015|work=The India Telegraph|date=13 August 2007}} Lord Blandford was educated at [[Harrow School]] where he was captain of the school polo team.
