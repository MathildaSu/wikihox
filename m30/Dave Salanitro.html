He is also the founder and CEO of '''Oh By Artifacts''' {{cite web|url=https://www.crunchbase.com/person/dave-salanitro#/entity |title= DAVE SALANITRO |work= Dave Salanitro |publisher=Crunchbase}} {{cite web|url=https://www.behance.net/davidsalanitro |title= DAVE SALANITRO |work= Dave Salanitro |publisher=Behance}} {{cite web|url= http://www.howdesign.com/design-business/self-promotion/bestselfpromos|title= DAVE SALANITRO |work= Dave Salanitro |publisher=HOW Magazine}} {{Citation|article=50-Plus Years of GDUSA People to Watch| title=Graphic Design USA |access-date=July 8, 2017 |url=http://gdusa.com/resources/50-years-of-gdusa-people-to-watch/}} ==Early Life== ===Education=== Salanitro attended [[California State University, Fresno]] and [[Fresno Pacific University]] where he studied Liberal Arts Studies. ==Career== ===Architect=== Salanitro finished his sixth semester at [[Fresno State University]], and continued working for his father, drafting construction drawings for restaurant interiors. In 1987, he parlayed an internship with the [[Fresno Art Museum]] and later became the Designer of Exhibitions and given governance over the galleries, collaborating with the executive director when the museum curated unique exhibitions. It was in this capacity that Salanitro designed his first book, ''The DEJUNA Set'' Wayne Papers (Collection 562)., UCLA Library Special Collections, Charles E. Young Research Library, UCLA,'' Subseries 2: Exhibits 1981-1991, June. ===Designer=== Salanitro started his career as a graphic designer working in production Publish magazine. {{Citation | author=Krasne, Alexandra| article=Designing with Attitude | title=Micro Publishing News | publication-date=May 1999 | publisher=Cygnus Business Media}}. {{Citation | author=Baggerman, Lisa | article=7 Red-Hot Firms to Watch | title=HOW | publication-date=December 1998 | publisher=F + W Media | issn=0886-0483}} ===Oh Boy Artifact=== Salanitro founded '''Oh Boy Artifact.''' in June of 1994. He is the recipient of numerous ADDYs, including the National Silver prize for Oh Boy Artifacts, for which he was also awarded 10 Best in Show Honors—including Best New Collection at Oh Boy Artifacts’ New York debut, and two at its London debut. In 2000 he took home five of the seven commendations, among them Best in Show, the Gold, and the Silver awards at the San Francisco Ad Club’s San Francisco Show. Salanitro’s work has been featured in 116 national and international dailies, periodicals, and volumes.
