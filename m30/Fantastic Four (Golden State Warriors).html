The fantastic four consists of [[Stephen Curry]], [[Klay Thompson]], [[Draymond Green]], and [[Kevin Durant]]. The fantastic four started off with just Stephen Curry at the start of the [[2009-10 NBA season]] until two seasons later, the Warriors drafted Klay Thompson in the first round of the [[2011 NBA Draft]], which would then form the "Splash Bros" and then, the Warriors would draft Draymond Green in the second round of the [[2012 NBA Draft]] to form the "Big Three", who would play together for four seasons and make the playoffs all four seasons playing together, including leading the Warriors to two trips to the [[NBA Finals]] and an NBA Championship in [[2015 NBA Finals|2015]], before the Warriors signed Kevin Durant to officially form the "Fantastic Four". ==Formation== ===Stephen Curry (2009-2011)=== The first of the "fantastic four" to join the Golden State Warriors was Stephen Curry, who was selected in the first round by the Warriors with the seventh overall pick in the [[2009 NBA Draft]]. Stephen Curry had a strong rookie year as he averaged 17.5 points, 5.9 assists, and 1.9 steals per game{{cite web|title=Stephen Curry NBA Stats|url=http://www.basketball-reference.com/players/c/curryst01.html|publisher=Basketball Reference|accessdate=August 2, 2016}} and finished second in [[NBA Rookie of the Year Award|NBA Rookie of the Year]] voting to [[Tyreke Evans]] of the [[Sacramento Kings]] and was named to the [[NBA All-Rookie Team|NBA All-Rookie First Team]].{{cite web|title=Kings' Evans takes home T-Mobile Rookie of Year award|url=http://www.nba.com/2010/news/04/29/ROY.release/|publisher=NBA Official Website|accessdate=August 2, 2016}} Stephen Curry was also strong in his sophomore season as he averaged 18.6 points, 5.8 assists, and 1.5 steals per game and shot a league-best 93.4 percent at the free throw line, and he also won the 2011 [[NBA Sportsmanship Award]].{{cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/warriors/news/curry_sportsmanship_050511.html |title=Stephen Curry Wins 2010–11 NBA Sportsmanship Award |work=NBA.com |date=May 5, 2011 |accessdate=August 2, 2016}} Despite his strong efforts, Stephen Curry was not able to get the Warriors to the playoffs in his first two seasons with the Warriors. ===Add Klay Thompson - "Splash Bros" (2011-2012)=== The Warriors deafted Klay Thompson with the 11th overall pick in the 2011 NBA Draft, thus forming what would be later known as the "[[Splash Brothers|Splash Bros]]". Klay Thompson really grew up in his rookie season but the two of them could not get the Warriors in the playoffs in their first season together as Stephen Curry had a injury-riddled season that year. ===Add Draymond Green - "Big Three" (2012-2016)=== The Warriors made big improvements for the [[2012-13 NBA season]] as they drafted [[Harrison Barnes]] and [[Festus Ezeli]] in the first round of the 2012 NBA Draft, but the biggest addition came when they drafted [[Draymond Green]] in the second round of the 2012 NBA Draft and traded for [[Andrew Bogut]] from the [[Milwaukee Bucks]], thus beginning to form a squad that would dominate in the NBA lead by the "Big Three" of Stephen Curry, Klay Thompson, and Draymond Green. The "Big Three" were able to lead the Warriors to the playoffs for the first time since [[2007 NBA Playoffs|2007]] and just the second time since [[1994 NBA Playoffs|1994]]. Other highlights of the sea on included Stephen Curry setting an NBA record for single-season three-pointers with 272 and the "Splash Bros" combining for a combined-NBA record of 483 three-pointers that season. Klay Thompson got the playoff push started for the Warriors with 22 points in Game 1, but the Warriors fell to the [[Denver Nuggets]] 97-95. Stephen Curry would carry the Warriors through the rest of the first round as he had 30 points in Game 2, 29 points in Game 3, 31 points in Game 4, and 22 points in Game 5 to lift the Warriors past the Nuggets in a six-game series. Stephen Curry would get off to a stung start in the second round playoff series against the [[San Antonio Spurs]] with 44 points in Game 1, but the Warriors fell to the Spurs in double overtime by a score of 129-127. Klay Thompson would lift the Warrors in Game 2 with a 34-point performance to lead the Warriors to a 100-91 win in Game 2. The Spurs would go on to beat the Warriors in six games, thus knocking the Warriors out of the playoffs. The Warriors took more steps toward rebuilding their dominant roster [[2013-14 NBA season|the following season]] be signing Denver Nuggets swingman [[Andre Iguodala]] and big man [[Marreese Speights]]. Despite being plagued by injuries early on, the Warriors bounced back in the second half of the season and the "Big There" led the Warriors to the playoffs as the #6 seed.
