xml:space="preserve" bytes="1405"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=James Barth|timestamp=20160318111524|year=2016|month=March|day=18|substed=yes|help=off}} {{notability|biographies|date=March 2016}} '''James Barth''' is an American audio engineer and music producer/composer. Born in Tulsa Oklahoma, Barth moved to Los Angeles after high school with his band-mates [[Bill Pitcock]], [[Phil Seymour]] and [[Dwight Twilley]] in hopes of starting a career in the music business. Working with the [[Dwight Twilley Band]]{{cite news|url=http://www.okmag.com/blog/2014/11/25/always-on-the-mind/|title=Always on the Mind - Tulsa power-pop stalwart Dwight Twilley talks nuance with a new album.|last=Wooley|first=John|date=2014-11-25|work=Oklahoma Magazine|publisher=Schuman Publishing|accessdate=18 March 2016}} at [[Shelter Records]] studios, Barth was an interracial part of the bands early days. Barth's recording credits include The Dwight Twilley Band, Phil Seymour, [[The Bridge Climbers]], [[Dick Simms]] and others. Barth arranged and recorded strings for recording artist Dwight Twilley, [[Dwight Yoakam]], [[Heather Myles]] and Dick Simms.
