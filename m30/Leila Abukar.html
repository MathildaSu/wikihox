ليلى أبو بكر | image = | imagesize = | caption = | birth_date = {{Birth based on age as of date|40|2015|01|11}} | birth_place = [[Somalia]] | occupation = political activist | ethnicity = [[Somali people|Somali]] | nationality = | title = | alma_mater = [[University of Queensland]] | religion = [[Islam]] }} '''Leila Abukar''' ({{lang-so|''Leyla Abukar''}}, {{lang-ar|ليلى أبو بكر}}) is a [[Somali people|Somali]]-[[Australia]]n political activist. ==Personal life== Abukar was born between 1974 and 1975 in [[Somalia]].{{cite news|title=Precious thing: take part in this special project|url=http://www.theage.com.au/it-pro/precious-thing-take-part-in-this-special-project-20140730-zy9ov.html|accessdate=19 January 2015|agency=The Age|date=30 July 2014}}{{cite news|title=LNP candidate Leila Abukar recounts her harrowing life in Somalia and why she's running for a seat|url=http://www.couriermail.com.au/news/lnp-candidate-leila-abukar-recounts-her-harrowing-life-in-somalia-and-why-shes-running-for-a-seat/story-fnihsrk2-1227180788658?nk=092974190d9884ad35bee6e3ee607f90|accessdate=19 January 2015|agency=The Courier-Mail|date=11 January 2015}} She attended a private school in [[Mogadishu]].{{cite news|title=Somalia: Harrowing journey to reach safe haven|url=http://www.smh.com.au/national/people/somalia-harrowing-journey-to-reach-safe-haven-20140730-zy7a9.html|accessdate=19 January 2015|agency=Sydney Morning Herald|date=30 July 2014}} When the [[Somali Civil War|civil war]] broke out in 1991, her father and eldest brother were killed by militants. Abukar's mother subsequently took the family to a UN asylum center in [[Kenya]]. The teenager therein volunteered as an office interpreter, teaching children and raising awareness on reproductive health. In 1997, Abukar moved alone to [[Australia]] through a women-at-risk program run by the Australian High Commission. [[Soroptimist International]] group workers helped her settle in to her new surroundings in [[Moorooka]], [[Brisbane]], with her family later joining her. For her tertiary education, Abukar earned a Bachelor's degree in International Relations from the [[University of Queensland]]. She also holds a post-graduate diploma and Master's degree in the discipline. Additionally, she has qualifications in Disability Support and Conflict Resolution.{{cite web|title=Leila Abukar is the Liberal National candidate for Yeerongpilly|url=http://www.crescentsofbrisbane.org/Newsletter/CCN0494.asp#Leila_Abukar_is_the_Liberal_National_candidate_for_Yeerongpilly|publisher=Crescent Community News|accessdate=19 January 2015}} Abukar is [[Muslim]].{{cite news|title=Candidate's war-torn past|url=http://www.northweststar.com.au/story/2832857/candidates-war-torn-past/|accessdate=15 February 2015|agency=North West Star|date=21 January 2015}} She is multilingual, speaking seven languages. A mother of two children,{{cite web|title=Faces of Brisbane - Leila Abukar from Somalia|url=http://blogs.abc.net.au/queensland/2010/07/faces-of-brisbane---leila-abukar-from-somalia.html?site=brisbane&program=612_faces_of_brisbane|publisher=ABC Brisbane|accessdate=19 January 2015}} she and her family reside in the Brisbane suburb of [[Yeerongpilly, Queensland|Yeerongpilly]]. ==Career== Abukar began her career as an interpreter for the [[Department of Immigration and Border Protection|Australian Department of Immigration]]. She has significant experience as a Community Advocate, working in a broad range of senior management posts with government, council and non-profit organizations. In addition, Abukar was previously a member of the Queensland Multicultural Roundtable, the Refugees Resettlement Advisory Council, and the Council for Multicultural Australia. She also campaigned against [[female genital mutilation]].{{cite news|title=Who cries for those who don't come by boat?|url=http://www.smh.com.au/action/printArticle?id=60386762|accessdate=15 February 2015|agency=Sydney Morning Herald|date=30 July 2014}} In 2014-2015, Abukar ran for office as the [[Liberal National Party of Queensland]] candidate for the [[Electoral district of Yeerongpilly|Yeerongpilly electoral district]]. She vied for the seat in a field of four other aspirants.{{cite web|title=Yeerongpilly (Key Seat)|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/qld-election-2015/guide/yeer/|publisher=ABC|accessdate=19 January 2015}} If elected, she would have become the first Somali and Muslim to serve in the Queensland Parliament.{{cite news|title=Queensland may elect its first Muslim MP|url=http://www.sbs.com.au/news/article/2015/01/29/queensland-may-elect-its-first-muslim-mp|accessdate=3 February 2015|agency=SBS|date=30 January 2015}} Abukar came in second place, earning 31.4% of the total vote in the first round run-off and 36.7% in the final round against the eventual winner [[Mark Bailey (politician)|Mark Bailey]] of the [[Australian Labor Party]]. ==Awards== Abukar has received various awards and recognition for her societal work. In 2001, she was presented a [[Centenary Medal]] by the Australian government for her contributions to Crisis Counselling, Advocacy and Settlement vis-a-vis the Somali community and other immigrant populations in [[Queensland]].{{cite web|title=Centenary Medal - ABUKAR, Leila Mohammed|url=http://www.itsanhonour.gov.au/honours/honour_roll/search.cfm?aus_award_id=1119315&search_type=simple&showInd=true|publisher=Australian Government|accessdate=3 February 2015}} ==References== {{Reflist|2}} ==External links== *[http://www.tallyroom.com.au/qld2015/yeerongpilly2015 The Tally Room – Yeerongpilly – Queensland 2015] {{Persondata | NAME = Abukar, Leila | ALTERNATIVE NAMES = | SHORT DESCRIPTION = Somali political activist | DATE OF BIRTH = 1974/1975 | PLACE OF BIRTH = Somalia | DATE OF DEATH = | PLACE OF DEATH = }} {{DEFAULTSORT:Abukar, Leila}} [[Category:1970s births]] [[Category:Living people]] [[Category:Ethnic Somali people]] [[Category:Somalian activists]] [[Category:University of Queensland alumni]] [[Category:Somalian emigrants to Australia]]
