xml:space="preserve" bytes="6385"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Kim Bong-han|timestamp=20150702075636|year=2015|month=July|day=2|substed=yes|help=off}} '''Bong-Han Kim''' (born 1916) was a [[North Korea]]n medical surgeon at [[Pyongyang Medical University]] and Kyung-Rak institute. He is recognized to have discovered the so called [[primo-vascular system]] in five research reports that were published during the early 1960s.{{cite journal|last=Soh|first=Kwang-Sup|author2=Kang, Kyung A. |author3=Ryu, Yeon Hee |title=50 Years of Bong-Han Theory and 10 Years of Primo Vascular System|journal=''[[Evidence-based Complementary and Alternative Medicine]]''|date=1 January 2013|volume=2013|pages=1–12|doi=10.1155/2013/587827|url=http://www.hindawi.com/journals/ecam/2013/587827/}}{{cite journal|last=Liu|first=Jun-Ling|author2=Jing, Xiang-Hong |author3=Shi, Hong |author4=Chen, Shu-Ping |author5=He, Wei |author6=Bai, Wan-Zhu |author7= Zhu, Bing |title=Historical Review about Research on "Bonghan System" in China|journal=''[[Evidence-based Complementary and Alternative Medicine]]''|date=1 January 2013|volume=2013|pages=1–7|url=http://www.hindawi.com/journals/ecam/2013/636081/|doi=10.1155/2013/636081}} == Early life and education == Bong-Han Kim was born in 1916.{{cite book|author=Kwang-Sup Soh, Kyung A. Kang, David K. Harrison,|title=The primo vascular system : its role in cancer and regeneration|year=2012|publisher=[[Springer Science+Business Media|Springer]]|location=New York|isbn=978-1-4614-0600-6|pages=3–5|url=http://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/978-1-4614-0601-3_1.pdf|edition=1.|doi=10.1007/978-1-4614-0601-3_1}} He obtained his medical degree from [[Seoul National University]] in 1946. After the [[Korean War]] broke out, Dr. Kim, who was a physiologist based in South Korea, crossed over to North Korea,{{cite journal|last=Kim|first=Hoon-Gi|title=Formative Research on the Primo Vascular System and Acceptance by the Korean Scientific Community: The Gap Between Creative Basic Science and Practical Convergence Technology|journal=Journal of Acupuncture and Meridian Studies|volume=6|issue=6|pages=319–30|date=2013|doi=10.1016/j.jams.2013.04.001|pmid=24290796|url=http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2005290113000897}} leaving his family behind.{{cite journal|last=Kang|first=Kyung Aih|title=Historical Observations on the Half-Century Freeze in Research between the Bonghan System and the Primo Vascular System|journal=Journal of Acupuncture and Meridian Studies|volume=6|issue=6|pages=285–92|date=2013|doi=10.1016/j.jams.2013.07.004|pmid=24290792|url=http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2005290113001556}} Prior to his arrival in North Korea, B.H. Kim was affiliated with the [[Korea Democratic Party]]. == Primo-vascular system == While working as Director of [[North Korea]]'s Kyung-Rak institute (KRI) from 1962 to 1965, B.H. Kim published five articles in the ''Journal of Jo Sun Medicine'' about [[acupuncture]], the Kyungrak system, and the "Sanal" theory. These articles form the basis of the proposed [[primo-vascular system]], which attracted some interest in the early 2010s.{{cite book|author=Kwang-Sup Soh, Kyung A. Kang, David K. Harrison,|title=The primo vascular system : its role in cancer and regeneration|publisher=[[Springer Science+Business Media|Springer]]|location=New York|isbn=978-1-4614-0600-6|pages=7–17|url=http://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007%2F978-1-4614-0601-3_2.pdf|edition=1.}} The [[North Korean government]] supported Kim's research by supplying his team with various analytical instruments such as [[microscope]]s and [[radioactive tracer]]s, most of which were imported from [[Eastern Europe]]. == Disappearance == In 1966, the Kyung-Rak research institute was shut down.
