xml:space="preserve" bytes="3162"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Jesse Rice|timestamp=20161019213435|year=2016|month=October|day=19|substed=yes|help=off}} '''Jesse Rice''' is an American singer-songwriter and producer.{{cite web|url=https://www.sesac.com/Events/Event_News_Details.aspx?id=1985|title=SESAC Celebrates at 2013 Nashville Music Awards|publisher=}} Rice co-wrote the multi-platinum, award- winning #1 song "[[Cruise (song)|Cruise]]" by [[Florida Georgia Line]], which was named Single of the Year in 2013 by the [[Country Music Association]] and the [[Academy of Country Music Awards|Academy Of Country Music]], as well as Country Song of the Year at the 2013 [[Billboard Music Awards]]. {{cite web|url=http://www.cmaworld.com/cma-awards/2013/11/07/winners-announced-47th-annual-cma-awards/|title=Winners Announced for The 47th Annual CMA Awards - 2016 CMA Awards|date=7 November 2013|publisher=}} "Cruise" has sold over 10 million copies, making it the highest-selling country music song of all time and the only country song to receive a Diamond certification by the [[RIAA]].{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/7318809/florida-georgia-lines-cruise-reaches-riaa-diamond-status|title=Florida Georgia Line's 'Cruise' Reaches RIAA Diamond Status|publisher=}} "Cruise" sat at #1 on the [[Billboard charts|Billboard]] Country charts for a record 27 weeks and was re-mixed and recorded by the rap artist [[Nelly]], whose version went to becone a Top 5 hit on the [[Billboard Music Charts|Billboard]] Pop charts.{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/5862305/florida-georgia-line-cruise-sets-all-time-country-sales-record|title=Florida Georgia Line's 'Cruise' Sets All-Time Country Sales Record|publisher=}} Rice received the Song Of the Year award for "Cruise" at the 2013 [[SESAC]] Music Awards.{{cite web|url=https://www.sesac.com/News/News_Details.aspx?id=1989|title=SESAC at CMA Awards|publisher=}} Other songs written by Rice include the [[Colt Ford]] single "[[The High Life (song)|The High Life]]", "Whoa" from the [[Chase Rice]] album ''[[Ignite the Night]]'', the [[Kristian Bush]] single "Light Me Up" from his solo album ''[[Southern Gravity]]'', and the song "While He's Still Around" from the upcoming Florida Georgia Line album ''[[Dig Your Roots]]''. Rice moved to [[Nashville]] in 2003, where he interned for [[EMI Music Publishing]] before signing his first publishing deal with Artist Revolution Publishing in 2007. He is now signed to a worldwide publishing deal with Ole Music Publishing.{{cite web|url=http://www.majorlyindie.com/roster.php?ContributorID=107|title=ole - Roster|publisher=}} ==References== {{reflist}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Rice, Jesse}} [[Category:American singer-songwriters]] [[Category:American record producers]]
