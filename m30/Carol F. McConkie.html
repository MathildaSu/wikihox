Cook]] | successor1 = | start_date1 = April 2013 | end_date1 = | end_reason1 = }} '''Carol Foley McConkie''' (born April 23, 1952) has been the first counselor in the general presidency of the [[Young Women (organization)|Young Women organization]] of [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints]] (LDS Church) since April 2013. Carol Foley was born in [[Spokane, Washington]]. Her parents converted to the LDS Church in [[Delaware]] when she was a toddler. She was raised in Delaware, [[New York]], and [[Arizona]]. She has a degree in English education from [[Arizona State University]], where she met her husband Oscar W. McConkie III. The McConkies were married in the [[Mesa Arizona Temple]] in 1973 and are the parents of seven children. From 2005 to 2008, McConkie served with her husband while he was [[mission president|president]] of the church's [[California]] [[San Jose, California|San Jose]] [[Mission (LDS Church)|Mission]]. McConkie has been a member of the general board of the Young Women and has been a local leader in [[ward (LDS Church)|ward]] and [[stake (Latter Day Saints)|stake]] Young Women, [[Relief Society]], and [[Primary (LDS Church)|Primary]] organizations. In April 2013, McConkie was called as first counselor to [[Bonnie L. Oscarson]] in the Young Women general presidency. ==See also== * [[Oscar W.
