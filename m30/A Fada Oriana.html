xml:space="preserve" bytes="1525"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=A Fada Oriana|timestamp=20150711131039|year=2015|month=July|day=11|substed=yes|help=off}} {{notability|books|date=December 2009}} '''''A Fada Oriana''''' ({{OCLC|4063855}}), [[Portuguese language|Portuguese]] for ''The Fairy [[Oriana]]'', is one of the most emblematic [[Children's literature|children's book]] of [[Portuguese literature]], written in 1958 by [[Sophia de Mello Breyner Andresen]]. The main character is a [[fairy]] who was given the responsibility of ensuring the well-being of a [[forest]] and its inhabitants, the animals and plants. After a while, Oriana befriends a nefarious fish, and while contemplating her image reflected in the river, Oriana is mesmerized by the fish. Deceived by the words of this fish, Oriana ends up neglecting and abandoning the forest, with serious consequences for its denizens. Deprived of her powers as punishment for this failure, Oriana begins a journey of self-consciousness to repair the evils caused by her omission. ==References== {{unreferenced|article|date=December 2009}} {{DEFAULTSORT:Fada Oriana}} [[Category:Children's fiction books]] [[Category:Portuguese literature]] [[Category:1958 books]] {{Child-picture-book-stub}} {{Portugal-stub}}
