Flash mobs are organized events that surprise and entertain onlookers when participants unsuspectingly breakout into a choreographed dance number and then quickly disperse.{{cite news | title = Are 'Flash Robs' Giving Flash Mobs a Bad Rap? | publisher = '[[Time (magazine)|Time Magazine]]'' | date= 2011-08-17 | url = http://newsfeed.time.com/2011/08/17/are-flash-robs-giving-flash-mobs-a-bad-rap/ | accessdate = 2011-08-18 | first=Madison | last=Gray}} Flash Mob America’s purpose is to “create joy through surprise and give back to the community by bringing everyone closer together.” FMA has produced flash mobs for such clients as [[American Red Cross]], [[Gay & Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation|GLAAD]], [[Heal the Bay]], [[Dell]], [[Oscar Mayer]], [[Suave]], [[Rachael Ray]], ''[[The Bachelorette]]'', and ''[[Modern Family]]''.{{cite news | title = Flash Mob America Creates Joy Through Surprise | publisher = ''The Morton Report'' | date= 2011-06-03 | url = http://www.themortonreport.com/entertainment/television/flash-mob-america-creates-joy-through-surprise/ | accessdate = 2011-08-18 | first=Mindy | last=Peterson}} ==History== Inspired by [[Michael Jackson|Michael Jackson’s]] emotional memorial, Brooks and Lawrence produced their first flash mob in July 2009 when they recreated Sweden’s “[[Beat It]]” flash mob.
