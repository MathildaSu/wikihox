xml:space="preserve" bytes="9199"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Assassination threats against Donald Trump|timestamp=20160816035846|year=2016|month=August|day=16|substed=yes|help=off}} [[Donald Trump]] has faced threats of—and in some cases, attempts at—assassination since he announced [[Donald Trump presidential campaign, 2016|his campaign]] to run for [[President of the United States]]. ==Assassination attempts== ===Thomas DiMassimo=== At a campaign rally at [[Dayton, Ohio]], Thomas DiMassimo attempted to rush the stage on which Trump was speaking. DiMassimo, 22, who had said on social media that he wanted to become a "martyr", was subdued by members of the [[United States Secret Service|Secret Service]] and later charged with disorderly conduct.{{cite web|url=http://www.dangerandplay.com/2016/03/12/thomas-dimassimo-i-want-to-be-a-martyr-trumps-would-be-killer-confesses/|title=Thomas Dimassimo: “I want to be a Martyr,” Trump’s Would be Killer Confesses|first=Mike|last=Cernovich|date=March 13, 2016|work=Danger & Play|accessdate=August 10, 2016}} ===Michael Sandford=== Michael Sandford, a 20-year-old Briton from [[Dorking]] in [[Surrey]], was arrested on June 18, 2016, for attempting to kill Trump. He was a resident of [[Hoboken, New Jersey]], for eighteen months before outstaying his visa and relocating to [[San Bernardino, California]], to practice shooting. Sandford bought tickets to Trump rallies in [[Las Vegas]] and in [[Phoenix, Arizona]]; at the former, he was arrested for trying to steal a gun from a security guard. Sandford, who is autistic and had been treated for [[mental health]] issues in England, said that he had been preparing to kill Trump for over a year.{{cite news|last1=Payton|first1=Matt|title=Michael Sandford: Everything we know about the British man who 'tried to assassinate Donald Trump'|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/americas/donald-trump-assassination-shoot-attempt-michael-sandford-gun-what-happened-everything-you-need-to-a7093476.html|accessdate=June 27, 2016|work=The Independent|date=June 21, 2016}} His parents said they believed that their son had been manipulated into plotting an attack due to his low social intelligence.{{cite news|title=Trump suspect Michael Sandford was a 'very quiet lad'|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-surrey-36588461|accessdate=June 27, 2016|publisher=BBC News|date=June 21, 2016}} On June 29, Sandford was charged with disrupting an official function and two firearms counts. The charges could bring a sentence of up to 30 years in prison.{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2016/jun/30/briton-michael-sandford-accused-trying-kill-donald-trump-is-charged-us|title=Briton accused of trying to kill Donald Trump is charged in US|date=June 30, 2016|work=The Guardian|accessdate=July 23, 2016}} ==Assassination threats== ===Emadeldin Elsayed=== Emadeldin Elsayed, a 23-year-old Egyptian studying to become an airline pilot in California, was investigated by the [[United States Secret Service|U.S. Secret Service]] in February 2016. Elsayed had made a threatening post against Trump on [[Facebook]], after Trump had declared that he would temporarily ban foreign Muslims from entering the U.S. He was reported to the [[Federal Bureau of Investigation]] by the owner of his flight school, Alex Khatib, and taken into detention. After Khatib was advised to expel his student, Elsayed had his visa revoked and was given a voluntary departure, escorted by federal agents, instead of a formal deportation. Elsayed was not charged with a criminal offense, and his treatment was criticized by the local [[American Civil Liberties Union]] and the Egyptian public as not in line with protection of free speech.{{cite news|last1=Stack|first1=Liam|title=Egyptian Aviation Student Who Made Trump Threat Is Leaving U.S.|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2016/03/08/us/egyptian-aviation-student-who-made-trump-threat-is-forced-to-leave-us.html?_r=0|accessdate=June 27, 2016|work=The New York Times|date=March 7, 2016}} ===Glenn Beck=== Radio host [[Glenn Beck]] was subjected to a federal investigation after reportedly threatening to stab Donald Trump.{{cite web|url=http://dfw.cbslocal.com/2016/03/04/glenn-beck-under-investigation-for-alleged-threat-against-trump/|title=Glenn Beck Under Investigation For Alleged Threat Against Trump|date=March 4, 2016|accessdate=August 10, 2016|work=CBS Local}} During his [[Glenn Beck Radio Program|radio program]], Glenn Beck had said, "If I was close enough and I had a knife. Really. I mean the stabbing just wouldn't stop." The conversation was reported as a threat against Donald Trump. The program's producer, Stu Burguiere wrote a Facebook post stating that the threat in the conversation was actually targeted at him.{{cite web|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2016/mar/5/glenn-beck-under-investigation-for-alleged-threat-/|title=Glenn Beck under investigation for alleged threat against Donald Trump|work=The Washington Times|date=March 5, 2016|accessdate=August 10, 2016}} Beck was later suspended from [[Sirius XM Holdings|Sirius XM]] after a subsequent interview in which a guest made similar threats.{{cite web|url=http://money.cnn.com/2016/05/31/media/glenn-beck-suspension-siriusxm/index.html|title=Glenn Beck suspended by SiriusXM|first=Tom|last=Kludt|date=May 31, 2016|work=CNN|accessdate=August 10, 2016}} ===Two Georgia teens=== In late March 2016, Sheriff Chuck Smith of [[Meriwether County, Georgia]], said that a 15-year-old boy was arrested for making an alleged death threat against Trump on social media. That same week, a 16-year-old boy was also arrested for making similar threats. Because both boys were minors, neither their names nor the nature of their alleged threats were released.{{cite web|url=http://nypost.com/2016/03/26/two-teens-arrested-for-threatening-trump-on-social-media/|title=Two teens arrested for threatening Trump on social media|agency=Associated Press|date=March 26, 2016|publisher=The New York Post|accessdate=August 10, 2016}} ===Brad Thor=== While appearing on the ''[[Glenn Beck Radio Program]]'', and on his Twitter account, thriller novelist [[Brad Thor]] asked, hypothetically as he later claimed, "what patriot will step up and [remove Trump from office]" should a Trump presidency become tyrannical.
