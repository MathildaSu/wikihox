He was a surgeon at [[Concord Hospital (New Hampshire)|Concord Hospital]] for four years, Selectman for the town of Wellesley, MA for six years, and was Executive Vice President for Clinical Research Services at [[PAREXEL| PAREXEL International]]. He is currently seeking the Democratic nomination for the [[Massachusetts gubernatorial election, 2014|2014 Massachusetts gubernatorial election]]. After completing medical school at [[Harvard Medical School|Harvard]], Avellone joined the [[United States Navy Reserve]], earning the rank of Lieutenant Commander. He later served as Health Care Advisor to [[Paul Tsongas|Senator Paul Tsongas]] during the The Senator's [[Democratic Party presidential primaries, 1992|1992 Presidential Campaign]] and on the Finance Committee for then-[[john Kerry|Senator John Kerry's]] [[United States presidential election, 2004|2004 Presidential Campaign]]. Avellone has also worked as a health care executive since 1991, serving in senior roles in several corporations. He declared his candidacy for [[Governor of Massachusetts]] in January, 2013. ==Early life and education== Avellone was born in Fremont, Ohio to Joseph C. Avellone II, a surgeon at [[Cleveland Clinic|Lutheran Hospital]] in [[Cleveland]], and Patricia Fox Avellone. He has five siblings. Avellone moved to New Hampshire to pursue a Bachelor's Degree from [[Dartmouth College]], where he played rugby and was a member of [[Sigma Alpha Epsilon]] fraternity. After completing his undergraduate education in 1970, he attended Dartmouth Medical School for two years. He enrolled in Harvard Medical School for his final two years, and went on to earn a Masters Degree in Public Administration from the [[John F. Kennedy School of Government]] at [[Harvard]].{{cite web|title=About Joe Avellone|url=http://www.avelloneforgovernor.com/about|publisher=Avellone for Governor|accessdate=5 November 2013}} In 1979, he was named number 3 in [[Time (magazine)|Time Magazine's]] ''50 Faces for the Future'', along with [[Bill Clinton]], [[Paul Tsongas]], and [[Jesse Jackson]].{{cite news|last=Time Staff|title=50 Faces for America's Future|url=http://archive.org/stream/learymaterials350ppi/learymaterials350ppi_djvu.txt|accessdate=12 December 2013|newspaper=Time Magazine|date=6 August 1979}} ==Surgical career and military service== After graduation from Harvard Medical School, Avellone did his residency at [[Peter Bent Brigham Hospital]], now known as Brigham and Women's Hospital. He went on to work as a surgeon at Concord Hospital in [[New Hampshire]] for four years. In 1979, Avellone joined the Navy Reserve as a member of the [[Medical Corps (United States Navy)|Medical Corps]]. Avellone is [[board certification|board certified]] in general surgery and is a fellow of the [[American College of Surgeons]].{{cite web|last=Healthgreades|title=Dr. Joseph C. Avellone III, MD - My Practice Details|url=http://www.healthgrades.com/physician/dr-joseph-avellone-yq849|publisher=healthgrades|accessdate=5 November 2013}} ==Health industry career== Following his surgical career, Dr. Avellone has worked as a healthcare executive in the Boston Area. He served as [[Chief Operating Officer]] of [[Blue Cross Blue Shield of Massachusetts]] for seven years. While at Blue Cross, Avellone headed HMO Blue, a [[health maintenance organization]] focused on lowering healthcare costs by limiting fees and coordinating care.{{cite news|url=http://www.southcoasttoday.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/19960803/NEWS/308039973&cid=sitesearch}} He then worked as [[CEO]] of biomedical company Veritas Medicine. In 2007 he began working for PAREXEL International, becoming Senior Vice President for Clinical Research in 2010, holding that position until October 1, 2013.{{cite web|last=Martin|first=Diana|title=PAREXEL APPOINTS SENIOR HEALTHCARE EXECUTIVE JOE AVELLONE TO CORPORATE VICE PRESIDENT OF CLINICAL RESEARCH OPERATIONS|url=http://www.parexel.com/company/news-and-events/parexel-news/archives/2007/parexel-appoints-senior-healthcare-executive-joe-avellone-to-cor/|publisher=PAREXEL|accessdate=5 November 2013}} He now holds a small advisory position at PAREXEL.{{cite web|last=Businessweek|title=Joseph Avellone: Executive Profile & Biography|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=34265182&ticker=PRXL&previousCapId=32717&previousTitle=PAREXEL%20INTERNATIONAL%20CORP|publisher=Bloomberg Businessweek|accessdate=5 November 2013}} ==Political career== ===Early years=== In 1990, Avellone ran successfully for the Board of Selectman in the town of Wellesley, Massachusetts. He was sworn in the following year, and won reelection five times. During his third term, he served as Chairman of the Board of Selectmen. Two years after his election, Avellone joined [[Paul Tsongas|Senator Paul Tsongas']] [[Democratic Party presidential primaries, 1992|Presidential Campaign]] as a Healthcare Advisor.{{cite web|last=Tsongas|url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2014/feb/8/dem-gov-hopeful-avellone-cites-business-experience/}} He has worked on the Presidential Campaigns of [[John Kerry]], [[Gary Hart]], and [[Edward Kennedy]].{{cite web|url=http://www.boston.com/metrodesk/2013/01/08/donald-berwick-former-obama-health-care-official-mulling-run-for-governor/Hxg1loA4Vhjz81vp3t6NBO/story.html}} ===2014 Massachusetts gubernatorial campaign=== {{see also|Massachusetts gubernatorial election, 2014}} On January 8, 2013, Avellone announced that he will run for [[Governor of Massachusetts]] in 2014 when incumbent Governor [[Deval Patrick]] retires.{{cite web|last=Gaines|first=Lee|title=Former Wellesley selectmen announces run for governor|url=http://www.wickedlocal.com/wellesley/news/x1441685507/Former-Wellesley-selectmen-announces-run-for-governor|publisher=Wicked Local|accessdate=5 November 2013}} ==Personal life== Avellone married Sandy Nabhan in 1975. Joe and Sandy currently live in Wellesley, Massachusetts.
