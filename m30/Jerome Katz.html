Katz''' is an American professor, consultant and author who specializes in the process and discipline of [[entrepreneurship]]. He is currently the Coleman Professor of Entrepreneurship at [[Saint Louis University]] in [[St Louis]], [[Missouri]]. He is also founding director of the university’s Billiken Angels Network investment program. ==Education== Katz attended [[Rhodes College]] in [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]] from 1970 to 1973, earning a BA (Honors) in [[psychology]], [[political science]] and urban studies. He then received a Masters in psychology from the [[University of Memphis]] in 1976 before going to [[Harvard University|Harvard]] to study administration, planning and social policy where he received a Certificate of Advanced Study in 1977. His PhD in organizational psychology from the [[University of Michigan]] was awarded in 1981. ==Career== ===Academia=== From 1978 to 1980 Katz was a project director at the Survey Research Center, Institute for Social Research at the University of Michigan, developing program evaluation methods for the US Department of Agriculture’s [[4-H]] clubs. From 1980 to 1987 he was an assistant professor at the [[University of Pennsylvania]]’s Wharton School of Business. In 1987 Katz moved to Saint Louis University as assistant professor of management and later became an associate, then full, professor. He started this business while studying in Memphis, then sold it prior to moving to Michigan. He then set up J.A. Katz Associates, currently operating in St Louis, in 1976. The firm consults on entrepreneurship, entrepreneurial development and entrepreneurship education to NGOs, large and small businesses. ===Billiken Angels Network=== In 2008 Katz founded the Billiken Angels Network as part of Saint Louis University’s entrepreneurship program. The network is an [[angel investor]] process which aims to pair up potential investors with startup businesses, so they can provide them with capital and expertise. In exchange investors receive equity or dividends from the business. It also runs workshops for businesses seeking funding.[http://www.stltoday.com/suburban-journals/metro/news/billiken-angel-network-to-host-funding-workshop/article_3f9b1d71-5a49-5229-8eb4-00239d680065.html Billiken Angel Network to host funding workshop]. St Louis Post-Dispatch (retrieved Dec 8, 2014). Billiken Angels differs from most angel investors in two ways. First it offers small investments of as low as $5,000 to businesses started by SLU students. Second, the group uses an additional network of volunteers, called Billiken Angel Fellows, to perform screening and due diligence on behalf of the angels. The university itself is an active partner in the network and has allocated $1 million for investment in its projects.Billiken Angels Network, [http://www.billikenangels.com/ban-faqs.html BAN FAQs] (retrieved Dec 8, 2014). (Rerieved Dec 8, 2014). ==Publications== Katz, with Richard Green, is the author of ''Entrepreneurial Small Business'', a textbook for small business owners published in US, international, Chinese and Korean editions. He is a founding editor for the annual book series ''Advances in Entrepreneurship, Firm Emergence and Growth''. He is also the author of over 100 academic papers, book chapters and technical reports, including four papers included in compendia of notable or classic works in entrepreneurship.
