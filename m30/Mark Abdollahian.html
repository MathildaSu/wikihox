xml:space="preserve" bytes="10039"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Mark Abdollahian|timestamp=20161013214715|year=2016|month=October|day=13|substed=yes}} '''Mark Abdollahian''' is a Clinical Professor in the School of Social Science, Policy & Evaluation at [[Claremont Graduate University]]. He is also Chief Executive Officer of ACERTAS and cofounder of Sentia Group. == Education == After receiving a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science, History and French from [[Case Western University]], Abdollahian acquired a Master of Arts in Foreign and Defense Policy, and a PhD in [[Political Economy]] and [[Mathematical Modeling]] from [[Claremont Graduate University]] (1996).[http://www.cgu.edu/PDFFiles/SPE/SPE_CV/MAA%20CV%20Jul%2013.pdf Curriculum Vitae] == Career == Abdollahian's studies included [[game theory|strategic decision making]], [[data analytics]], [[predictive analytics]], [[international political economy]], [[sustainable development]], [[economics]], [[Economic growth|growth]], [[econometrics]], and [[computational modeling]]. He has lectured to audiences worldwide, and he served as a board member for several private and nonprofit enterprises. He has co-authored a book on [[power transition theory]] with [[A.F.K. Organski]] and [[Jacek Kugler]]. He has also written many articles on stability across business, politics, economics, and [[civil service reform in developing countries]].[http://www.cgu.edu/pages/5148.asp CGU faculty page] His works in predictive analytics have been employed by the US government, the [[World Bank]], the [[United Nations]], as well as private sector companies, such as [[Arthur Andersen]], [[Motorola]], [[Raytheon]], [[British Aerospace]], [[Chevron Corporation|Chevron]] and [[DeBeers]]. He received multiple grants from Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA). He began teaching at Claremont Graduate University in 2003. In 2005, he helped cofound the Trans Research Consortium, which aims at providing scientific indicators of government performance for all levels of decision makers. In 2011, he was selected CEO of Beijing Ilinoi Investment Company. == Media == As both private corporations and national governments are seeking more advanced scientific methods to understand and predict conflicts, Abdollahian's studies have appeared in the media, such as in ''[[CNN]]'' Tara Kangarlou, "Team of political scientists saw Egypt's turmoil coming," [http://www.cnn.com/2013/07/07/politics/egypt-predictive-analysis/ ''CNN'', July 7, 2013]Tara Kangarlou, "State Department hopes it can find peace among data," [http://www.cnn.com/2013/04/30/politics/state-data-analysis ''CNN'', May 1, 2013] and ''[[The New Yorker]]'' Wendell Steavenson, "Pessoptimism," [http://www.newyorker.com/talk/comment/2011/03/14/110314taco_talk_steavenson ''The New Yorker'', March 14, 2011] he discusses about the prospects of predictive analysis in international politics. ''[[BBC]]'' Sharon Weinberger, "Predicting the future: Forecasting the news of tomorrow," [http://www.bbc.com/future/story/20120206-forecasting-the-news-of-tomorrow/4 ''BBC'', February 22, 2012] has interviewed him on the privacy concerns of data analytics. In China, Joey Zhouzheng has interviewed him on business, Chinese-America relations, and technology.
