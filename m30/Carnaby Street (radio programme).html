Online | opentheme = Crunchy Granola Suite | endtheme = Crunchy Granola Suite | website = http://www.manxradio.com/on-air/carnaby-street/carnaby-street/ | podcast = }} '''''Carnaby Street''''', is a radio programme broadcast on [[Manx Radio]] on Saturday mornings and features music from the 1960s. http://www.manxradio.com/on-air/carnaby-street/carnaby-street/ It is presented by Chris Williams and features music and news from two chosen years. The programme is produced by Blackpool Bullet Productions for Chris Williams Entertainments and is sponsored by the [[Isle of Man Transport|Isle of Man Railways]]. The show is broadcast across the [[Isle of Man]], [[North West England]], [[North Wales]], Southern Scotland and northern parts of Ireland on [[FM broadcasting|FM]] and [[Medium wave|MW]] frequencies as well as world wide through the [[Manx Radio]] website. The title of the show is taken from [[Carnaby Street]], seen by many as the epi-centre of [[Swinging London]] in the 1960s. The show commences at the conclusion of the 08:30am news bulletin and begins with Williams playing the show's theme tune, ''Crunchy Granola Suite'' by [[Percy Faith|Percy Faith & His Orchestra.]] Williams then gives a brief outline of the chosen years of that week's show, together with the artists which will be featured, and concludes his introduction with the phrase; ''"Let's get on with it!"''http://www.manxradio.com/radioplayer/od/1393/ ==Show format== The format takes the form of a contemporary presentation in two chosen years. Williams, with the assistance of his ''"co-presenters" Doris the Tea Lady, Harry the Engine Driver and Roger the Fireman,'' take the listener onboard the [[Isle of Man Transport|Isle of Man Railways]] time train, whereupon they are transported back to the chosen year. Williams then presents music from the particular year as well as contemporary news items which are spoken about in the context of the [[Present tense|present tense]]. The show leans heavily on the style of presentation from [[Radio Caroline]], using radio jingles and competitions which would of been familiar to listeners of [[Radio Caroline]] in the 1960s. If the chosen year is pre-1964, jingles from [[Radio Luxembourg]] are used instead. Numerous competitions from the programme's sponsors are also featured with various prizes offered. The music is by various well-known 60s artists, and the playlist also includes three million selling records from both of the featured years. The style of presentation of the show is exceedingly upbeat and positive, being referred to by Williams as: ''"the best little 60's show on the radio."'' Often heard are quips by ''Doris'', which invariably take the form of [[Double entendre|''double entendre.'']] The show generally ends with Williams reviewing some aspects of the show whilst ''Crunchy Granola Suite'' plays in the background. The edition is then available on the [[Manx Radio|Manx Radio website]] to be listened to again for the following seven days. Whilst [[Manx Radio]] is a commercial radio station, advertising content on the show is for the show's sponsor only. However, contemporary radio adverts are also played in order to re-create the flavour of radio broadcasting in the 1960s. ==History== The programme can trace its lineage from previous [[Manx Radio]] programmes of a similar genre such as ''The Golden Oldies Show'' which was presented by Stu Lowe.http://www.manxradio.com/ Following Lowe's departure from [[Manx Radio]], Andy Wint took over the presentation and set about enhancing the presentation style and the show's format, providing a more professional tone to the production. This has been further enhanced by Williams resulting in the show's current format. ==Guest presenters== In May 2015 Chris Williams had to temporarily stand down from presenting ''Carnaby Street'' following a bout of illness, with Simon Clarke deputising.
