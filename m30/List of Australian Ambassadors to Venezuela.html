(resident in Brasilia) |website = }} The '''Australian Ambassador to Venezuela''' is the [[Government of Australia|Australian Government]]'s foremost diplomatic representative in [[Venezuela]]. The Australian Government established an Embassy in [[Caracas]] in 1979 and appointed its first resident Ambassador, A.D. Brown. Previously, responsibility for Australian diplomatic representation in Venezuela was held in Brazil (1973–1974) and then Peru (1974–1979).{{citation|title=CA 8010: Australian Embassy, Venezuela [Caracas]|url=http://naa12.naa.gov.au/scripts/SearchOld.asp?Number=CA+8010|publisher=National Archives of Australia|accessdate=23 January 2016}} The Australian Embassy in Caracas is now closed.{{citation|url=http://dfat.gov.au/geo/venezuela/pages/venezuela-country-brief.aspx|accessdate=23 January 2016|title=Venezuela country brief|author=Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade|publisher=Australian Government}} Currently, the Australian Ambassador to Venezuela is accredited from Santiago.
