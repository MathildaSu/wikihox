He is well known and respected from the community. His biggest cash win to date exceeds $701,757, and is coming from 2012 [[World Series of Poker]] ''No-Limit Hold'em/Six Handed'' tournament, when he finished second to [[Greg Merson]]. [http://www.pokernews.com/live-reporting/2012-world-series-of-poker/event-57-no-limit-hold-em-six-handed/ PokerNews WSOP 2012 report] As for June 2015 he has two [[WSOP]] bracelets. First one came in 2003 [[43th Annual World Series of Poker]], [[Pot Limit]] [[Hold'em]] tournament. In that event he defeated [[Chris "Jesus" Ferguson]] in second place. His second [[WSOP]] bracelet is from 2015 [[46th Annual World Series of Poker]], [[Heads-up]] [[No Limit]] event, he defeated 143 poker player field. On the final table he was [[Heads-up]] against [[Paul Volpe]], the game lasted 24 hands and finally Lehr won the first place with $334,430 in cash, which also made him a part of the [[World Series of Poker multiple bracelet winners]].[http://www.cardplayer.com/poker-news/18886-keith-lehr-wins-2015-wsop-10-000-heads-up-no-limit-hold-em-championship WSOP: Keith Lehr Wins 2015 Heads-up..] [http://www.wsop.com/tournaments/updates.asp?grid=1136&tid=14210 Keith Lehr wins second bracelet] Regarding to this game he said: ''“I’m not sure how many more of these I can win. They are getting tougher to get each time I come out here.”''[http://www.wsop.com/news/2015/Jun/6272/KEITH-LEHR-IS-HEADS-ABOVE-ALL-THE-REST.html WSOP: Keith Lehr Heads Above All The Rest] As for June 2015 his total earnings exceed more than $2,190,000.
