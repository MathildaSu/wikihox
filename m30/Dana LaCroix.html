xml:space="preserve" bytes="8896"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Dana LaCroix|timestamp=20161203213409|year=2016|month=December|day=3|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Infobox musical artist | name = Dana LaCroix | image = | caption = Dana LaCroix Performing Live, 2012 | image_size = | background = solo_singer | birth_date = {{Birth date and age|1966|04|01}} | origin = [[Toronto, Ontario]], United States | genre = [[Folk rock]], [[Roots rock]] | years_active = 1986–present | website = {{URL|danalacroix.com}} }} '''Dana LaCroix''' (born April 1, 1966) is a Canadian-American singer and songwriter. She has released five roots and [[blues]]-influenced studio albums that incorporate elements of [[Pop music|pop]], [[roots-rock]], [[country]] and [[Folk music|folk]] music. LaCroix was born in [[Toronto]], Canada, to Naomi LaCroix (née: Tanaka), a registered nurse and [[Pat LaCroix]], a Canadian musician and photographer who toured in the early sixties with [[The Halifax Three]], a group he founded with [[Denny Doherty]], later of [[The Mamas and The Papas]] She cites The Halifax Three as her earliest musical influence. She is the sister of famous Canadian actress, [[Lisa LaCroix]]. Touring since 1993, LaCroix has appeared in the United States, Canada, Norway and Denmark, at venues such as [[Midtfyns Festival]], [[The Opera House]], [[Regent Theater]], and on national media including CBC’s [[Morningside (radio program)|Morningside]], [[This Morning (radio program)|This Morning]] and [[Morning Edition]], and [[The Artie Lange Show]].{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fU-non7irVg|title=The Artie Lange Show - Dana LaCroix Performs "Cinderella's Sister"|first=|last=Artie Lange|date=18 November 2013|publisher=|accessdate=23 October 2016|via=YouTube}} LaCroix began her professional career in her teens as a featured performer at [[Expo 86|Expo ’86]]{{cite web|url=http://shop.vinylrecords.co.uk/humber-at-expo-86|title=Humber College Of Music Department - Humber At Expo 86|work=vinylrecords.co.uk|accessdate=23 October 2016}} in [[Vancouver, Canada]], with the [[Humber College]] Big Band and went on to work with [[Motown]] session guitarist, Leroy Emmanuel,{{cite web|url=http://www.rbmaradio.com/shows/leroy-emmanuel-fireside-chat|title=Fireside Chat Leroy Emmanuel|work=rbmaradio.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} and Norris Vines,{{cite web|url=http://www.insideottawavalley.com/news-story/4854978-one-time-member-of-the-platters-to-perform-at-gallipeau-centre-theatre-sept-20/|title=One time member of the Platters to perform at Gallipeau Centre Theatre Sept. 20|first=|last=InsideOttawaValley.com|work=insideottawavalley.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} formerly of [[The Platters]], as a back-up and featured singer in a Motown tribute group that played around Toronto in the late 1980s. She moved to New York City in 1989 here she worked with [[Moogy Klingman]], and a local 24-piece folk-rock band called The Poppies and others, before relocating [[Copenhagen]], Denmark in 1990. In Denmark she toured with Danish-American guitarist, singer and songwriter, [[Paul Banks]] {{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/paul-banks-mn0002149394|title=Paul Banks - Biography & History - AllMusic|work=allmusic.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} and took over for [[Champion Jack Dupree]] as the musical partner of Danish bluesman, Kenn Lending.{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/1906099-Kenn-Lending-Blues-Band|title=Kenn Lending Blues Band|work=discogs.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} She worked with Lending under the moniker Lending-LaCroix for two years, releasing a studio recording entitled "Down Home Blues" {{cite web|url=http://www.lending.dk/recordings/|title=Recordings|work=lending.dk|accessdate=23 October 2016}} for Olufsen Records.{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/label/34662-Olufsen-Records|title=Olufsen Records|work=discogs.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} A noted songwriter, LaCroix was approached by Danish director, [[Nicolas Winding Refn]] in 2002 to deliver music to his mystery thriller film, [[Fear X]], starring [[John Turturro]]. Two of LaCroix’s self-penned numbers, Lonely Rooms and Pride, from her independent debut release, "Pride", were included in the film. The tracks were produced by Canadian producer, Rick Whitelaw{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/rick-whitelaw-mn0000935792/credits|title=Rick Whitelaw - Credits - AllMusic|work=allmusic.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} LaCroix began working with Spanish bassist, songwriter and producer, Yadam Gonzales,{{cite web|url=http://www.musicvf.com/songs.php?page=artist&artist=Yadam%20Gonzales&tab=songaswriterchartstab|title=Yadam Gonzales ••• Top Songs as Writer ••• Music VF, US & UK hits charts|work=musicvf.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} soon thereafter, co-writing music for Dutch pop star, [[Hind Laroussi|“Hind’s”]] 2005 release, "Halfway Home".{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Hind-Halfway-Home/release/3476923|title=Hind - Halfway Home|work=discogs.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} An acclaimed workshop leader, LaCroix has presented at [[Academy of Music and Dramatic Arts]] in [[Odense]], Denmark, The Danish Musical Academy{{cite web|url=http://www.musicalakademiet.dk/en/|title=Det Danske Musicalakademi - en|work=musicalakademiet.dk|accessdate=23 October 2016}} in [[Fredericia]], Denmark, and The Psychotherapy Networker Symposium{{cite web|url=https://www2.psychotherapynetworker.org/symposium/2016/113-the-performer-in-you/|title=113 – The Performer in You|date=25 November 2015|work=psychotherapynetworker.org|accessdate=23 October 2016}} in [[Washington, DC]], the largest conference of its kind in the US. Today LaCroix performs with her band in Canada,{{cite web|url=http://www.oldmilltoronto.com/gp_entertainment/dana-lacroix-trio/|title=Dana LaCroix Trio - Old Mill - Toronto Hotel|work=oldmilltoronto.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} the US {{cite web|url=http://www.pressconnects.com/story/entertainment/2016/03/08/songwriter-celebrates-new-roots-binghamton/81480780/|title=Songwriter celebrates new roots in Binghamton|work=pressconnects.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} and Denmark,{{cite web|url=http://www.spillestedetblokhus.dk/arrangementer/dana-lacroix/|title=Dana LaCroix – Spillestedet Blokhus|date=9 August 2016|work=spillestedetblokhus.dk|accessdate=23 October 2016}} continues to release cds consisting of mostly original music,{{cite web|url=http://www.dailyfreeman.com/article/DF/20110916/NEWS/309169978|title=CD REVIEW: Dana LaCroix's 'Jump In' at the highest level|work=dailyfreeman.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} and has begun a duo project{{cite web|url=http://www.thisiscooperstown.com/events/dana-lacroix-murali-coryell-and-band-oneonta-theatre|title=Dana Lacroix & Murali Coryell and Band at the Oneonta Theatre|work=thisiscooperstown.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}}{{cite web|url=http://www.thedailystar.com/news/entertainment_news/pair-of-shows-set-at-b-side-ballroom/article_74b42876-c3cd-5404-9859-27c99f2d633c.html|title=Pair of shows set at B Side Ballroom|work=thedailystar.com|accessdate=23 October 2016}} with [[Murali Coryell]], son of jazz legend, [[Larry Coryell]].
