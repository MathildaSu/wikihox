xml:space="preserve" bytes="7191"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Georgia M. Green|timestamp=20150713015303|year=2015|month=July|day=13|substed=yes|help=off}} '''Georgia M. Green''' is an American linguist. She earned her PhD in Linguistics from the University of Chicago in 1971. She was a fellow at the Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences at Stanford University in 1978-79. She is currently a Professor Emeritus of Linguistics at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign where she was also a member of the Beckman Institute Cognitive Science Group.{{Cite web|url = http://semanticsarchive.net/Archive/zYxYjBiZ/green.weblink.html|title = Semantics Archive|date = |accessdate = July 12, 2015|website = |publisher = |last = |first = }}{{Cite journal|url = http://openscholarship.wustl.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1658&context=law_lawreview|title = Conference Participants, Annotated Table of Contents, Problems Considered|last = |first = |date = January 1995|journal = Washington University Law Review|doi = |pmid = |access-date = July 12, 2015}} Green’s research explores areas of pragmatics that show the effect of speaker intention on language interpretation, including word order effects of information structure{{Cite news|url = http://niemanreports.org/articles/just-write-what-happened/|title = Just Write What Happened|last = Woo|first = William F.|date = |work = Nieman Reports|access-date = July 12, 2015|via = }}, discourse particles, and language applications to judicial contexts{{Cite book|title = Language in the Judicial Process|last = Levi|first = Judith N. and Anne Graffam Walker|publisher = Plenum|year = 1990|isbn = |location = |pages = xii}}. Her first book on meaning in language were respected for daring to use example sentences with political topics{{Cite web|url = http://languagelog.ldc.upenn.edu/nll/?p=2567|title = Making Linguistics Relevant|date = August 21, 2010|accessdate = July 12, 2015|website = The Language Log|publisher = |last = |first = }}. She has served as a peer reviewer or advisory editor for journals including ''Language, Linguistics and Philosophy, Natural Language and Linguistic Theory, Law and Social Inquiry,'' and ''Discourse Processes'' and publishers including Academic Press.{{Cite book|title = Syntax and Semantics vol. 11: Presupposition|last = Oh|first = Choon-Kyu and David A Dinneen;|publisher = |year = 1979|isbn = |location = |pages = }} Since 1997 she has served on the usage panel for the ''American Heritage Dictionary''.{{Cite web|url = https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/usagepanel.html|title = Usage Panel|date = |accessdate = July 12, 2015|website = The American Heritage Dictionary|publisher = |last = |first = }} ==Publications== ===Books=== *Green, Georgia M. and Jerry L.
