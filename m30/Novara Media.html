xml:space="preserve" bytes="9569"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Novara Media|timestamp=20151003141241|year=2015|month=October|day=3|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Infobox website | name = Novara Media | logo = | logocaption = | screenshot = | collapsible = | collapsetext = | caption = | url = {{URL|http://novaramedia.com}} | slogan = Media for a Different Politics | commercial = Nonprofit | type = | registration = | language = English | num_users = | content_license = | content_licence = | programming language = | owner = | author = [[Aaron Bastani]], James Butler | editor = Craig McVegas | launch_date = {{Start date and age|2011|07|df=no}} | alexa = | revenue = | current_status = Active | footnotes = }} '''Novara Media''' is a London-based new media organisation with a specific focus on politics and current affairs. Its mission statement is to create a "new media for a different politics". It was started in June 2011 by Aaron Bastani, initially as an hour-long radio show broadcast weekly on London's [[ResonanceFM]].{{Cite web|title = New weekly Resonance FM show will discuss politics, activism and social change|url = https://www.opendemocracy.net/ourkingdom/aaron-peters/new-weekly-resonance-fm-show-will-discuss-politics-activism-and-social-chang|website = openDemocracy|accessdate = 2015-10-03}} The weekly show remains central in Novara's output and is now known as NovaraFM with Bastani joined by co-hosts James Butler, co-founder of Novara, and Ash Sarkar.{{Cite web|title = Support Novara Media|url = http://support.novaramedia.com/|website = Novara Media|accessdate = 2015-10-04}} In 2013 Novara Media expanded into video with the launch of NovaraTV. Early the following year Novara launched it popular blogging site Novara Wire.{{Cite web|title = Novara Wire|url = http://wire.novaramedia.com/|website = Novara Wire|accessdate = 2015-10-04}} One of the major ideas that Novara has popularised is "Fully Automated Luxury Communism",{{Cite web|title = Fully automated luxury communism|url = http://www.theguardian.com/sustainable-business/2015/mar/18/fully-automated-luxury-communism-robots-employment|website = the Guardian|accessdate = 2015-10-03|first = Brian|last = Merchant}}{{Cite web|title = We Don't Need More Austerity: We Need Luxury Communism {{!}} VICE {{!}} United Kingdom|url = http://www.vice.com/en_uk/read/luxury-communism-933|accessdate = 2015-10-04}} a set of propositions which aim at the consistent reduction of working hours, while increasing leisure time, through the increased application of [[automation]] within a [[post-capitalist]] set of values. In 2015 the [[Institute for Public Policy Research]] referred to Novara Media as a key example of an innovative website providing alternative commentary on British politics.{{cite web|title=IPPR Report: "The democracy commission: Reforming democracy to combat political inequality" |url= http://www.ippr.org/read/the-democracy-commission-reforming-democracy-to-combat-political-inequality#reviving-our-democracy-addressing-political-inequality | accessdate = August 26, 2015 }} At present
