She is the founder and Editor of CLiberiaClearly, an entertainment blog and platform which publish news about Liberian entertainers or celebrities globally.{{cite news|url=http://gossipliberia.com/entertainment/controversial-blogger-berenice-mulubah-interview-on-hott-fm-107-9/|work=Gossipliberia|title=controversial blogger berenice mulubah interview on hott fm 107.9|author= G's Lib|accessdate=22 December 2016}}{{cite news|url=http://whisperingstories.com/bloggers-bench-berenice-mulubah/|work=whisperingstories|title=Bloggersâ€™ Bench with Berenice Mulubah|author=whisperingstories|accessdate=22 December 2016}} ==Education and Early Life== Berenice was born in Harper City,Maryland County of Liberia. She relocated to the United States of America in August 1997, because of the civil war in Liberia. She continued her High School Education at Simon Gratz High School n Philadelphia, where she won a City Wide Black History Month Essay Competition. She graduated from the school in 1998 and enrolled into the Forest Park Community College, St. Louis, Mo in 2001 where she studied Music, she later dropped and had the opportunity to join the United States Marine Corps in 2002. She continued her Education in the Military and earned a certificate in Mass Communication at the Full Sail University. ==Career== While in United States, Berenice began an active blogger in 2013. She began by starting a website which is consider as the most controversial blog in the Liberian Entertainment industry.(CLiberiaClearly) which feature Liberian entertainers globally.{{cite news|url=http://www.talkzone.com/hosts/156/internationalview/1436|work=talkzone|title=Berenice Mulubah|author=talkzone |accessdate=22 December 2016}} She also launched a campaign on Rape Prevention in 2015, educating people about rape and prevention. ==Controversial Posts== Berenice has done controversial reports of senator and former Liberian footballer, George Weah, DenG, Chyper the King, Frank Artus, F dot A and Flex.{{cite news|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/201602091849.html|work=allafrica|title=Liberia: What's Up With Jon Bricks and Berenice Mulubah?|author= Robin Dopoe Jr.|accessdate=22 December 2016}} ==Awards/Achievements== {| class="wikitable" |- !
