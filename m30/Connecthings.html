xml:space="preserve" bytes="23575"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Connecthings|timestamp=20170109133031|year=2017|month=January|day=9|substed=yes|help=off}} {{Orphan|date=January 2017}} '''Connecthings''' is a French [[Multinational corporation|multinational]] software company with dual headquarters in [[Paris, France]] and [[New York City|New York City, New York]],{{cite web|url=http://www.connecthings.com/about/#contact-us|title=About Us|website=Connecthings}} that operates contactless [[smart city]] technology, including [[Bluetooth low energy beacon|Bluetooth]] and [[Near field communication|NFC]] beacons, as well as [[SaaS]] management platforms. The company specializes in the [[Internet of Things (IoT)]] technology market, and partners with [[Municipality|municipalities]], transit authorities, [[international airport]]s, stadiums and commerce centers to connect passive, public objects with mobile users in bringing contextualized, personalized information. Connecthings was founded in July 2007 by Laetitia Gazel Anthoine and currently operates in six countries which include France, Spain, Italy, Germany, Brazil and The United States. ==Founder and CEO == Laetitia Gazel Anthoine founded Connecthings in 2007 with the idea that [[smartphone]] technology would ultimately connect the physical and digital world.{{cite web|url=http://www.connecthings.com/about/#team|title=Team|website=Connecthings}} Prior to founding the company, Laetitia worked as a senior consultant for [[Orange S.A.|Orange]], the French multinational [[telecommunication]]s corporation, as well as [[Nortel]], the Canadian telecommunications corporation, where she helped implement [[wireless security]] networks for the [[Singapore]] Coast Guard.{{cite web|url=https://www.linkedin.com/in/gazelanthoine|title=Laetitia Gazel Anthoine|website=LinkedIn}} Aside from serving as the CEO of the company, Laetitia serves as a board member for the [[French Institute for Research in Computer Science and Automation|French Institute for Research in Computer Science and Automation (INRIA)]],{{cite web|url=https://www.inria.fr/en/institute/organisation/committees/board-of-directors|title=Board of Directors|website=Inria}} as well as serving as a mentor for the Women’s Business Mentoring Initiative (WBMI) program.{{cite web|url=http://women-business-mentoring-initiative.com/fr/mentors/membres/laetitia-gazel-anthoine|title=MENTORS - Laetitia Gazel Anthoine|website=WBMI - Women Business Mentoring|accessdate=16 December 2016}} Laetitia is a graduate of the École Supérieure d'Électricité, known as [[Supélec|Supelec]], with degrees in engineering, telecommunications, and project management. ==Technology== ===Beacons === Beacons are one of the more popular tools used when creating a smart and connected system.{{cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/beacons-and-ibeacons-create-a-new-market-2013-12|title=BEACONS: What They Are, How They Work, Apple's iBeacon Technology Is Ahead Of The Pack|website=Business Insider|last1=Danova|first1=Tony|accessdate=16 December 2016}} Connecthings utilizes three types of beacon technology: * [[Bluetooth low energy beacon|Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) beacons]]: Beacon technology that utilizes a low energy standard communication protocol called Bluetooth 4.0.{{cite web|url=http://www.connecthings.com/platform/#nfc|title=NFC|website=Connecthings}} It allows compatible smartphones, with Connecthings [[Mobiles for development|Mobile SDK]] applications, to detect the signal of nearby Bluetooth beacons - One|website=Search Engine Land|last1=Lyne|first1=Marc|accessdate=16 December 2016}} ===ADTAG=== ADTAG is the Connecthings SaaS platform used by cities and organizations to manage and contextualize in real-time the beacon network deployed in urban spaces. The platform allows users to program and define the mobile experience at a specific point and time, as well as provide analytics and customized metrics.{{cite web|title=Our Platform|url=http://www.connecthings.com/platform/#nfc|website=Connecthings}} Unique to the ADTAG platform is the ability to personalize the mobile experience at each connected point. To do so, ADTAG integrates various real-time data flows to contextualize notification content sent to mobile users while on the move in the city. ADTAG infrastructure allows for integration and management from the same interface of any type of Bluetooth beacons, from different manufacturers and customers.{{cite web|url=http://industrialiot5g.com/20160616/channels/news/exploring-role-contactless-beacons-smart-city-tag17|title=Exploring The Role Of Contactless Beacons In A Smart City|website=Industrial IoT 5G|last1=Loda|first1=Kyle|accessdate=16 December 2016}} Additionally, the ADTAG platform enables multiple organizations and businesses to access the same beacon network within a city. This consolidation allows for organizations and brands to create [[Public-Private Partnerships|public-private partnerships (P3)]] with cities to solve complex problems such as promote safety and
