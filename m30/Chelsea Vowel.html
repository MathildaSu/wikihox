Anne]], [[Alberta]], [[Canada]] | website = {{URL|http://apihtawikosisan.com/}} }} '''Chelsea Vowel''' (born 1978) is a [[Canada|Canadian]] writer and social [[activist]], best known as the creator of the media outlet âpihtawikosisân.{{cite web|website=http://apihtawikosisan.com/|publisher=Apihtawikosisan|accessdate=December 2014}} Her work has been published in popular newspapers such as the [[National Post]], [[The Globe and Mail]], [[The Huffington Post]],{{cite web|last1=Vowel|first1=Chelsea|website=http://www.huffingtonpost.ca/chelsea-vowel/|publisher=Huffington Post|accessdate=December 2014}} the [[Toronto Star]] and [[Indian Country Today Media Network]]. ==Personal life== Vowel is a [[Metis people (Canada)|Metis]] woman, born in the [[Plains Cree]] community of [[Lac Ste. Anne, Alberta|Lac Ste. Anne]], [[Alberta]], Canada in 1978. Her hobbies include writing and songwriting in the Plains [[Cree Language]], and [[Roller derby]]. She has two daughters and two step daughters. ==Career== Vowel holds a [[Bachelor of Education]] and a [[Bachelor of Laws]] from the [[University of Alberta]]. She currently lives in [[Montreal]], [[Quebec]] teaching [[Inuit]] youth under protection. Vowel uses the name âpihtawikosisân for her website,{{cite web|last1=Vowel|first1=Chelsea|title=âpihtawikosisân|website=http://apihtawikosisan.com/|accessdate=December 2014}} Twitter page{{cite web|website=https://twitter.com/apihtawikosisan}} and online persona. This alias is a [[Plains Cree]] word meaning half-son, which is used to refer to the Metis who are of mixed [[European]] and [[First Nations]] descent. She identifies herself as writing from a Metis perspective with the goal of accurately representing her people. ==Politics== Vowel's activism is primarily for [[Indigenous rights]], including the [[Idle No More]] movement.{{cite web|last1=Vowel|first1=Chelsea|title=Idle no more sounds vague lets talk specifics|website=http://www.theglobeandmail.com/globe-debate/idle-no-more-only-sounds-vague-lets-talk-specifics/article7098267/|publisher=The Globe and Mail|accessdate=December 2014}} She is known for her opposition toward the use of Indian headdresses by non-indigenous people,{{cite web|title=An open letter to non-natives in headdresses|website=http://apihtawikosisan.com/hall-of-shame/an-open-letter-to-non-natives-in-headdresses/|accessdate=December 2014}} and her support of a national inquiry into the cases of missing and murdered Indigenous women in Canada, including those occurring on the [[Highway of Tears]]. ==References== {{Persondata | NAME = Vowel, Chelsea | ALTERNATIVE NAMES = | SHORT DESCRIPTION = Canadian activist | DATE OF BIRTH = 1978 | PLACE OF BIRTH = [[Lac Ste.
