xml:space="preserve" bytes="9479"> {{Article for deletion/dated|page=Conphidance|timestamp=20170603130610|year=2017|month=June|day=3|substed=yes}} {{notability|1=Biographies|date=June 2017}} {{COI|date=May 2017}} {{infobox person | name=Conphidance | birth_name=Uchenna Echeazu | occupation=Actor, Musician, Writer, Comedian, Producer, Dance Choreographer | known_for= ''[[American Gods (TV series)|American Gods]]'', ''[[Complications (TV series)|Complications]]'', ''[[The Sacrament (2013 film)|The Sacrament]]'' | birth_place=[[Nigeria]] }} '''Uchenna Echeazu''', better known by his stage name '''Conphidance''', is an actor, writer, musician, comedian and producer. He began his entertainment career as a drummer and dancer. {{cite web|last1=Nevins|first1=Julia|title=UF alumnus lands television role|url=http://www.alligator.org/the_avenue/movies_and_tv/article_5ef0f640-0a74-11e5-9589-abd77ac14740.html|website=The Alligator|accessdate=June 4, 2015}} He is mostly known for his role as Okoye in the television series ''[[American Gods (TV series)|American Gods]]'' and recurring role as CJ in ''[[Complications (TV series)|Complications]]'', as well as a supporting role in the 2013 film ''[[The Sacrament (2013 film)|The Sacrament]]''. == Early life == Conphidance was born and raised in [[Nigeria]]. His family later moved to the [[United States]]. He is the younger brother to musician Olisaemeka "slashWisDom" Echeazu.{{cite web|last1=Nevins|first1=Julia|title=UF alumnus lands television role|url=http://www.alligator.org/the_avenue/movies_and_tv/article_5ef0f640-0a74-11e5-9589-abd77ac14740.html|website=The Alligator|accessdate=June 4, 2015}} Conphidance attended the University of Florida, where he majored in biological engineering and minored in biomechanics and African studies, with a specialization in visual and performing arts. While there, he also served the president of the African Student Union for two years and was its dance choreographer for five years, and also wrote, directed, and acted in several plays.{{Cite web |url=http://rollingout.com/2015/06/10/atlanta-math-teacher-conphidance-leaves-classroom-pursue-passion-acting/ |title=Atlanta math teacher, Conphidance, leaves the classroom to pursue his passion for acting |last=Caslin |first=Yvette |website=Rolling Out |publication-date=June 10, 2015 |access-date=May 15, 2017}} Before becoming a full-time entertainer, Conphidance worked as a math teacher at [[Westlake High School (Georgia)|Westlake High School]] in [[Atlanta]], and also served as the assistant coach of the varsity boys' soccer team. He left teaching in the summer of 2010 to pursue acting full-time. == Acting career == Conphidance played a supporting role in ''[[The Sacrament (2013 film)|The Sacrament]]'' (2013), with [[Amy Seimetz]], [[Kate Lyn Sheil]], and [[Joe Swanberg]], and ''[[Fist Fight]]'' (2017), opposite [[Ice Cube]].{{cite web|last1=Entertainment|first1=Olamild|title=Nigerian Actor, Conphidance, Lands Recurring Role On Complications, a USA Network Series|url=http://www.olamildent.com/2015/06/nigerian-actor-conphidance-lands.html|website=Olamild Entertainment|accessdate=June 8, 2015}}{{cite web|last1=Caslin|first1=Yvette|title=Atlanta math teacher, Conphidance, leaves the classroom to pursue his passion for acting|url=http://rollingout.com/2015/06/10/atlanta-math-teacher-conphidance-leaves-classroom-pursue-passion-acting/|website=Rolling Out|accessdate=10 June 2015}}{{cite web|last1=Caslin|first1=Yvette|title=Atlanta math teacher, Conphidance, leaves the classroom to pursue his passion for acting|url=http://rollingout.com/2015/06/10/atlanta-math-teacher-conphidance-leaves-classroom-pursue-passion-acting/|website=Rolling Out|accessdate=10 June 2015}} He later appeared in an episode of the [[USA Network]] series ''[[Satisfaction (2014 TV series)|Satisfaction]]''. Conphidance also played the recurring role of Curtis "CJ" James Thompson in ''[[Complications (TV series)|Complications]]'', another USA Network series, opposite [[Jason O'Mara]] and [[Chris Chalk]]. Later that year, he was cast in ''[[Survivor's Remorse]]'' opposite [[Tichina Arnold]], [[Teyonah Parris]], and [[Erica Ash]]. The following year, he guest-starred in ''[[Hidden America with Jonah Ray]]'' and played supporting roles in ''[[The Inspectors (TV series)|The Inspectors]]'' on [[CBS]] and ''[[Good Behavior]]'' on [[TNT (TV channel)|TNT]]. He appears as Okoye opposite [[Orlando Jones]] in the 2017 [[Starz]] fantasy series ''[[American Gods (TV series)|American Gods]]'', based on the novel by [[Neil Gaiman|Neil Gailman]]. He also stars in the [[United States Navy SEALs|Navy SEAL]] drama ''[[Six (TV series)|Six]]'' on the [[History (U.S. TV channel)|History Channel]] . {{cite web|last1=Bibbiani|first1=William|title=SXSW 2017 Interview American Gods Producers Bryan Fuller and Michael Green|url=http://www.craveonline.com/entertainment/1229049-sxsw-2017-interview-american-gods-producers-bryan-fuller-michael-green#/slide/1|website=Crave Online|accessdate=March 12, 2017}} {{cite web|last1=Snetiker|first1=Marc|title=American Gods bosses answer burning questions from episode 2|url=http://ew.com/tv/2017/05/07/american-gods-episode-2-bryan-fuller-michael-green/|website=Entertainment Weekly|accessdate=7 May 2017}} Conphidance also produced and stars in the 2017 film ''Klippers'', written and directed by [[Ofu Obekpa]].
