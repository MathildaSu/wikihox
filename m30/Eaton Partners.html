The firm is also one of the oldest placement agents in the industry.[http://dealbook.nytimes.com/2013/11/11/private-equitys-online-courtship Private Equity's Online Courtship. New York Times, Nov. 11, 2013]Energy Trust Partners Seeks $600M for Its Fourth Fund. Dow Jones LBO Wire, April 11, 2013 Eaton Partners raises capital primarily from [[institutional investors]] including private and public [[pension funds]], [[endowment fund]]s, [[family offices]], [[fund of funds]], foundations and other large pools of money that invest in alternative asset classes. In 1989, the firm helped raise capital for a fund managed by [[Tiger Management Corp.]], one of the earliest hedge funds, which was founded by [[Julian Robertson]].[http://www8.gsb.columbia.edu/articles/node/1587#.U4oVwJSqJ69 A Pioneer. Columbia Business, October 9, 2013] As of April 2014, Eaton Partners acquired a secondary advisory group that assists clients in the purchase and sale of limited partnership interests in various types of private funds including private equity, real asset, real estate, venture capital and illiquid hedge funds.[http://www.finalternatives.com/node/26740 Eaton Partners Forms Secondary Advisory Group]. FINalternatives, April 11, 2014 The firm was founded in 1983 by Charles Eaton and has over 55 employees and six offices; three in the US, two in Asia and one in Europe. The firm's headquarters are in [[Rowayton]], [[Connecticut]].[http://www.eatonpartnersllc.com Company Website] In 2010, the firm was the first placement agent in the U.S. to launch a fund raising platform for private equity firms in China.[http://blogs.wsj.com/deals/2010/08/30/the-go-go-world-of-private-equity-in-china/ Private Equity Slowdown? Not for Yuan Funds. The Wall Street Journal, August 30, 2010.] In 2013, [[Private Equity International]] named Eaton Partners the "Placement Agent of the Year in North America" ahead of [[Park Hill Group]] and [[Credit Suisse]].[http://media.visioncapital.com/lib/docs/121133-pei113awardssec.pdf Private Equity International Awards 2012. Private Equity International, Annual Review 2012] Eaton Partners is a Registered Broker-Dealer and a member of [[FINRA]]. The firm is also registered as an Introducing Broker with the [[Commodity Futures Trading Commission]] and member of the [[National Futures Association]] and [[SIPC]]. The firm's U.K. operating subsidiary is authorized and regulated by the [[Financial Conduct Authority]] and their Hong Kong based arm is approved as a Type 1 License company under the [[Securities and Futures Commission]] in Hong Kong.[http://www.eatonpartnersllc.com Company Website] == Business Lines == '''Fund raising-''' Eaton Partners has relationships with over 4,000 active institutional investors in the US, Europe, Asia and the Middle East. Since its founding, the firm has raised more than $57 billion globally across 80 different private fund vehicles.[http://www.avcj.com/avcj/news/2334164/eaton-partners-hires-jackson-chan-for-asia-distribution Eaton Partners hires Jackson Chan for Asia distribution]. Asian Venture Capital Journal, May 14, 2014 '''Secondary advisory-''' Eaton Partner’s secondary advisory team assists the general partners of private funds in managing the sale of limited partnership interests within their existing funds and also raising new capital through stapled transactions, fund recapitalizations and fund restructurings. The team also advises limited partners on the purchase or sale of fund interests in the secondary markets. == Competitors == Eaton Partners competes primarily with large investment banks (most notably [[Credit Suisse]] and [[UBS AG|UBS]] as well as a range of smaller firms including [[Triago]], [[Atlantic-Pacific Capital]], [[Campbell Lutyens]], [[Probitas Partners]] and [[Park Hill Group]], an advisory business owned by [[The Blackstone Group]].
